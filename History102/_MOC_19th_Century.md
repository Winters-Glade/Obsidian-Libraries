# Map of Content — 19th Century & Industrial Age

This MOC organizes the 11 videos from **History102** (Rudyard Lynch and Austin Padgett) covering the 19th century and the Industrial Age. The collection explores the economic, political, social, and cultural transformations that defined this pivotal century — from the steam engine to the fall of empires, from the American frontier to the Mediterranean's decline.

---

## Core Economic/Industrial Transformation

| Note | Description |
|------|-------------|
| [[Industrial Revolution]] | The foundational transformation of the era — how Britain became the world's first industrial power, the technologies that drove change (steam, railways, steel), and the social upheaval that created modern capitalism and the working class. The Industrial Revolution is the economic engine behind nearly every other 19th-century development. |
| [[Capitalism's Rise+Fall+Rise]] | A sweeping history of capitalism as a system — its emergence alongside the Industrial Revolution, its 20th-century crises (Great Depression, world wars), and its neoliberal resurgence. Connects directly to the economic dimensions of all other 19th-century topics. |
| [[Rise+Fall of Modern Britain]] | Britain's trajectory from industrial pioneer to global hegemon to relative decline. A case study in the life cycle of great powers, intimately tied to the Industrial Revolution and the spread of capitalism. |

## The British World and Victorian Society

| Note | Description |
|------|-------------|
| [[Victorian Britain]] | Britain at its zenith — the society, politics, culture, and empire of the Victorian era (1837-1901). Explores how industrial capitalism shaped class structure, gender roles, morality, and global power. |
| [[Sex and Power in History]] | Examines the Victorian sexual system, the rise of feminism, the medicalization of sexuality, and the politics of gender — all deeply connected to the social transformations of the industrial 19th century. |

## The American Experience

| Note | Description |
|------|-------------|
| [[American Civil War]] | The defining crisis of 19th-century America — the clash between industrializing North and slaveholding agrarian South over slavery, federal power, and the nation's future. A war that determined whether the United States would remain unified and free. |
| [[Frontier Era America]] | The story of westward expansion (1803-1890) — Manifest Destiny, the displacement of Native peoples, the gold rushes, the cattle kingdom, homesteading, and the closing of the frontier that shaped American identity. |
| [[Native American History]] | The 19th-century experience of Native peoples — forced removal (Trail of Tears), the Indian Wars, the reservation system, assimilation policies (boarding schools, Dawes Act), and cultural survival against overwhelming odds. |
| [[Latino History]] | The history of Latino peoples in the Americas — Spanish colonial legacy, wars of independence, the Mexican-American War and territorial loss, migration patterns, and the emergence of Latino identity in the U.S. |

## Continental and Global Shifts

| Note | Description |
|------|-------------|
| [[East Europe's Age of Empires]] | The multi-ethnic empires of Eastern Europe (Austrian, Russian, Ottoman) struggling with nationalism, industrialization, and decline. The Balkan powder keg that would trigger World War I. |
| [[Mediterranean's Decline]] | The long eclipse of the Mediterranean world as the Atlantic economy rose — Ottoman decay, Italian and Spanish weakness, lost empires, and the region's transition from center to periphery of global affairs. |

## Study Notes

The 19th century can be understood through four interlocking transformations:

1. **Industrialization**: the shift from agrarian to industrial economies ([[Industrial Revolution]], [[Capitalism's Rise+Fall+Rise]])
2. **Nation-building**: the formation of modern nation-states through war, unification, and empire ([[American Civil War]], [[Frontier Era America]], [[East Europe's Age of Empires]])
3. **Imperial expansion and contraction**: the peak of European global dominance and early signs of decline ([[Victorian Britain]], [[Mediterranean's Decline]], [[Rise+Fall of Modern Britain]])
4. **Social reordering**: the creation of new classes, gender roles, and racial hierarchies ([[Sex and Power in History]], [[Native American History]], [[Latino History]])
