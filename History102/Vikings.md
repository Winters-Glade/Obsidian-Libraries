# Vikings

## Overview
The Viking Age (c. 800–1060 AD) was a 250-year period during the Dark Ages defined by Scandinavian expansion, raiding, trade, and settlement across Europe. Rudyard Lynch characterizes it as the "apex of the YOLO" — a time when people undertook extreme risks driven by cultural and economic forces.

## Factors Behind the Viking Age
1. **Overpopulation and primogeniture** — younger sons with no inheritance sought fortune abroad
2. **Technological innovation** — the longship enabled deep riverine penetration and coastal raids
3. **Political fragmentation in Europe** — weak post-Roman states were vulnerable targets
4. **Pagan culture** — valorization of martial glory and the promise of Valhalla

## Key Events & Figures
- Lindisfarne raid (793) — traditional start of the Viking Age
- Danelaw in England — Viking settlement and legal influence
- Norman conquest of England (1066) — descendants of Vikings conquered England
- Exploration: Greenland, Vinland (North America), Kiev Rus, Constantinople

## Legacy
- Transformed European trade routes
- Founded the first Russian state (Kiev Rus)
- Left genetic and cultural imprint across Britain, Ireland, France, and Eastern Europe
