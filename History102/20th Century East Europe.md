# 20th Century Eastern Europe

Source: History 102 podcast with Rudyard Lynch and Austin Padet
Video: [[_MOC_20th_Century_Wars]]

## Summary

The podcast argues Eastern Europe's 20th century was the greatest tragedy in human history — equivalent to the fall of Rome or the Bronze Age collapse — yet has been written out of core history. Eastern Europe was consumed by authoritarian tyranny (while Western Europe suffered from "quiet socialism"). The region endured World War I, Stalin's purges, the Russian Civil War, World War II, the Holocaust, and brutal regimes in Albania, Romania, and elsewhere. Unlike ancient tragedies, Eastern Europeans were profoundly aware of what they were doing and knew it was morally wrong.

## Key Points

- **Greatest tragedy** — the podcast argues Eastern Europe's 20th century suffering is the worst in human history, on par with the fall of Rome
- **Written out of history** — this tragedy has been systematically excluded from core historical narratives
- **East vs West** — Eastern Europe suffered authoritarian/totalitarian tyranny; Western Europe suffered "quiet socialism"
- **Marx vs Rousseau** — Eastern left based on Marx (hierarchical, structured); Western left based on Rousseau (follow inner feelings, remove tradition)
- **Merchant class absence** — unlike Anglo-Saxon countries, Eastern Europe lacked a merchant class that could restrain the state through rule of law
- **Civilizational analysis** — the Anglo-Saxon merchant-dominated system is a historic outlier, not the global norm
