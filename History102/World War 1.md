# World War I

Source: History 102 podcast with Rudyard Lynch and Austin Padet
Video: [[_MOC_20th_Century_Wars]]

## Summary

World War I was a war of prosperity fought by European powers at their demographic and industrial peak. Lynch argues it was caused fundamentally by the Industrial Revolution — rapid German unification created a power that outmatched Britain and France, while Russia was industrializing so fast that Germany calculated 1914 was their last window to win. The war's complexity (assassination cascading through alliances) obscures its core dynamic: every major power felt this was their last chance to survive.

## Key Points

- **War of Prosperity** — unlike the 30 Years' War or Hundred Years' War (wars of desperation), WW1 was fought by wealthy, young, expanding societies
- **German unification** shifted the European balance — Germany had more industry and people than Britain and France combined but no colonial empire
- **Russia's growth** terrified Germany — Russia was industrializing rapidly and had massive population growth; Germany calculated they had until 1916 to win
- **Alliance system** — Britain/France allied with Russia out of fear of Germany; Germany feared being crushed between them
- **Every power thought** this was the last time they could survive — the French and British feared German economic dominance, the Germans feared Russian military dominance
