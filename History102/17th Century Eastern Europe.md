# 17th Century Eastern Europe

**Source:** History102 with Rudyard Lynch
**Video:** [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uMi1v9V7A-o)
**Duration:** 2:00:00

## Summary

Rudyard Lynch and Eric Weinstein (original History102 co-host) cover 17th century Eastern Europe — a region of vast empires, fierce frontier societies, and profound transformations. They examine the Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth (the "noble democracy" that eventually collapsed), the rise of the Russian Empire under the Romanovs, the Cossack rebellions (Khmelnytsky Uprising), the Swedish Deluge that devastated Poland, the Ottoman Empire's advance into Europe (culminating in the Siege of Vienna 1683), and the gradual shift of power from the Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth to Russia, Prussia, and Austria. They discuss the unique social structures of Eastern Europe — the "second serfdom" that bound peasants more tightly than in the West, the role of the nobility (szlachta in Poland, boyars in Russia), and the religious divisions between Catholic, Orthodox, and Muslim worlds. They also cover the cultural achievements of the region and how the different developmental paths of Eastern and Western Europe shaped the continent's subsequent history.
