# Medieval Europe (High Middle Ages)

## Overview
The European High Middle Ages (roughly 1000–1300 AD) were a period of remarkable civilization, contradicting popular stereotypes of backwardness. Lynch argues that the Middle Ages are one of the most misunderstood eras — the stereotype and the reality are almost opposites.

## Periodization
- **Middle Ages proper**: 500–1500 AD (between the fall of Western Rome and the fall of Eastern Rome / Constantinople)
- **High Middle Ages**: c. 1000–1300 — the period of castles, cathedrals, Crusades, and the plague
- **Dark Ages**: 400–1000 AD (precedes the High Middle Ages)

## Why the Middle Ages Are Misunderstood
1. **Anti-religious bias** — academia cannot accept that a deeply religious society also flourished intellectually and technologically
2. **Whig history** — the narrative of inevitable progress treats the medieval period as an embarrassing detour
3. **Selective focus** — textbooks jump from Rome to the Renaissance, skipping 1000 years

## Achievements of High Medieval Civilization
- **Gothic cathedrals** — engineering marvels
- **Universities** — Bologna, Paris, Oxford were founded
- **Legal systems** — common law, Magna Carta
- **Technological innovation** — heavy plow, horse collar, windmills, mechanical clocks
- **Scholasticism** — synthesis of faith and reason (Thomas Aquinas)
