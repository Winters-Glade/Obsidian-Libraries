# Early Modern MOC — Age of Exploration, Renaissance & Enlightenment

> Map of Content for the early modern period of European and world history, covering the Age of Exploration, the Gunpowder Empires, the origins of modern science, the Ancien Regime, the creation of France, the Enlightenment, 17th century Europe, and the Age of Romanticism.

## Period Overview

The early modern period (roughly 1500-1800) was a time of unprecedented transformation:

- **Globalization:** European exploration unified the world's trade routes for the first time
- **Centralization:** Gunpowder enabled larger, more powerful states
- **Intellectual Revolution:** The Scientific Revolution and Enlightenment challenged traditional authority
- **Political Change:** Absolutism, constitutionalism, and revolutionary ideas competed
- **Cultural Flowering:** From Baroque to Rococo to Romanticism

## Key Themes

1. **Centralization of Power:** The shift from feudal fragmentation to centralized states, enabled by gunpowder and administrative innovation
2. **The Rise of Reason:** From the Scientific Revolution through the Enlightenment, traditional authority was challenged by empirical investigation and rational critique
3. **Global Integration:** The Columbian Exchange, colonial empires, and the first global trade networks
4. **The Democratization of Warfare:** Gunpowder leveled the battlefield between nobility and commoners (in Europe)
5. **Counter-Movements:** Romanticism, religious revival, and traditionalism as reactions to Enlightenment modernity
6. **Divergent Paths:** Why Europe and Asia took different developmental trajectories despite shared technologies
