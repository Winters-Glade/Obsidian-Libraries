# Gunpowder Empires

**Source:** History102 with Rudyard Lynch
**Video:** [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IkVhkQYty00)
**Duration:** 1:58:00

## Summary

Rudyard Lynch and Austin Padgett explore the Gunpowder Empires — the period from the Black Death to European colonialism when centralized autocratic states emerged across Asia built around gunpowder weaponry. They contrast the European experience (where gunpowder democratized warfare, broke feudalism, and led to nation-states and eventually democracy) with the Asian experience (where gunpowder enabled emperors to crush local nobilities and merchant classes, leading to social conservatism and fossilization). Key examples include Hawaii (unified by Kamehameha using guns, becoming a New England puppet state), the Ottoman Empire, Safavid Persia, and the Mughal Empire in India. They discuss how Babur used guns and horse archers to conquer northern India, the caste system's role in Hindu social rigidity, the Battle of Panipat, the Taj Mahal as cultural culmination, and how the British eventually conquered India against overwhelming odds. The episode also covers African gunpowder states like Songhai and the Sokoto Caliphate, and the Sahel region's decline due to the Atlantic slave trade.
