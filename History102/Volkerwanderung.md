# Volkerwanderung (The Migration Period)

## Overview
The Volkerwanderung — German for "The People's Wandering" — was a transformative era in European history. This period of late antiquity (between the fall of Rome and the medieval world) saw three massive migratory patterns that completely redrew the map of Europe.

## The Three Great Migrations
1. **Germanic** — conquered Western Europe and the former Western Roman Empire
2. **Hunnic & Slavic** — populated a vast territory from Greece to Russia, Berlin to Siberia
3. **Arabic/Islamic** — took North Africa and the Mediterranean

## Key Points
- The Byzantines were the only state to survive through the entire period, though internally transformed
- The map of Europe at the start and end of the Volkerwanderung was completely unrecognizable
- Study of this era is heavily reliant on German-language scholarship, making English resources scarce
