# Medieval Eastern Europe

## Overview
The formation of Eastern European states (Poland, Russia, Hungary, the Balkans) during the medieval period. Each of these societies had a wildly different social structure and background, pitted against one another in a "battle royale" for supremacy.

## Key Players
- **Poland** — Western-leaning Catholic kingdom, protector of Latin Christendom's eastern frontier
- **Russia (Kiev Rus → Muscovy)** — emerged from Viking-founded trading posts, adopted Orthodox Christianity from Byzantium
- **Hungary** — Magyar invaders who settled and converted to Catholicism, became a crucial buffer state
- **Balkan states** — Bulgaria, Serbia, Croatia — shaped by Byzantine and Slavic influences

## Why This History Is Overlooked
- Modern history writing struggles with topics that are "too religious, too personal"
- American audiences tend to flatten the distinct identities of these nations
- Eastern Europe had the potential to become a world civilization — the "battle royale" outcome was not predetermined

## Major Themes
- **The East-West Schism** — Catholic vs Orthodox Christianity created lasting cultural and political divisions
- **Mongol invasion** — devastated Russia and the steppe, set back development for centuries
- **Baltic Crusades** — the Teutonic Knights and the Christianization of the Baltic peoples
- **The rise of Poland-Lithuania** — one of Europe's largest and most tolerant early modern states
