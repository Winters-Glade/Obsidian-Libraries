# World War II — European Theater

Source: History 102 podcast with Rudyard Lynch and Austin Padet
Video: [[_MOC_20th_Century_Wars]]

## Summary

World War II in Europe was driven primarily by Hitler's ambition and the unresolved tensions of WW1. Every country that lost WW1 (Germany, Russia) or won less than desired (Japan, Italy) fell to totalitarianism. The harshness of the Treaty of Versailles — neither hard enough to destroy Germany nor soft enough to avoid resentment — created the conditions for Nazi rise. Europe's interwar period was economically catastrophic, fueling authoritarianism. Hitler's explicit goal was colonizing and genociding Eastern Europe.

## Key Points

- **WW1's aftermath** — every country that lost WW1 fell to totalitarianism; even nominal winners like Japan and Italy turned fascist because they didn't win "hard enough"
- **Treaty of Versailles** — neither hard enough nor soft enough; dismemberment would have prevented resurgence, leniency (like post-Napoleonic treatment of France) would have avoided resentment
- **Economic catastrophe** — Europe's 1920s were a depression; America prospered off European debts
- **Hitler's explicit program** — his manifesto stated goals of removing Jews and Slavs and colonizing Eastern Europe; he pursued exactly what he announced
- **Rise of big government** — even in Britain, France, and America, the Depression drove expansion of state power
- **German narrative** — Germany saw itself as victim of Jewish conspiracies, despite being the aggressor
