# Origins of Science

**Source:** History102 with Rudyard Lynch
**Video:** [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m4LxXofFziA)
**Duration:** 2:00:00

## Summary

Rudyard Lynch and Austin Padgett examine the origins of science, arguing it's one of the most important events in history — competitive with the Industrial Revolution. They debunk four common narratives: 1) we have no idea how pre-modern humanity perceived reality, 2) science pulls on ancient and medieval roots we've forgotten, 3) science and religion are not actually at odds, and 4) what we practice now is not truly science. They trace the origins of systematic knowledge from ancient Mesopotamia and Egypt, through Greek natural philosophy, Islamic Golden Age scholarship, to the European Scientific Revolution. They discuss how medieval scholasticism and the university system laid groundwork, the role of Christianity in fostering systematic inquiry (belief in a rational Creator implies a rational universe), and how the discovery of the New World (unknown to Aristotle) shattered faith in ancient wisdom, opening space for modern science. They cover key figures like Galileo, Bacon, Newton, and Descartes, and discuss how the scientific method emerged from a specific cultural and institutional context that didn't exist elsewhere.
