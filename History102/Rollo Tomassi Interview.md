# Rollo Tomassi Interview: How Dating Died

> **Source:** [History102](https://www.youtube.com/@History102-qg5oj) — [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=byWUptWmueY)

## Guest Biography

Rollo Tomassi is the author of *The Rational Male* series, a prominent voice in the manosphere and the red pill / sexual strategy space. He writes extensively on gender dynamics, hypergamy, female nature, and the modern dating market. He was joined by Jason Hartman in this interview. Also present: host Rudyard and co-host David Hamilton (author of *The Nice Guy's Guide to Dating*).

## Key Arguments & Discussion Points

### 1. The Dating Crisis as Society's #1 Problem
- Host Rudyard opens by stating the sexual/dating crisis is the #1 problem facing society, with major social and political repercussions.
- Anything that controls the birth rate has giant societal impact.
- Two-thirds of young men ages 18-30 are single; for men over 30, the number is around 80%. One-third of men at age 30 are virgins.
- Historically, when average age of marriage goes above 28, revolution follows. Large groups of sexually frustrated young men are "revolution fodder."
- Female mental health is at the worst levels in history.
- The French Revolution parallels: social/economic problems (people hungry, can't marry) became political problems.
- "Once you mess up sexual incentives among men, your society is going to fall apart."

### 2. The Pivot Point: 1965-1975
- Rollo traces everything back to one invention in 1965: **hormonal birth control** — the first time in human history that the control of reproduction was placed in the hands of one sex.
- Rollo claims hormonal birth control has been more significant than the atom bomb because nothing has done more to change the course of human reproduction.
- The decade 1965-1975 saw a confluence of changes: sexual revolution, free love movement, Playboy magazine, rise of militant feminism, Betty Friedan, Gloria Steinem.
- **No-fault divorce**: Ronald Reagan (as governor of California) signed it into law in 1969, just 4 years after the pill gained hold. This removed the penalty for hypergamous behavior in women and effectively rewarded it.
- Jason Hartman notes that no-fault divorce was a "tectonic shift" — it allowed women to continue hypergamy without penalty.

### 3. The Economic Drivers
- Government perspective: half the workforce (women in the home) didn't contribute to the tax base or GDP.
- Corporatocracy perspective: married couples use one of everything — split them up and you can sell two of everything, doubling the consumer market.
- Women entering the workforce pushed wages down (same effect as mass immigration).
- The 1971 switch to fiat currency meant credit companies wanted more participants — women getting credit cards and bank accounts was about economics, not feminism.
- Edward Bernays' "Torches of Freedom" campaign (1920s) — getting women to smoke cigarettes to double the tobacco market. "This is all about money."

### 4. The Statistics (Rollo's Data)
- **Average age of first birth**: Changed dramatically from 1971 onward. Currently closer to 30 in many countries.
- **Children per woman**: Dropped from 5 in 1970 worldwide to about 2.5 by 2015.
- **Marriage rate**: Currently 5.1 per 1,000 people — the lowest rate of marriage in recorded human history (since records began ~1850).
- **Births out of wedlock**: 42% of all births in the United States — across all demographics.
- **Inflation**: Cumulative inflation from 1970 to 2015 went from 306% to 2,326%.
- **Incarceration rates of men**: Skyrocketed from 1970 through 2010.
- **Divorce rate**: Currently 56%.

### 5. Mouse Utopia and the Technology Trap
- The "mouse utopia" experiments (John Calhoun, 1960s): Mice given infinite food and health bred to 2,000+ but broke down socially — females became aggressive, males withdrew, birth rates collapsed.
- Rudyard draws the parallel: Western society today is mouse utopia, but "worse because mice don't have cell phones, the internet, or a global sexual marketplace."
- Rollo pushes back on the "cycle of history" view (tough times → strong men → good times → weak men → hard times). He argues technology has changed the game so dramatically that cycles no longer apply.
- **World War I analogy**: Everyone expected a glorious 19th-century war; instead they got industrial-scale killing with machine guns and mustard gas. Social media is the "50 caliber machine gun" of social dynamics.
- **Genetic gap**: Technology advances so rapidly that humans cannot develop ethical best practices before the technology changes again. We're still trying to figure out Tinder and Bumble while AI revolutionizes the landscape.

### 6. Male Escapism and Sedation
- Men are becoming addicted to escapes: porn, video games, VR, OnlyFans.
- Rollo argues the "Lost Boys generation" has no incentive to participate.
- **OnlyFans is not about sex** — it's about the illusion of connection. Men pay for the "virtual girlfriend experience." The top earners are women who look attainable (a "7"), not unattainable centerfolds.
- "Textbooks in 100 years will use OnlyFans as an example of how far our society fell."
- However, Rudyard pushes back: these sedations are a Band-Aid. Under-30s know porn is not enough. Relationships are about more than sex — for many men, a woman is the only person they have a truly intimate connection with.

### 7. The Female Perspective
- Women's #1 frustration: "They can't find a high quality guy" — but they discarded him a thousand times.
- **Women have priced themselves out of the sexual marketplace**:
  - More women go to college (60/40 ratio, worse for graduation rates).
  - More women make more money than men in some states.
  - Hypergamy means women want a man who makes more money than them, is taller, more educated, funnier.
  - As women achieve more, their pool of acceptable men shrinks dramatically.
- **The Female Delusion Calculator** (demo on the show):
  - Criteria: white male, age 32-50, 6'0"+ tall, not obese, earns $100k+/year, unmarried.
  - Result: 0.33% probability of finding this man.
  - Even with lowered standards (5'10", $75k), it's still only 1.1%.
- Rollo recounts advising Robert Kiyosaki's female staff: they need to "get in touch with your feminine side" — which is a repudiation of everything they've been told since childhood. Their response: "So I have to dumb myself down?" They conflate femininity with being dumb.

### 8. The Education and 80/20 Split
- 60/40 college enrollment favoring women; men drop out at much higher rates.
- **80/20 rule in dating**: 80% of men are deemed unattractive by women. Of the remaining 20%, only about 4.5% are attractive enough that women will initiate.
- This creates "women-inspired polygyny" — women share high-value men while mid- and lower-tier men have no options.
- Rollo: "Women have become the men they wanted to marry."

### 9. Social Constructionism vs. Evolutionary Biology
- Rollo argues that every modern "ism" (feminism, social justice, etc.) rests on three legs: social constructionism, the blank slate, and emotionalism/subjectivism.
- Evolutionary psychology has disproven the blank slate since the late 70s/early 80s, but society hasn't caught up.
- Men prioritize empiricism before emotion; women prioritize emotion before reason. This is not social construction — it's biological hardware.
- "Evolutionary psychology will destroy feminism. It will destroy political ideology. It asks the right questions."
- Feminism has metastasized from basic rights into being fundamentally anti-male. It "actively disadvantages men to get equality."
- The double standard: it's okay to bash men for being not emotional enough, but not okay to suggest women should be more empirical.

### 10. The Coming Reset
- Societies don't survive without stable birth rates. Men uphold every aspect of society — they wage war, build buildings, do technological innovation. Without incentive for men, society falls apart.
- "We're only three or four Jenga pieces away from society falling apart."
- Rollo: "There is no cycle. You're more fucked than you think you are."
- Jason Hartman concluding: "It's an amazing time to be alive, but culturally we are in an epic disaster. I want to believe the pendulum swings back, but I don't see it happening."

## Points of Agreement/Disagreement

### Agreement
- All participants agree on the diagnosis: the dating market is broken, birth rates are collapsing, and the consequences will be catastrophic.
- Agreement that 1965-1975 was the pivotal turning point.
- Agreement that technology is accelerating the breakdown faster than society can adapt.
- Agreement that social constructionism has been disproven by evolutionary biology.

### Nuance
- Rollo is skeptical of the "mouse utopia" cycle theory — he argues technology has created a fundamentally unprecedented situation, not a repeating cycle.
- Rudyard believes sedations (porn, OnlyFans) are inadequate Band-Aids and that young men are still radicalizing. Rollo and Jason see them as more effective pacifiers.
- Rollo emphasizes biological/evolutionary explanations; Jason emphasizes the economic/corporate conspiracy angle, though Rollo is skeptical of deliberate "cabal" narratives and sees it as emergent behavior from human incentives.
