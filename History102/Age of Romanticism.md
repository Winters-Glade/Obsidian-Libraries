# Age of Romanticism

**Source:** History102 with Rudyard Lynch
**Video:** [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TqV9cicTu_c)
**Duration:** 2:00:00

## Summary

Rudyard Lynch and Austin Padgett explore the Age of Romanticism — the late 18th to mid-19th century cultural movement that reacted against Enlightenment rationalism and the Industrial Revolution. They examine Romanticism's emphasis on emotion, intuition, nature, individualism, and the sublime. Key figures include Goethe, Wordsworth, Coleridge, Byron, Shelley, Keats, Blake, Friedrich (painting), Beethoven, Chopin, Liszt, Wagner, and Turner. They discuss how Romanticism shaped nationalism (folk culture, language, and the Volk), the Gothic revival in architecture, the Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood, and the darker currents — the cult of the genius, the valorization of suffering, and the connection to political reaction. They also examine Romanticism's legacy in modern environmentalism, existentialism, and the continuing tension between Romantic and Enlightenment worldviews. The episode considers whether we live in a Romantic or an Enlightenment age, and whether the Romantic critique of modernity (alienation, disenchantment, loss of meaning) is more valid than ever.
