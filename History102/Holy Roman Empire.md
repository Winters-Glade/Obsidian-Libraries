# Holy Roman Empire

## Overview
The Holy Roman Empire spanned nearly 1000 years of European history (800–1806 AD). Despite its centrality to European civilization, it is severely understudied in the English-speaking world due to the post-WWII disconnection from German history.

## Key Facts
- Founded when Charlemagne was crowned Emperor in 800 AD
- Dissolved by Napoleon in 1806
- Encompassed modern Germany, Austria, Czechia, Switzerland, the Low Countries, and parts of Italy and France
- Never a centralized state — a complex patchwork of kingdoms, duchies, prince-bishoprics, and free imperial cities

## Why It's Understudied
- English-language scholarship on pre-Nazi Germany is remarkably scarce
- German is the second largest ethnic group in the US, yet knowledge of medieval German history is minimal
- Historians have actively removed German history from popular memory after WWII

## The Structure of the Empire
- Elective monarchy — the Emperor was chosen by prince-electors
- The Emperor's power was constrained by powerful nobles and the Church
- The Investiture Controversy — the struggle between Emperor and Pope for temporal authority

## Legacy
- The Empire's fragmented structure foreshadowed modern federalism
- German culture (fairy tales, folk traditions, legal concepts) shaped modern Europe
- The "Holy Roman Empire was neither holy, nor Roman, nor an empire" (Voltaire) — yet it was one of Europe's most durable political entities
