# Enlightenment

**Source:** History102 with Rudyard Lynch
**Video:** [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8GYYbXxj8QQ)
**Duration:** 2:10:02

## Summary

Rudyard Lynch and Austin Padgett explore the Enlightenment — the 18th century intellectual movement that fundamentally transformed Western thought. They examine its origins in the Scientific Revolution and its key ideas: reason over tradition, natural rights, social contract theory, separation of powers, religious toleration, and progress. They discuss major figures including Locke (tabula rasa, natural rights), Montesquieu (separation of powers), Voltaire (religious toleration), Rousseau (social contract, general will), Diderot (the Encyclopedia), Hume (empiricism, skepticism), Kant (sapere aude — dare to know), and Adam Smith (free markets). They critically examine whether the Enlightenment delivered on its promises — discussing the paradoxes of Enlightenment thinkers who advocated liberty while colonialism and slavery expanded, the French Revolution's descent into Terror, and whether reason alone can provide a foundation for morality. They also discuss the Counter-Enlightenment (Burke, de Maistre) and the legacy of Enlightenment ideas in modern political debates about universalism vs particularism, technocracy, and the role of tradition.
