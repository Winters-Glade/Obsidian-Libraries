# World War II — Pacific Theater

Source: History 102 podcast with Rudyard Lynch and Austin Padet
Video: [[_MOC_20th_Century_Wars]]

## Summary

The Pacific War stemmed from Japan's drive to conquer China and become the new Chinese Empire. Japan was the only non-European country to successfully modernize, but felt snubbed by Western powers. Unlike Germany's single dictator, Japan's descent into totalitarianism was a collective process with no clear "Hitler" — a systemic failure where Japanese cultural norms prevented anyone from saying no to the group. The Great Depression hit Japan harder than most, and the military gradually seized control.

## Key Points

- **Framework** — historian Matthew White argues the world wars were caused by the breakdown of empires: in Europe by the Ottoman collapse, in the East by the fall of the Chinese Empire
- **Pacific War as side theater** — the war in the Pacific can be viewed as a side theater to the Japanese-Chinese War, just as the European front was a side theater to the German-Russian war
- **Japanese modernization** — the only non-European country to successfully industrialize; they tried to ape Europe and expected respect
- **No single villain** — unlike Germany's Hitler, Japan had no one responsible; it was a gradual spiral where cultural norms prevented dissent
- **Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere** — Japan cloaked imperial ambitions in anti-colonial rhetoric ("Asia for Asians"), though many Japanese were genuinely idealistic and many more were cynical
- **American colonies forgotten** — Philippines was an American colony, Indonesia Dutch, Malaysia/Burma British, Vietnam/Cambodia/Laos French
