# History of Trade

**Source:** History102 with Rudyard Lynch
**Video:** [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XLRAZlJdK-w)
**Duration:** 1:50:00

## Summary

Rudyard Lynch hosts Ryan Peterson (founder/CEO of Flexport) for a special episode on the history of global trade and its implications for world civilization. They examine macro data from Angus Maddison's "Contours of the World Economy," the exponential growth of global trade since the shipping container revolution of the 1960s (which reduced costs by 90%), and the unprecedented economic growth since the end of the Cold War. They trace trade networks from the Bronze Age (with Babylon as center), through the classical ecumene (Rome to Han China via the Silk Road), the medieval Mongol-unified system, to European colonialism and the modern era. They discuss how the center of the world economy moved from Afghanistan (Eurasia's midpoint) to Europe, briefly to Greenland (post-WWII America), and debate where it's going. Other topics include gold's value over millennia, Viking-Islamic trade, China's economic influence on Southeast Asia, Rome's hyperinflation, Spain's decline from silver inflation, the Dao of economics, Keynesianism vs Austrian economics, the modern phenomenon of "fake economies," and the role of cryptocurrencies and network states.
