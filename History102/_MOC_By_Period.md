> A chronological overview of all major periods covered in the History102 vault, connecting the batch MOCs into a unified timeline.

## Prehistory & Ancient World (~5000 BCE – 500 CE)

### [[_MOC_Ancient_Classical]]
> From the first cities of Mesopotamia to the fall of the Western Roman Empire.

Beginning with the [[Mesopotamia|first civilizations in the Fertile Crescent]], the ancient world saw the rise and collapse of the Bronze Age civilizations, the flowering of [[Ancient Greece|classical Greece]], the conquests of [[Alexander the Great]], the [[Rise of Rome|ascent of Rome]], the stability of the [[Pax Romana]], and the gradual [[Fall of Rome|unraveling of the Western Empire]]. Parallel developments in [[Ancient India|India]] and [[Ancient China|China]] established the civilizational patterns that would shape the Eastern world for millennia.

## Late Antiquity & Medieval World (~500 – 1500 CE)

### [[_MOC_Medieval_Migration]]
> From the fall of Rome through the Middle Ages.

The [[Volkerwanderung|Migration Period]] reshaped Europe as Germanic, Slavic, and Arabic peoples moved across the continent. The [[Dark Age Europe|Dark Ages]] gave way to the High Middle Ages — a period of cathedral-building, university-founding, and the [[Crusades|Crusades]]. The [[Holy Roman Empire]] dominated Central Europe while [[Medieval Asia]] saw its own trajectory. [[Medieval Britain|Britain]], [[Medieval Eastern Europe|Eastern Europe]], and the [[Vikings|Viking world]] each developed distinct identities.

**9 notes** | [[_MOC_Medieval_Migration|Open MOC →]]

### [[_MOC_Steppe_Asia]]
> The horse empires of the steppe and the kingdoms of Asia.

The [[Steppe Empires|steppe peoples]] — from the [[Aryan Invasions|Aryan migrations]] to the [[Mongol Empire|Mongol conquests]] — repeatedly reshaped Eurasian civilization. The [[Silk Road]] connected East and West. [[Imperial China|China]], [[Japanese History|Japan]], [[South-East Asian Kingdoms|Southeast Asia]], and [[Tzarist Russia|Russia]] each developed distinctive civilizational patterns shaped by their geography and the steppe periphery.

**9 notes** | [[_MOC_Steppe_Asia|Open MOC →]]

### [[_MOC_Religions_Mysticism]]
> The evolution of religious belief from the Axial Age to modernity.

The [[Axial Age]] (~500 BCE) established the moral and spiritual foundations of the world's major religions. The [[Rise of Christianity]] transformed the Roman world, while the [[Rise of Islam]] reshaped the Mediterranean and beyond. [[Mysticism in History]] explores the esoteric traditions that persisted alongside organized religion. The [[Catholic Church|Catholic Church]], [[Protestant Reformation]], and [[Death of God]] trace the arc of Western Christianity from dominance to crisis.

**9 notes** | [[_MOC_Religions_Mysticism|Open MOC →]]

## Early Modern World (~1500 – 1800 CE)

### [[_MOC_Early_Modern]]
> Exploration, science, and the birth of the modern world.

The [[Age of Exploration]] united global trade routes for the first time. [[Gunpowder Empires]] centralized state power. The [[Origins of Science|Scientific Revolution]] and [[Enlightenment]] challenged traditional authority. Europe saw the rise of [[Ancien Regime|absolutism]], the [[Creation of France|creation of France as a nation]], and the cultural flowering of the [[Age of Romanticism]]. Eastern and Western Europe diverged in the crucible of the [[17th Century Western Europe|17th century]].

**10 notes** | [[_MOC_Early_Modern|Open MOC →]]

### [[_MOC_Revolutions_Empires]]
> Revolutionary upheavals and the rise of colonial empires.

The [[English Civil War]] established constitutional government in England. The [[French Revolution]] and [[Napoleonic Wars]] reshaped Europe and spread revolutionary ideals. The [[American Revolution]] created the United States. Meanwhile, the [[British Empire]], [[Spanish Empire]], and [[French Empires]] built global colonial systems — from the [[Colonialism in Asia|colonization of Asia]] to the [[African Colonialism|Scramble for Africa]] and the [[Africa's Slave Trades|transatlantic slave trade]].

**11 notes** | [[_MOC_Revolutions_Empires|Open MOC →]]

## 19th Century (~1800 – 1900)

### [[_MOC_19th_Century]]
> The Industrial Age and the transformation of society.

The [[Industrial Revolution]] fundamentally transformed economics, society, and warfare. [[Victorian Britain]] and the [[Rise+Fall of Modern Britain]] traced Britain's trajectory from industrial pioneer to global hegemon to relative decline. The [[American Civil War]] and [[Frontier Era America]] shaped the United States, while [[East Europe's Age of Empires]] saw the great multi-ethnic empires struggle with nationalism. [[Capitalism's Rise+Fall+Rise]] examined the economic system that defined the era.

**11 notes** | [[_MOC_19th_Century|Open MOC →]]

---

## 20th Century (~1900 – 2000)

### [[_MOC_20th_Century_Wars]]
> Wars, ideologies, and the reshaping of the global order.

The two World Wars — [[World War 1]] and [[WW2 in Europe|WW2]] — devastated Europe and reshaped global power. The [[Soviet Union]] attempted to build a new civilization. [[Communism]] became the dominant ideology of the 20th century. [[Mexican History]] traced three tyrannies. The cultural decades of the [[1950s]] and [[1960s]] defined modern America. [[Prussian Empire]] explored the German path to modernity through militarism.

**10 notes** | [[_MOC_20th_Century_Wars|Open MOC →]]

### [[_MOC_Cold_War_Modern]]
> The Cold War and the American era.

The [[Cold War]] divided the world into two armed camps. [[Pax Americana]] explored the post-WWII American-led order. [[Corporate Era America]] examined the managerial revolution. [[Age of Neo-Liberalism]] traced the ideological framework of the post-Cold War world. [[Age of the Last Men]] posed Nietzsche's question about bureaucratic nihilism. [[Modern China]] traced China's resurgence.

**7 notes** | [[_MOC_Cold_War_Modern|Open MOC →]]
