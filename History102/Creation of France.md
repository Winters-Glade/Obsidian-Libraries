# Creation of France

**Source:** History102 with Rudyard Lynch
**Video:** [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Qz-BgJG2rPU)
**Duration:** 2:18:00

## Summary

Rudyard Lynch and Austin Padgett explain the long and complex creation of France, from the collapse of the Western Roman Empire through the medieval period to the consolidation of French national identity. They cover the division of Charlemagne's empire, the Capetian dynasty's slow centralization of power from the Ile-de-France, the Hundred Years' War and Joan of Arc, the Wars of Religion between Catholics and Huguenots, the absolutism of Louis XIV, and the revolutionary period that forged modern French identity. They discuss geography (the natural borders of France), linguistic unification (the imposition of Parisian French over regional languages like Occitan, Breton, Alsatian), the role of the Catholic Church, and the tension between centralization and regional identity that persists to this day. They also touch on French colonial expansion, the Napoleonic Code's influence worldwide, and France's unique path to nationhood compared to other European states.
