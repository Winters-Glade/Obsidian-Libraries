# Medieval & Migration Era — Map of Content

This MOC connects all History102 videos covering the Medieval and Migration Era, from the fall of Rome to the late Middle Ages.

## The Migration Period (Volkerwanderung)
- [[Volkerwanderung]] — The great migrations (Germanic, Slavic, Arabic) that reshaped Europe

## The Dark Ages (c. 400–1000 AD)
- [[Dark Age Europe]] — Civilizational decline after Rome, the period before medieval revival
- [[Vikings]] — Scandinavian expansion during the Dark Ages (c. 800–1060)

## The High Middle Ages (c. 1000–1300 AD)
- [[Medieval Europe]] — The flowering of European civilization: cathedrals, universities, and chivalry
- [[Holy Roman Empire]] — The 1000-year entity that dominated Central Europe
- [[Crusades]] — The religious wars that defined Europe's relationship with the wider world
- [[Medieval Britain]] — Anglo-Saxon England, Norman Conquest, Magna Carta, and Parliament

## Beyond Europe
- [[Medieval Asia]] — The reversal of fortune: how Asia went from world leader to backwater
- [[Medieval Eastern Europe]] — The formation of Poland, Russia, Hungary, and the Balkans

## Chronological Flow

```
Fall of Rome (476)
    ↓
Volkerwanderung — Migration Period
    ↓
Dark Age Europe (400–1000) ← Vikings raid and settle
    ↓
Holy Roman Empire founded (800)
    ↓
Medieval Europe / High Middle Ages (1000–1300)
    ↓
Crusades (1095–1291) ← Medieval Britain develops
    ↓
Medieval Asia declines ← Medieval Eastern Europe forms
    ↓
Fall of Constantinople (1453) → Renaissance
```

## Cross-Cutting Themes
- **Barbarians and civilization** — the polarity between settled and mobile societies
- **Religious transformation** — Christianization of Europe, East-West Schism, Crusades
- **Institutional innovation** — feudalism, parliament, universities, common law
- **The Great Reversal** — Europe's rise relative to Asia during this period
- **Historiography** — how we write (and fail to write) about these eras
