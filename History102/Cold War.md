# Cold War

**Source:** History102 with Rudyard Lynch
**Video:** [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xBmX_7i3s0w)
**Duration:** 2:41:10

## Summary

A sprawling, wide-ranging conversation on the Cold War covering its origins in World War II through the fall of the Soviet Union. Rudyard and Austin argue that the Cold War began almost immediately after WWII ended, with figures like Patton and Churchill wanting to turn on Stalin while FDR pursued a more conciliatory (and arguably naive) approach. They divide the conflict geographically into the European front (centered on Germany) and the Asian front (Korea, Vietnam, China), then chronologically through American nuclear hegemony, Soviet resurgence, and the neoliberal breakthrough under Reagan and Thatcher that ultimately bankrupted the USSR. A significant portion is devoted to the Third World proxy wars — Latin America, Africa, the Middle East — where the hosts argue ideology was often a thin justification for tribal power grabs and bunker regimes. The conversation closes by framing the Cold War as a "happy ending" that nonetheless created the conditions for the "mouse utopia" problems of the present day.
