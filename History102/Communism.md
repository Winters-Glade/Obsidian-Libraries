# Communism

Source: History 102 podcast with Rudyard Lynch and Austin Padet
Video: [[_MOC_20th_Century_Wars]]

## Summary

Communism is presented as one of the great moral lessons of our time — costing nearly 100 million lives — yet the West has learned none of those lessons. The podcast argues that Marxism has so thoroughly shaped modern thought that even conservatives think in categories Marx invented. Class analysis, materialist worldviews, the oppressor-oppressed framework, government bureaucracy as an improving force — all originate from Marx. The modern left is traced back through the Illuminati and German occult movements of the 18th century.

## Key Points

- **100 million lives** — the cost of communist experiments in the 20th century, with lessons unlearned
- **Marxism's invisible dominance** — Marx invented class analysis, predicting the future via history, the oppressor-oppressed framework, materialism, economic determinism
- **No right-wing vision of the future** — the future is whatever the left says it is (more equality, technology, socialism); conservatives have no alternative vision
- **Illuminati thesis** — the modern left traces to the 18th century Bavarian Illuminati (militantly atheist, materialist, egalitarian), whose diaspora after suppression seeded German intellectual movements
- **Critical theory** — the dominant worldview inside the West today is a direct derivation of Communism
- **Chinese Communist Party** — America's greatest rival operates under communist ideology
