# The Soviet Union

Source: History 102 podcast with Rudyard Lynch and Austin Padet
Video: [[_MOC_20th_Century_Wars]]

## Summary

The Soviet Union was a direct rejection of historic common sense — an attempt to create a completely new civilization operating under different principles. Within the context of the 20th century it makes sense as a continuation of trends already visible under the Tsars. Alexis de Tocqueville's contemporaries predicted Russia and America would be the two great powers of the next century. The Soviet experiment represented a hyper-conservative autocratic society trying to radically overturn its own nature, resulting in what the podcast calls the "logical extension of insanity."

## Key Points

- **Rebellion against nature** — the Soviet Union rejected historic common sense and tried to create civilization on completely new principles
- **Continuation of Tsarist trends** — many elements of the Soviet system were extensions of patterns already in place under the Russian Empire
- **French observers** — de Tocqueville's friend wrote an anthropological report on Russia comparing it to America, seeing both as the future great powers
- **250-year imperial cycle** — thinkers predicted America would last ~250 years (the American Revolution was 1776, ~250 years ago from present)
- **Cycles of regimes** — Aristotle and Polybius established theories of regime cycles; the Founding Fathers predicted the American republic's lifespan
- **Materialist worldview** — Soviets operated on the belief that everything is material and ideas are fabrications of the establishment
