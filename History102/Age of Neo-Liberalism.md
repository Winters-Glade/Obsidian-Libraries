# Age of Neo-Liberalism

History102 video by Rudyard Lynch and Austin Padgett. Covers the political, economic, and cultural era from the end of the Cold War through Trump and COVID.

## Summary

This episode examines the rise and fall of the neoliberal order (c. 1989-2020), defined as the post-Cold War increase in capitalism and democracy that brought the greatest breakthrough in wealth and quality of life in human history within a single generation. The hosts argue neoliberalism was an attempt to reconcile two fundamentally different strands of liberalism — Anglo-Saxon (freedom from government) and French (state protection from suffering) — into a global technocratic system. They trace how the Soviet Union's collapse opened the door for capitalist democracy worldwide, but the resulting "end of history" myth became a shared illusion maintained by a coalition of bureaucrats, media, older women, and managerial elites. The 2008 financial crisis exposed deep fractures: Europe's austerity crisis (Greece, Brexit), Russia's authoritarian turn under Putin, China's state-capitalist rise, and America's growing internal divide. The COVID pandemic and Trump's political ascent ultimately shattered the neoliberal consensus, revealing the system as a fragile "terrarium" that insulated elites from consequences while atomizing populations. The video ends on a cliffhanger, predicting the neoliberal order will fall apart in some cataclysmic event, with America as the central battleground between its traditional libertarian culture and the progressive bureaucratic empire.

## Key Concepts

- **Neoliberalism**: Fusion of technocratic capitalist agenda with psychological manipulation; marketed like a product requiring plausibility, not logical consistency.
- **Two Liberalism**: Anglo-Saxon (freedom from coercion) vs. French (freedom from suffering) — fundamentally incompatible traditions reconciled only superficially.
- **The Terrarium**: The insulated, hothouse environment of modern consciousness maintained by a coalition of bureaucratic interests.
- **Natural Elites**: Aristotelian concept of people who perform great tasks and earn status through respect; suppressed by bureaucratic systems except in tech and finance.
- **Line Go Up / Line Go Down**: The material trajectory of the industrial world; COVID as the inflection point.
- **Narrator Class Collapse**: Journalists, intellectuals, and authors who once narrated lived reality went insane and betrayed the population.
