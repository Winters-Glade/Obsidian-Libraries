# The 1950s

Source: History 102 podcast with Rudyard Lynch and Austin Padet
Video: [[_MOC_20th_Century_Wars]]

## Summary

The 1950s serve as America's mythological origin point — both conservatives and liberals use it as a symbol, with the left rejecting it and the right wanting to return to it. The podcast argues this is a false dichotomy: the 1950s were already a progressive, socialist-leaning society. The decade represents the consolidation of the managerial/bureaucratic class's dominance over American culture, following its political victory in WW1 and the New Deal.

## Key Points

- **Mythological origin** — the 1950s are our society's reference point for what came before; we can't mentally conceive of the world before WWII
- **Already socialist** — the 1950s were deeply socialist in their big-government, regulated economy structure
- **Mob psychology** — the podcast discusses how modern society has been taken over by mob psychology, where the left is described as the "mob religion"
- **Managerial class** — the decade represents when the bureaucratic/managerial class consolidated cultural dominance
- **Internet amplifies** — mob psychology is exacerbated by the internet, making manipulation easier
- **No logical consistency** — political tribes flip positions rapidly without internal coherence
