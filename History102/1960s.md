# The 1960s

Source: History 102 podcast with Rudyard Lynch and Austin Padet
Video: [[_MOC_20th_Century_Wars]]

## Summary

The podcast argues the 1960s were the most important cultural decade in the West — a cultural shift on the scale of the foundation of a new religion or a new civilization. While WW1 (1910s) was the most important political decade, setting the political trajectory for the entire century, the 1960s represented the cultural revolution where the bureaucratic/managerial class gained dominance over the entire culture. The books "Leviathan and Its Enemies" (Sam Francis) and "Revolt of the Elites" (Christopher Lasch) frame the analysis.

## Key Points

- **Most important cultural decade** — the 60s represent a civilizational-scale cultural shift that has never been honestly examined
- **Managerial class takeover** — WW1 gave the managerial/bureaucratic class political dominance; the 1960s extended that to cultural dominance
- **Society's implicit moral code** — we live by a deeply horrifying implicit moral code we're not allowed to discuss: people in "oppressed" categories can't be held responsible
- **Deception at society's core** — we pretend to be a WASP patriarchal Christian society but are actually a Marxist multicultural bureaucratic atheist secular society
- **Regulatory state** — consolidation of corporate power through regulation; top 4 firms in any industry controlled 20% market share in the 1960s vs 80% today
- **Buckley's conservative movement** — post-Goldwater, Bill Buckley's National Review nested conservatism inside a neoliberal/neocon framework
