# 17th Century Western Europe

**Source:** History102 with Rudyard Lynch
**Video:** [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KdFGw2pWrlw)
**Duration:** 2:00:00

## Summary

Rudyard Lynch and Austin Padgett cover 17th century Western Europe — a century of crisis, war, and transformation. They examine the Thirty Years' War (1618-1648) that devastated Central Europe, the rise of absolutism in France under Louis XIII and Richelieu, the English Civil War and Cromwell's Commonwealth, the Dutch Golden Age and the Dutch Republic's commercial empire, and the scientific breakthroughs of the century (Galileo, Descartes, Newton, Boyle, Harvey). They discuss the Peace of Westphalia (1648) which created the modern state system, the Fronde rebellion in France, the Wars of Louis XIV, and the Glorious Revolution of 1688 in England. They examine how the 17th century was a period of economic contraction and demographic crisis across Europe (the "General Crisis" thesis), with population decline, inflation, and social unrest. They also cover the cultural achievements — the Baroque in art and music (Bernini, Caravaggio, Rembrandt, Monteverdi), the birth of opera, and the philosophical foundations of the coming Enlightenment.
