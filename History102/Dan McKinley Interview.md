# Dan McKinley Interview

> **Source:** [History102](https://www.youtube.com/@History102-qg5oj) — [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=alElTSstzqw)

## Guest Biography

Dan McKinley is a psychometrician and the founder of Personabilities.com, a platform offering multi-metric personality assessments rooted in Jungian psychology, body language analysis, and AI-based language modeling. He has developed dozens of assessments that analyze how people think, their values, skill sets, temperament, and interpersonal dynamics. His methodology integrates body language cues, microexpressions, eye movements, and psycholinguistic algorithms to infer personality traits, psychological states, character attributes, and group dynamics. He describes his work as examining the "entire matrix" of what makes someone unique: temperament (surface level), culture (subconscious programming), multi-intelligence (unconscious abilities), and neurology (Jungian archetypes).

## Key Arguments & Discussion Points

- **Jungian Four-Archetype Model** — Dan analyzes people through four Jungian archetypes: dominant (natural state), auxiliary (support under mild stress), demon/challenger (fight-or-flight), and shadow (opposite, activated under extended trauma). He applied this live to both co-hosts Rudyard and Dave.
- **Rudyard's Archetypes** — Dominant: the Hermit (wizard/monk — intelligent, revelatory, isolated truth-seeker, high fluid intelligence). Auxiliary: the Sage (otherworldly, prescient, big-picture, symbolic). Demon/Challenger: the Innocent (detail-oriented, conscientious, conventional, "vanilla"). Shadow: the Explorer (adaptable, high openness, hands-on, systemizing, argumentative). Overall: extraordinarily high openness, moderate conscientiousness, leans introverted.
- **Dave's Archetypes** — Dominant: the Everyman (strong Protestant work ethic, conscientious, practical, "letter of the law," stable, routine-oriented). Auxiliary: the Judge (left-brained, hyper-rational, high integrity, fiduciary, obstinate). Demon/Challenger: the Lover (right-brained, emotional, romantic, pliant, FOMO-driven). Shadow: the Fae (mystical, effervescent, timeless, sensitive, empathetic, story-driven). Overall: lower openness, high conscientiousness, moderately extroverted, low agreeableness, very low neuroticism.
- **Intelligence as Multi-Intelligence** — IQ tests capture only "standard G." Dan argues intelligence is multivariate — there are many types (formally assessed via Personabilities' 24 intelligence types). Rudyard's own IQ of 110 is cited as evidence that standard tests miss broader cognitive abilities.
- **Anxiety vs. Fear-Based Societies** — Post-industrial society induces anxiety (decadence, paralysis), which is distinct from fear-based tribal cultures. Anxiety drives wokeness, political tension, and discord. Pushed far enough, anxiety cultures revert to fear-based traits: superstition, tribalism, loss of causal logic, inability to make rational arguments.
- **Seven Emotions Driving History** — Both Dan and Rudyard independently compiled the same list of seven core emotions that power societal change (guilt, shame, envy, anxiety, fear, etc.), converging on the same taxonomy from separate research.
- **Competency Crisis** — Anxiety paralyzes action, leading to an impending "very, very intense competency crisis" as people stop working and producing.
- **Testosterone Decline & Generational Archetype Shifts** — Testosterone has dropped in free fall across generations; archetypes that were once common among boomers are now rarer in zoomers. Dave, while masculine by zoomer standards, is less so compared to boomer norms.
- **Self-Knowledge as Meritocracy** — Understanding your true personality, values, and archetypes lets you make better decisions about career, relationships, and life path. Dan believes "fulfilling the measure of our creation" means doing what one is naturally suited for — a system of meritocracy putting the right people in the right roles.
- **Critique of Western Psychology** — Dan considers most Anglo psychology (except Jung and Freud) "complete trash." Eastern psychology (Indian, Chinese, Russian) is far more developed, especially regarding consciousness, multi-layered understanding, and tiers of self-development.
- **Physiognomy as Correlative Art, Not Causal Science** — Facial features correlate with hormones and genetics but don't fully determine psychology. Physiognomy was studied by Western academics until WWII, after which it became politically taboo. Dan treats it as one data point among many, checked for congruence with body language and other cues.
- **Astrology as Proto-Data Science** — Ancient astrology was computational analysis from antiquity, practiced by Pythagoras, Newton, and other foundational thinkers. Modern pop astrology is "complete BS," but the correlative, archetypal approach of historical astrology has value. Birth season correlates with personality (e.g., summer-born more extroverted, higher income).
- **Personabilities.com Assessments** — Four distinct assessments: (1) Intelligence Types (24 types, unconscious operating system), (2) Culture Test (values, subconscious beliefs, "programming of the mind"), (3) Temperament Test (surface personality, how others see you), (4) Neurology Test (collective unconscious, "hardware of the mind," permanent traits).
- **Psycholinguistics** — Dan has done extensive work on the causal link between word choice and later behavior, studying how language reveals and predicts actions.

## Points of Agreement/Disagreement

- **Agreement:** Dan and Rudyard independently arrived at the same list of seven core emotions driving historical change — a convergence they both found striking.
- **Agreement:** Both share a deep skepticism of mainstream Western psychology, favoring Jungian, Eastern, and esoteric frameworks for understanding consciousness and personality.
- **Agreement:** Shared diagnosis that modern anxiety-based societies are decadent, and that the logical endpoint of wokeness is reversion to pre-modern tribal social values.
- **Agreement:** Strong alignment on the importance of self-knowledge for personal fulfillment and on putting people in roles suited to their nature (meritocracy).
- **Agreement:** Both view physiognomy and astrology as correlative rather than causal — useful as arts, not hard sciences.
- **Agreement:** Recognition that chronic stress and trauma fundamentally reshape neurological nature and activate deeper/shadow archetypes.
- **(Soft) Disagreement:** Dave presses on whether these frameworks are actionable or merely interesting. Dan emphasizes practical application through the Personabilities assessments.
- **(Soft) Disagreement:** Dave questions whether planetary positions at birth could have any causal effect, while Dan notes there is some evidence for seasonal/hormonal correlations with personality.
