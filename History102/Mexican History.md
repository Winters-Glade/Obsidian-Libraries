# Mexican History

Source: History 102 podcast with Rudyard Lynch and Austin Padet
Video: [[_MOC_20th_Century_Wars]]

## Summary

Mexico is the only country in North America where a pre-established native civilization has survived to the present, with half of Mexico's blood being of native origin. Historian T.R. Fehrenbach splits Mexican history into three tyrannies: tyranny by the land (native era), tyranny by the Spanish (colonial period), and tyranny of the past (independence era). Mexico's central problem is its inability to "hit escape velocity" — to clean up its own act to the point of organically spilling influence outward like Rome, Britain, or China.

## Key Points

- **Native survival** — half of Mexico's population is of native origin today, unique in North America
- **Three tyrannies** — tyranny by the land (native), tyranny by the Spanish (colonial), tyranny of the past (independent)
- **Escape velocity** — great nations don't decide to be great; they clean up their internal problems so thoroughly that dominance happens organically
- **Organic vs. Great Man empires** — Rome, Britain, America formed empires unintentionally; Alexander, Napoleon, Genghis Khan were "great man" empires that couldn't sustain themselves after the leader died
- **Mesoamerican civilization** — oldest sites date to 500 BC (likely older); Spanish destroyed most records; the Olmec were the first Mesoamerican civilization
- **Civilizational cycles** — Fehrenbach fits Mesoamerica into Spengler/Quigley-style civilizational cycles
