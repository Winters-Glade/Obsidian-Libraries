# Age of Exploration

**Source:** History102 with Rudyard Lynch
**Video:** [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ykEnbkdi95Q)
**Duration:** 2:18:00

## Summary

Rudyard Lynch and Austin Padgett discuss the Age of Exploration (15th-17th centuries), arguing it's arguably one of the most important events in history — the unification of the entire world by European seafarers. They cover the technological and navigational breakthroughs that enabled long-distance ocean travel, the European desire to reach Asia (inspired by Marco Polo), the Treaty of Tordesillas dividing the world between Spain and Portugal, the conquests of the Aztec and Inca empires by Cortez and Pizarro, Magellan's circumnavigation, and the Columbian Exchange. They discuss the "Faustian spirit" (via Oswald Spengler) as the uniquely European drive to explore and conquer, the role of gunpowder and advanced ships, and the devastating 90% population loss in the Americas from disease. Other topics include the Northwest Passage, the Piri Reis map, the search for El Dorado, Sir Francis Drake's circumnavigation, and the broader philosophical implications of discovering continents unknown to ancient wisdom.
