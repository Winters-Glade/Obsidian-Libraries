# L'Ancien Regime

**Source:** History102 with Rudyard Lynch
**Video:** [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ukdwV99lI6M)
**Duration:** 2:15:00

## Summary

Rudyard Lynch and Austin Padgett cover 18th century Europe (1648-1789), also known as the Ancien Regime — a historical plateau between the wars of religion and the French Revolution. They describe it as a highly aristocratic, cultivated, and civilized culture that ultimately birthed the Enlightenment. Topics include: the consolidation of absolute monarchy (Louis XIV's Versailles system), the aristocratic reaction, the science of the Enlightenment (Newton, Locke), the development of the public sphere (salons, coffeehouses, print culture), the fiscal crisis of the old regime, and the growing tensions between the Third Estate and the nobility. They discuss how France's inability to reform its tax system (because the nobility refused to be taxed) led to the Revolution, the role of the Parlements as obstructionist bodies, the American Revolution's influence on French thinkers, and the economic conditions that made revolution possible. They also cover daily life under the Ancien Regime — the three estates, rural vs urban life, the growth of the bourgeoisie, and the cultural achievements of the period including Rococo art, classical music, and Enlightenment philosophy.
