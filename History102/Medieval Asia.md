# Medieval Decline of Asia

## Overview
In 1000 AD, Asia was vastly more advanced than Europe in almost every metric — technology, economy, culture, and geopolitics. By 1500, Europeans could defeat large parts of the Asian ecosystem with small expeditionary forces. This video explores how and why Asia fell behind during the medieval period.

## The Great Reversal
- **1000 AD**: China, India, and the Islamic world were the centers of global civilization
- The Silk Road connected the four great civilizations
- Asian technological superiority in printing, gunpowder, navigation, and administration

## Causes of Asian Decline
1. **Fiat currency collapse** — China's Song Dynasty introduced paper currency; its crash destabilized the economy
2. **Mongol invasions** — devastated the Islamic world and China, disrupted the Silk Road
3. **Institutional stagnation** — bureaucratic systems that had once enabled advancement became rigid and extractive
4. **Naval retreat** — Ming Dynasty abandoned the treasure fleet (Zheng He), ceding the oceans to Europe

## The Economic Center of Gravity
- Shifted from between Persia and India (medieval period) westward toward Europe
- By the early modern period, Europe had become the center of the global economy
- A brief post-WWII moment saw the economic center near Greenland (between America and Europe)

## Lessons
- Gradualistic change over centuries can produce decisive historical shifts
- The decline was invisible to those living through it — only visible in retrospect
- Technological leads are not permanent; institutions and policies determine long-term outcomes
