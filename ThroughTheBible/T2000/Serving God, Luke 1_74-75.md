## Summary

Luke 1:74-75 records Zacharias' prophecy that we, being delivered from our enemies, might serve God without fear in holiness and righteousness before Him all our days. Pastor Chuck explores the nature of true service to God — not motivated by fear or obligation, but flowing from gratitude for deliverance. The passage reveals God's desire for a people who serve Him freely, joyfully, and consistently.

## 📖 Research Context

Zacharias' Benedictus (Luke 1:68-79) is a prophetic song saturated with Old Testament imagery — the horn of salvation from David's house (Psalm 132:17), the covenant with Abraham (Genesis 12:1-3), and the prophetic hope of deliverance. The phrase 'serve Him without fear' echoes the original purpose of the Exodus: 'Let My people go, that they may serve Me' (Exodus 7:16). True service flows from redemption, not obligation — Israel was delivered FROM Egyptian bondage FOR the worship of God. This note captures the essence of covenant theology: God's salvation always has a purpose — that we might serve Him in holiness and righteousness.

Key Old Testament cross-references: Deuteronomy 6:13 (fear the Lord and serve Him), Joshua 24:14-15 (choose whom you will serve), Psalm 100:2 (serve the Lord with gladness), Malachi 3:16-18 (the Lord's treasured possession).

## 📜 Historical Background

Zacharias' prophecy, known as the Benedictus, was spoken after the birth of John the Baptist. Zacharias had been struck mute for doubting the angel Gabriel's announcement, and when he confirmed John's name, his speech was restored. Filled with the Holy Spirit, he prophesied about God's visitation to redeem His people. The prophecy draws heavily on Old Testament themes of covenant, deliverance, and the Abrahamic promise. Luke 1:74-75 captures the purpose of redemption: not merely rescue from enemies, but the positive call to serve God in holiness.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

Hebrews 9:14 speaks of Christ's blood purging our conscience to serve the living God. Romans 6:22 describes the fruit of holiness as the result of being made free from sin. 1 Peter 1:15-16 calls believers to be holy as He who called us is holy. 1 Peter 1:18-19 expands this — we were redeemed with the precious blood of Christ, not with perishable things, purchased for a life of holy service. Titus 2:11-14 connects the grace of God to our training in righteousness, teaching us to renounce ungodliness and live self-controlled, upright, and godly lives as we await our blessed hope. Revelation 5:9-10 declares that we were redeemed by Christ's blood from every tribe and tongue to become a kingdom of priests who reign for God. The theme of serving without fear connects to 1 John 4:18 — perfect love casts out fear. The book of Hebrews repeatedly emphasizes that Christ's once-for-all sacrifice enables us to draw near and serve God acceptably.

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith taught that many people serve God out of fear — fear of punishment, fear of missing out, fear of what others think. But God wants us to serve Him from a place of deliverance and freedom. When we truly understand what Christ has done for us — delivering us from the dominion of sin and death — our service becomes a joyful response rather than a fearful obligation. The key is serving "without fear" — a liberated service that flows from a secure relationship with a loving Father.
