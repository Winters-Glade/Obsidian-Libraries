# 226 Our Spiritual Warfare — 2 Corinthians 10:3-5

> **Series:** T2000 Topical Series | **Pastor:** Chuck Smith | **Video:** [#226](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=24D8MHFvFkk)

## Overview

Though we walk in the flesh, we do not war according to the flesh. The weapons of our warfare are not carnal but mighty through God for pulling down strongholds. Paul calls believers to cast down arguments and every high thing that exalts itself against the knowledge of God, bringing every thought into captivity to the obedience of Christ. This passage reframes the conflicts of the Christian life as fundamentally spiritual, not merely human. The strongholds Paul targets are not physical fortresses but intellectual and spiritual barriers — the proud arguments and pretensions that rebel against God's authority.

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Spiritual Warfare | The Christian's battle is not waged against flesh and blood but against spiritual forces — the weapons are divine, not human |
| Strongholds | Satan's fortresses of deception — false philosophies, pride, and arguments that exalt themselves against the knowledge of God |
| Obedience | The goal of spiritual warfare is not destruction but liberation — bringing every thought into captivity to the obedience of Christ |
| Divine Power | The weapons of our warfare derive their power from God, not from human strength or strategy |
| Discerning Thoughts | The battlefield is the mind — thoughts, imaginations, and intellectual pretensions must be taken captive for Christ |

## Pauline Context

2 Corinthians 10-13 is Paul's most poignant and passionate defense of his apostolic ministry against false teachers who questioned his authority. The spiritual warfare passage (10:3-5) introduces the section, establishing that Paul's battle is not personal but cosmic — he is not fighting the Corinthians but the spiritual deceptions that have captivated them. The military imagery — warfare (strateia), weapons (hopla), strongholds (ochyrōmata), pulling down (kathairesis) — reflects the intensity of the conflict. This passage anticipates the full armor of God in Ephesians 6:10-18, where Paul names specific pieces of spiritual armor. The "knowledge of God" (gnōsis tou theou) that the strongholds oppose is the true knowledge Paul has been defending, in contrast to the false knowledge claimed by his opponents.
