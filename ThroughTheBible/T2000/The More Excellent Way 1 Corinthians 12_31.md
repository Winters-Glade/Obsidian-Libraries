# 219 The More Excellent Way — 1 Corinthians 12:31

> **Series:** T2000 Topical Series | **Pastor:** Chuck Smith | **Video:** [#219](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GBzMU2xz2qQ)

## Overview

After discussing the variety of [[Spiritual Gifts]], Paul directs his readers to 'a more excellent way' — the way of love. Love is the essential context for the exercise of all [[Spiritual Gifts]]. Without it, the most spectacular manifestations are empty and meaningless. Paul's transition from [[Spiritual Gifts]] to the love chapter is one of the most important pivots in his letters — he redirects the Corinthians from their fascination with the more visible gifts to the foundational virtue without which all gifts ring hollow. The "more excellent way" (kath' hyperbolēn hodon) is not a new gift but the character of Christ Himself.

## Pauline Context

1 Corinthians 12:31 is the hinge verse connecting Paul's teaching on [[Spiritual Gifts]] (1 Corinthians 12) to his exposition of love (1 Corinthians 13). The Greek phrase "kath' hyperbolēn hodon" suggests an extraordinary or surpassing way — not just better but supremely excellent. Paul's argument is that the Corinthian [[church]] was preoccupied with the more spectacular gifts (tongues, prophecy) while neglecting love, the true mark of Christian maturity. This theme runs throughout Paul's letters — in Romans 13:8-10, love fulfills the law; in Galatians 5:6, faith works through love; in Ephesians 4:15-16, speaking the truth in love causes the body to grow. For Paul, love is not merely one Christian virtue among many but the very life of Christ manifested in believers.
