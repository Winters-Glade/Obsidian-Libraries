# 211 Unsearchable Riches — Romans 11:33-36

> **Series:** T2000 Topical Series | **Pastor:** Chuck Smith | **Video:** [#211](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S3qHRbfEEkA)

## Overview

Paul breaks into a spontaneous doxology of praise as he contemplates the depth of God's wisdom and knowledge. God's judgments are unsearchable and His ways past finding out. All things are from Him, through Him, and to Him — to whom be glory forever. This is the climax of Paul's three-chapter reflection on God's dealings with Israel (Romans 9-11). After tracing the mysteries of election, hardening, and ultimate restoration, Paul can only worship. The doxology summarizes Paul's entire theology: all things originate from God (source), are sustained through God (means), and exist for God (purpose).

## Pauline Context

Romans 11:33-36 is the concluding doxology to chapters 9-11, the most sustained theological argument in Paul's letters. The doxology echoes Old Testament wisdom literature (especially Job 11 and Isaiah 40) and reflects Paul's rabbinic training in its structure — three rhetorical questions followed by a declaration of God's incomprehensibility. The key phrase "for of Him and through Him and to Him are all things" (ex autou kai di' autou kai eis auton ta panta) is the theological foundation for Paul's cosmic Christology in Colossians 1:16-17 and Ephesians 1:10, where all things are created by Christ, through Christ, and for Christ. This doxology mirrors the pattern of Paul's letters: deep theological reflection leads to worship, not merely instruction.
