## Summary

John 11:25-27 records Jesus' profound declaration to Martha at the tomb of Lazarus: 'I am the Resurrection and the Life: he that believeth in Me, though he were dead, yet shall he live: and whosoever liveth and believeth in Me shall never die.' Pastor Chuck explores Christ's victory over death and the question Jesus asks every person: 'Believest thou this?' The resurrection of Lazarus foreshadows Jesus' own resurrection and guarantees ours.

## 📖 Research Context

This is the fifth 'I AM' statement in John's Gospel — 'I AM the Resurrection and the Life.' The context is Jesus' most dramatic miracle: raising Lazarus from the dead. The deliberate delay of two days (John 11:6) was so that Lazarus would be four days in the tomb — Jewish tradition held that the soul lingered near the body for three days, hoping for return; after day four, death was irreversible. The statement 'I AM the Resurrection and the Life' is the climax of John's revelation of Jesus' divine power over death — surpassing all the other I AM statements in its direct claim to authority over the final enemy. Martha's confession — 'I believe that You are the Christ, the Son of God, who was to come into the world' — mirrors Peter's confession in Matthew 16:16 and echoes the purpose statement of the Gospel (John 20:31). This miracle is the seventh and greatest sign in John's Gospel (turning water to wine, healing the official's son, healing at Bethesda, feeding the 5,000, walking on water, healing the blind man, raising Lazarus). It is the sign that directly precipitates the decision to kill Jesus (John 11:45-53).

Key Old Testament cross-references: 1 Kings 17:17-24 (Elijah raises the widow's son), 2 Kings 4:18-37 (Elisha raises the Shunammite's son), Isaiah 26:19 (Your dead shall live; my dead bodies shall arise), Daniel 12:2 (many who sleep in the dust of the earth shall awake).

## 📜 Historical Background

Lazarus of Bethany was the brother of Mary and Martha — a family Jesus loved. When Lazarus fell ill, Jesus deliberately delayed two days before going to Bethany, knowing Lazarus would die so that God's glory could be displayed. By the time Jesus arrived, Lazarus had been in the tomb four days — Jewish tradition held that the soul hovered near the body for three days, so after day four there was no hope. Martha's confession of faith — 'I believe that You are the Christ, the Son of God' — is one of the highest Christological declarations in the Gospels.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

1 Corinthians 15:20-22 — Christ is the firstfruits of the resurrection. 1 Corinthians 15:55-57 — death is swallowed up in victory; O death, where is your sting? The sting of death is sin, but thanks be to God who gives us the victory through our Lord Jesus Christ. Romans 6:5 — if we have been united with Him in a death like His, we shall certainly be united with Him in a resurrection like His. 1 Thessalonians 4:14 — if we believe that Jesus died and rose again, even so God will bring with Him those who sleep in Jesus. Philippians 3:10-11 — the power of His resurrection. Revelation 1:18 — I am He who lives, and was dead, and behold I am alive forevermore.

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith noted that Martha believed in the resurrection 'at the last day' — a future event. Jesus redirected her faith from a distant hope to a present Person. The resurrection is not just a doctrine or an event — it is a Person. 'I AM the Resurrection.' Faith in Christ brings eternal life that begins now, not just after death. The question Jesus asked Martha is the question He asks everyone: 'Believest thou this?'
