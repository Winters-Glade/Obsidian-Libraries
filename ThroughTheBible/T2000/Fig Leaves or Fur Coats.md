## Summary

This teaching contrasts two kinds of covering found in [[Genesis 30_1-43.md]]. After eating the forbidden fruit, Adam and Eve's immediate response is to sew fig leaves together to cover their nakedness — a symbol of humanity's first attempt at self-atonement, covering sin with human effort. But God, in His mercy, provides a different covering: coats of animal skins. This requires the death of an animal — the first [[sacrifice]] in Scripture — pointing to the foundational truth that without the shedding of blood there is no remission of sin. The contrast between fig leaves (man's inadequate covering) and fur coats (God's provision through sacrifice) sets the pattern for the entire biblical story: human religion versus divine [[Redemption]].

## Key Takeaways

1. Fig leaves represent every human attempt to deal with sin through our own efforts — good works, religion, morality, philosophy.
2. God rejects fig leaves; He requires a covering that involves the shedding of blood.
3. The coats of skin point forward to Christ — the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world.
4. God not only forgives sin but clothes us with His righteousness.
5. The contrast between fig leaves and fur coats is the contrast between religion and the gospel — what we do for God versus what God does for us.

## Further Context

### Historical & Cultural Background
Fig leaves were a practical choice in the ancient Near East — the *Ficus carica* was abundant in Canaan, and its large, broad leaves could be sewn together to form a crude covering. However, fig leaves quickly wither and crack, making them a temporary and inadequate solution. Animal skins, by contrast, required the death of an animal — a costly covering that established the principle of substitutionary sacrifice. The Hebrew word for "coats" (*kuttoneth*) is the same word used for the high priest's tunic, suggesting a link between God's clothing of Adam and Eve and the later priestly garments.

### Old Testament Connections
- **Isaiah 64:6** — "All our righteousnesses are as filthy rags" — the prophetic verdict on all human attempts at self-justification, echoing the insufficiency of fig leaves.
- **Zechariah 3:1-5** — Joshua the high priest stands before the Lord in filthy garments; God removes them and clothes him with rich apparel — a prophetic picture of imputed righteousness.
- **Exodus 28:39-43** — The priestly garments made of fine linen, covering nakedness in the service of God — the replacement of fig leaves with God-provided covering for ministry.
- **Proverbs 31:25** — "Strength and honour are her clothing" — the virtuous woman clothed not with fig leaves but with the character that comes from God.

### New Testament Connections
- **2 Corinthians 5:21** — "He hath made him to be sin for us... that we might be made the righteousness of God in him" — the ultimate exchange, where Christ takes our sin and gives us His righteousness.
- **Galatians 3:27** — "For as many of you as have been baptized into Christ have put on Christ" — the believer is clothed with Christ Himself, the final fulfilment of the coats of skin.
- **Revelation 19:8** — "Arrayed in fine linen, clean and white: for the fine linen is the righteousness of saints" — the wedding garment of the Bride of Christ.
- **Romans 3:21-26** — The righteousness of God apart from the law is revealed — God's own righteousness provided for those who believe.
- **Philippians 3:8-9** — Paul counts all things loss that he may be found in Christ, not having his own righteousness which is of the law but that which is through faith.

### Extra-Biblical Sources
- The early church fathers (Irenaeus, *Against Heresies* 3.23.5) saw the coats of skin as God's accommodation to humanity's fallen state and a type of the mortal body.
- Jewish midrash (Pirke De-Rabbi Eliezer, ch. 20) teaches that the garments God made were from the skin of the ram that Abraham later offered on Mount Moriah — linking creation, fall, and redemption.
- Augustine (*City of God* 13.13) contrasted the fig leaves of human pride with the divine humility that provides real covering.
