# 200 The Gospel of Grace — Acts 20:24

> **Series:** T2000 Topical Series | **Pastor:** Chuck Smith | **Video:** [#200](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xEeLyznjNXw)

## Overview

Paul's farewell address to the Ephesian elders at Miletus — one of the most emotionally moving passages in Acts. Paul knows he is heading to Jerusalem where 'chains and tribulations await.' His declaration — 'None of these things move me; nor do I count my life dear to myself, so that I may finish my race with joy, and the ministry which I received from the Lord Jesus, to testify to the gospel of the grace of God' — encapsulates his apostolic commitment.

## 📜 Historical Background

Miletus, located on the western coast of Asia Minor (modern-day Turkey) near the mouth of the Maeander River, was an ancient city with a rich history as a center of Greek philosophy and commerce. When Paul summoned the Ephesian elders to meet him there (a journey of about 30 miles south), he was en route to Jerusalem, determined to complete his ministry despite the Holy Spirit's warnings of imprisonment awaiting him (Acts 20:23). This farewell address is unique in Acts as the only speech directed to Christian leaders, making it a pastoral testament of extraordinary depth. The emotional scene includes Paul's declaration that he did not covet anyone's silver or gold, his quotation of Jesus' words 'It is more blessed to give than to receive' (a saying not recorded in the Gospels), and the poignant moment when the elders wept and kissed him.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

Paul's farewell to the Ephesian elders resonates deeply with his later letters. His declaration 'I have finished my race' in Acts 20:24 echoes 2 Timothy 4:6-8, written near the end of his life: 'I have fought the good fight, I have finished the race, I have kept the faith.' The 'gospel of the grace of God' (to euangelion tes charitos tou Theou) summarizes Paul's entire theology — salvation is entirely God's unmerited favor. His charge to the elders to 'shepherd the church of God, which He purchased with His own blood' (Acts 20:28) is a high view of the church. The warning about 'savage wolves' (Acts 20:29) foreshadows the false teachers Paul confronts in his pastoral epistles (1 Timothy 1:3-7, 2 Timothy 3:1-9).

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith often preached from this passage about finishing well. Paul's determination — 'none of these things move me' — is the mark of a person whose life is completely surrendered to God's purpose. Smith taught that the greatest tragedy is not starting the race poorly but failing to finish it well. Paul's motivation was the gospel of grace — the good news that sinners are saved not by works but by God's unmerited favor. Smith emphasized that grace is not just the starting point of the Christian life but the sustaining power for every step. When Paul said he did not count his life dear to himself, he modeled the paradox of the gospel: only those who lose their lives for Christ's sake truly find them (Matthew 16:25).
