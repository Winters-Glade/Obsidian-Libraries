# 203 From Darkness to Light — Acts 26:18

> **Series:** T2000 Topical Series | **Pastor:** Chuck Smith | **Video:** [#203](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HdLNq6MuRUE)

## Overview

Paul recounts before Agrippa the commission he received from Christ on the Damascus Road. Acts 26:18 captures the essence of Christian conversion in five transformative movements: opening eyes, turning from darkness to light, turning from the power of Satan to God, receiving forgiveness of sins, and receiving an inheritance among those sanctified by faith in Christ. This verse is often called 'Paul's Great Commission.'

## 📜 Historical Background

Paul's defense before King Herod Agrippa II took place in the audience hall of the Herodian palace in Caesarea. Agrippa II was the last king of the Herodian dynasty, ruling territories granted by the Roman emperor. He was known for his expertise in Jewish customs and Scriptures — Festus sought his counsel precisely because of this (Acts 25:26-27). Paul's speech is his third defense in three chapters and the most detailed account of his conversion. Acts 26:18 captures the commission in five transformative movements: (1) opening eyes — removing spiritual blindness; (2) turning from darkness to light — moral transformation; (3) turning from the power of Satan to God — changing allegiances; (4) receiving forgiveness of sins — pardon for the past; (5) receiving an inheritance among the sanctified — a secure future.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

The five-fold commission in Acts 26:18 summarizes the entire New Testament message of salvation. It echoes the transfer 'from the power of darkness into the kingdom of the Beloved Son' in Colossians 1:12-14, where Paul describes redemption as deliverance, transfer, and inheritance. Peter uses the same language in 1 Peter 2:9-10, calling believers 'a chosen generation, a royal priesthood' who were 'not a people but are now the people of God.' The opening of blind eyes fulfills Isaiah's messianic prophecy (Isaiah 42:6-7, 61:1 — quoted by Jesus in Luke 4:18). The inheritance among the sanctified by faith echoes the promised inheritance to Abraham (Galatians 3:14) and the 'incorruptible inheritance reserved in heaven' (1 Peter 1:4).

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith frequently preached from this 'mini-Gospel' within Acts, calling Acts 26:18 the most complete description of salvation in Scripture. He highlighted the five transformational movements as a checklist for genuine conversion: blind eyes opened, a decisive turn from darkness and Satan, forgiveness of sins, and the assurance of an eternal inheritance. Smith emphasized that salvation is not merely believing facts about Jesus but a radical transfer from one kingdom to another. The apostle Paul himself was the living demonstration: the most unlikely candidate, a violent persecutor, became the greatest missionary. Smith encouraged every believer to remember their own 'Damascus Road' moment — when Christ opened their eyes and turned them from darkness to light.
