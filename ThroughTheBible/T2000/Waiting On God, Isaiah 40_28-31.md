## Summary

'But they that wait upon the Lord shall renew their strength; they shall mount up with wings as eagles.' This iconic passage marks the transition in [[Isaiah]] from [[Judgment]] to [[Restoration|comfort]]. Pastor Chuck explores what it truly means to wait on the Lord and the supernatural strength that comes from dependence on Him.

## 📜 Historical Background

[[_Bible/Isaiah/Isaiah 40.md]] opens the second major section of Isaiah (chapters 40-66), often called the 'Book of Comfort.' The historical situation shifts from [[Judgment]] to future [[Restoration]]. The people are in captivity, and God speaks words of comfort: 'Comfort ye, comfort ye my people.' The chapter emphasizes God's incomparable greatness — He measures the waters in the hollow of His hand, sits above the circle of the earth, and calls the stars by name. Against this backdrop of God's majesty, the [[Prophecy|promise]] that those who wait on Him will renew their strength takes on profound significance. This period of Israel's history is also addressed by the [[Prophecy|prophetic]] ministries of [[Jeremiah]], [[Ezekiel]], and [[Daniel]].

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

The 'voice crying in the wilderness' of [[Isaiah 40_3]] is identified by all four Gospels as [[John the Baptist]], preparing the way for Jesus ([[Matthew 3_3]], [[Mark 1_3]], [[Luke 3_4]], [[John 1_23]]). Jesus Himself promises rest and renewal in [[_Bible/Matthew/Matthew 11_28-30.md]]. Paul speaks of 'renewing the mind' in [[Romans 12_2]]. The eagle's wings imagery is echoed in [[Revelation 12_14.md]], where the woman is given eagle's wings to escape persecution — symbolizing God's sustaining power.

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith taught that 'waiting' is not passive resignation but active trust. The Hebrew word 'qavah' implies looking expectantly, like a [[servant]] watching for their master's signal. Waiting on God means exchanging our limited strength for His unlimited power. The three verbs — mount up, run, walk — show that God's strength meets us at every stage: soaring in high moments, running in seasons of activity, and walking faithfully through everyday challenges.
