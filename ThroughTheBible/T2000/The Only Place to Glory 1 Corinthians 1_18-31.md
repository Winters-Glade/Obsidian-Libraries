# 214 The Only Place to Glory — 1 Corinthians 1:18-31

> **Series:** T2000 Topical Series | **Pastor:** Chuck Smith | **Video:** [#214](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rkwN8gHzTlw)

## Overview

The message of the cross is foolishness to those who are perishing but the power of God to those being saved. God chose the foolish things of the world to confound the wise, so that no flesh should glory in His presence. Christ Jesus is made unto us wisdom, righteousness, sanctification, and redemption. Paul here confronts the Corinthian church's fascination with human wisdom and eloquence by exalting the cross as the only true ground of boasting. The cross overturns every human value system — what the world calls wisdom, God calls foolishness, and what the world calls weakness, God calls power.

## Pauline Context

1 Corinthians 1:18-31 is Paul's definitive critique of the Corinthian obsession with rhetorical wisdom and status. Paul's contrast between the wisdom of the world and the wisdom of God reflects his conviction that the cross fundamentally reorders all human values. The fourfold description of Christ — wisdom, righteousness, sanctification, redemption — summarizes Paul's soteriology: Christ is everything believers need. This passage anticipates Paul's argument in 2 Corinthians 10-13, where he boasts in his weaknesses so that Christ's power may rest on him. Paul's use of Isaiah 29:14 and Jeremiah 9:23-24 shows that the Old Testament already anticipated God's reversal of human values. The theme of boasting excluded by grace runs through Romans 3:27 and Ephesians 2:8-9.
