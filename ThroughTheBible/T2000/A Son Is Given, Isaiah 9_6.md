## Summary

Isaiah 9:6 proclaims the birth of the promised child: 'For unto us a Child is born, unto us a Son is given.' This [[Prophecy|Messianic prophecy]] unveils the divine names of the coming King — Wonderful, Counselor, The Mighty God, The Everlasting Father, The Prince of Peace. The titles directly affirm the [[deity]] of the [[Messiah]].

## 📜 Historical Background

Isaiah 9:6-7 is one of the most famous Messianic prophecies. It follows the Immanuel prophecy of Isaiah 7:14. The four throne-names reflect His divine nature and eternal reign.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

The angel Gabriel announces to Mary that she will conceive a Son named Jesus (Luke 1:31-33). Matthew directly quotes Isaiah 9:1-2 in reference to Jesus' ministry (Matthew 4:14-16).

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith often emphasized that 'the Child born' speaks of His humanity; 'the Son given' speaks of His deity. He was born in time but given from [[eternity]].

## 📚 Hebrew Poetry & Prophetic Context

Isaiah 9:6 is part of the **Immanuel prophecy cycle** (Isaiah 7-12), a collection of Messianic oracles. The four throne-names form a graded **parallelism** moving from earthly to divine: Wonderful Counselor to The Mighty God to The Everlasting Father to The Prince of Peace. The Hebrew perfect tense (*yulad-lanu*) speaks of a future event with the certainty of something already accomplished — a hallmark of Hebrew [[Prophecy]]. Though this lecture appears in the Psalms section of the T2000 playlist, its content connects the poetic and prophetic traditions.

## 📖 Research Context

### The Messianic Oracle

Isaiah 9:6 is part of **Isaiah's Book of Immanuel** (chapters 7-12), addressed to King Ahaz during the Syro-Ephraimite War (734-732 BC). The [[Prophecy]] moves from the sign of Immanuel (7:14) through increasingly explicit Messianic dimensions. The Hebrew perfect tense verbs — "a child is born, a son is given" — suggest certainty: it is as good as done.

### The Four-Fold Title

Each compound name uses the divine name:
1. **Pele' Yo'etz** (פֶּלֶא יוֹעֵץ) — "Wonderful Counselor" — *pele* denotes something beyond human capability, a miracle
2. **El Gibbor** (אֵל גִּבּוֹר) — "Mighty God" — directly calls the child "God" (*El*)
3. **Avi-'Ad** (אֲבִי־עַד) — "Everlasting Father" — possessor of eternity
4. **Sar-Shalom** (שַׂר־שָׁלוֹם) — "Prince of Peace" — *shalom* meaning complete well-being

The four names are sometimes understood chiastically: the first and last (Wonderful Counselor, Prince of Peace) describe His work; the middle two (Mighty God, Everlasting Father) describe His nature.

### New Testament Fulfillment

Isaiah 9:6-7 is fulfilled in Jesus' birth (Matthew 1:22-23; Luke 1:31-33). The angel Gabriel's words to Mary echo the Isaiah naming formula. "The government upon His shoulder" finds its ultimate expression in Christ's ascension (Philippians 2:9-11).
