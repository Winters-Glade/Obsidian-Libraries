# 237 Christ Preeminent — Colossians 1:15-18

> **Series:** T2000 Topical Series | **Pastor:** Chuck Smith | **Video:** [#237](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8WbwKBLdjdE)

## Overview

Christ is the image of the invisible God, the firstborn over all creation. By Him all things were created — in heaven and on earth, visible and invisible. He is before all things, and in Him all things hold together. He is the head of the body, the [[church]], the beginning, the firstborn from the dead — that in all things He might have the preeminence. Paul presents the supreme Christology in Scripture, exalting Christ as Creator, Sustainer, and Head of the Church.

This passage is likely an early Christian hymn celebrating the supremacy of Christ. Paul wrote Colossians to combat a dangerous heresy (sometimes called the "Colossian heresy") that combined Jewish legalism, Greek philosophy, and angel worship — diminishing Christ's unique supremacy. Paul's response is to exalt Christ to the highest possible position. Christ is "the image of the invisible God" (eikōn tou theou) — not a lesser reflection but the perfect representation of God's essence. "Firstborn" (prōtotokos) speaks not of chronological creation but of supreme rank and sovereignty, as in Psalm 89:27 where God says of David's line "I will make him my firstborn, higher than the kings of the earth." Christ is before all things, the Creator of all things, the Sustainer of all things, the Head of the [[church]], and the first to rise in resurrection glory — preeminent in every sphere.

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Christology | Christ is fully God and fully revealed God — the image of the invisible, the exact representation of the divine nature |
| Supremacy of Christ | Christ holds the highest rank in every sphere: over creation (Creator), over the cosmos (Sustainer), over the church (Head), over death (Firstborn from the dead) |
| Creation | All things — visible and invisible, physical and spiritual, earthly and heavenly — were created by, through, and for Christ |
| The Church | The [[church]] is Christ's body and He is its head — a relationship of vital union, authority, and life-giving connection |
| Preeminence of Christ | Paul's central goal: "that in all things He might have the preeminence" — Christ is not one option among many but the supreme Lord over everything |

## Background & Context

Colossae was a small city in Phrygia (modern Turkey), overshadowed by its neighbors Laodicea and Hierapolis. The Colossian [[church]] was founded not by Paul himself but by Epaphras, a convert from Paul's ministry in Ephesus (Colossians 1:7). The heresy threatening the [[church]] involved speculative philosophy, Jewish observances, and the worship of angels (Colossians 2:8, 16-23). Paul never names his opponents but counters their teaching by elevating Christ above every competing power. This passage (1:15-20) is the highest Christology in the New Testament, parallel to John 1:1-18 and Hebrews 1:1-4. The mention of "thrones, dominions, principalities, powers" in verse 16 directly counters the angel worship of the heretics — Christ created the very angels the false teachers were venerating. The cross is the place of cosmic reconciliation (1:20), a theme Paul develops in the next teaching (Note 238).
