## Summary

Proverbs 30:24-28 highlights four small but wise creatures: ants ([[_Concepts/Doctrines/preparation]]), coneys (security), locusts ([[unity]]), and spiders (perseverance).

## 📜 Historical Background

Proverbs 30 is attributed to Agur, son of Jakeh. The four creatures demonstrate [[wisdom]] despite their size: ants prepare for the future, coneys recognize their vulnerability and dwell in the rocks, locusts advance in unity, and spiders persevere.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

Jesus uses nature to teach: 'Consider the lilies' ([[Matthew 6_28.md]]). Paul uses the body with many members to teach unity ([[C2000/Transcripts/1_Corinthians/1 Corinthians 12.md]]).

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Four lessons: Ant — prepare today for tomorrow; Coney — know your weakness and find safety in the Rock; Locust — work together; Spider — persist until you reach your goal.

## 📚 Hebrew Roots & Wisdom Context

Proverbs 30 is attributed to **Agur son of Jakeh** (אָגוּר בִּן־יָקֶה), a [[wisdom]] writer otherwise unknown. His style uses **numerical sayings** ("three things... four things"), a pattern found in later Jewish [[wisdom]] literature. The ant (*nemalah*, נְמָלָה), coney (*shafan*, שָׁפָן — the rock hyrax), locust (*arbeh*, אַרְבֶּה), and spider (or lizard, *semamit*) each teach a spiritual lesson. This reflects the [[wisdom]] tradition's conviction that God's truth can be read in creation as well as Scripture.

## 📖 Research Context

### Numerical Proverbs — The Pattern of Four

Proverbs 30:24-28 is a **numerical proverb** — a wisdom form that lists items in a numbered sequence (see also [[Proverbs 6_16-19.md]]; 30:15-16, 18-19, 21-23, 29-31). These sayings use a "three... four" pattern suggesting completeness.

This section is attributed to **Agur son of Jakeh** (Proverbs 30:1) — a non-Israelite sage whose inclusion demonstrates that divine [[wisdom]] is not limited to Israel.

### Qal va-Homer Reasoning

The proverb uses **qal va-homer** (קַל וָחֹמֶר) — arguing from the lesser to the greater. If small creatures achieve so much through [[wisdom]], how much more should humans learn:

| Creature | Weakness | Strategy | Lesson |
|----------|----------|----------|--------|
| **Ant** | Small, weak | Stores food in summer | Preparation |
| **Coney** (hyrax) | Vulnerable | Dwells in the rocks | Security |
| **Locust** | Individually fragile | Advances in swarms | Unity |
| **Spider/Lizard** | Easily caught | Enters palaces | Persistence |
