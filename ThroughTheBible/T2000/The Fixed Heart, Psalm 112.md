## Summary

Psalm 112 describes the blessings of the man who fears the Lord: his heart is 'fixed, [[trust|trusting]] in the Lord.' This stability comes from a life rooted in [[Righteousness]], generosity, and the [[fear of the Lord]] — producing confidence even in adversity.

## 📜 Historical Background

Psalm 112 is an acrostic wisdom psalm that forms a companion to Psalm 111. The 'fixed heart' (verses 7-8) is the centerpiece — a heart that is established, steadfast, and unafraid of evil tidings. The righteous man is generous, just, and compassionate.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

Jesus speaks of the wise man who built his house upon the rock ([[Matthew 7_24-25.md]]). Paul writes of being 'rooted and built up in Him' ([[Colossians 2_7.md]]). The fixed heart — established, stable, [[trust|trusting]] in the [[Messiah]] — is the mark of mature [[Faith]].

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith taught that a 'fixed heart' is the secret of peace in a chaotic world. Evil tidings are inevitable, but the person whose heart is fixed on God is not shaken. That stability comes from the [[fear of the Lord]].

## 📚 Hebrew Poetry & Wisdom Context

Psalm 112 is an **acrostic wisdom psalm** companion to Psalm 111 — both have 22 lines corresponding to the Hebrew alphabet. The phrase "fixed heart" uses the Hebrew *kun* (כּוּן), meaning "established, prepared, made firm" — the same root used of God establishing David's throne (2 Samuel 7:12-13). This psalm is the wisdom ideal: the person whose life is **integrated** (heart fixed), **generous** (gives to the poor), and **steadfast** (unafraid of evil tidings).

## 📖 Research Context

### Acrostic Wisdom Psalm

Psalm 112 is an **alphabetic acrostic** belonging to the **Wisdom Psalm** genre. It forms a deliberate pair with Psalm 111 (also an acrostic): Psalm 111 celebrates God's character; Psalm 112 describes the one who fears God. Together they teach that human righteousness is a response to divine character.

### Key Hebrew Terms

- **"Blessed"** (*ashrei* אַשְׁרֵי, v. 1) — The happiness of living under God's blessing; the beatitude form links this psalm to Psalm 1 and Jesus' Beatitudes
- **"Fixed"** (*kun* כּוּן, vv. 7-8) — Established, firm, stable; "His heart is fixed" through [[trust]] in the Lord
- **"Fear the Lord"** (*yare YHWH*) — Not terror but reverent awe, the foundation of wisdom (Proverbs 1:7)

### The Righteous Man in Wisdom Tradition

The portrait of the righteous man: he fears God and delights in His commandments (v. 1), his household is blessed (v. 2), he is gracious and just (v. 4), he deals generously (v. 5), and he is unafraid of bad news because his heart is fixed on God (vv. 7-8). This portrait foreshadows the New Testament's teaching that hearts are established by grace (Hebrews 13:9).
