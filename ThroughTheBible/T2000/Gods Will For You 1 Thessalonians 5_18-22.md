# 240 God's Will For You — 1 Thessalonians 5:18-22

> **Series:** T2000 Topical Series | **Pastor:** Chuck Smith | **Video:** [#240](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PRRok7czbYM)

## Overview

"In every thing give thanks: for this is the will of God in Christ Jesus concerning you. Quench not the Spirit. Despise not prophesyings. Prove all things; hold fast that which is good. Abstain from all appearance of evil." Paul gives practical, straightforward instructions for Christian living — a life of gratitude, openness to the Spirit, discernment, and holiness.

These verses are Paul's final exhortations closing his first letter to the Thessalonians. They are remarkably practical — a series of short, punchy commands that cover the essential postures of the Christian life. The centerpiece is "this is the will of God" — Paul answers the question "How do I know God's will?" with specific, daily actions rather than hidden mysteries. "In everything give thanks" is not a command to feel grateful for suffering but to find reasons for gratitude within every circumstance. "Quench not the Spirit" (mē to pneuma sbennute) uses the image of fire — the [[Holy Spirit]]'s work in the [[church]] (through prophecy, teaching, conviction, and [[Spiritual Gifts]]) should be fanned into flame, not extinguished. "Prove all things" (panta dokimazete) calls believers to test everything — like a metallurgist testing metals for purity — and hold fast only what is good.

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Will of God | God's will is not hidden or mysterious — it includes concrete daily postures: rejoicing, praying, giving thanks, and abstaining from evil |
| Thanksgiving | Gratitude is not optional but commanded — not for everything but in everything, recognizing God's sovereignty over every circumstance |
| Sanctification | Holiness is both negative (abstaining from evil) and positive (holding fast to good) — the Spirit empowers both |
| Discernment | Believers are called to test everything — not naive gullibility but active evaluation of teaching, prophecy, and practice |
| Holy Spirit | The Spirit can be quenched by neglect, resistance, or unbelief — believers must cultivate and not suppress His work |

## Background & Context

First Thessalonians closes with an extended series of exhortations (5:12-28) that cover the [[church]]'s relationships, ministries, and personal conduct. The letter's eschatological focus (chapters 4-5) — the return of Christ and the day of the Lord — leads naturally to practical application. If Christ could return at any moment, how should believers live? Paul's answer: rejoicing, praying, thankful, Spirit-sensitive, discerning, and holy. The command to "despise not prophesyings" (propheteias mē exoutheneite) suggests that the Thessalonian [[church]] struggled with how to evaluate prophetic utterances in their gatherings. Paul's solution is not to suppress them but to test them. The letter ends with Paul's characteristic closing: "The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you" (1 Thessalonians 5:28).
