# 194 Remission of Sins — Acts 10:42-44

> **Series:** T2000 Topical Series | **Pastor:** Chuck Smith | **Video:** [#194](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0uBIQNBamxk)

## Overview

The watershed moment of Acts — the first Gentile converts. Peter preaches to Cornelius' household in Caesarea, and while he is still speaking, the Holy Spirit falls on them just as on the apostles at Pentecost. This event settles the question of whether Gentiles must become Jews before becoming Christians, leading to the Jerusalem Council in Acts 15.

## 📜 Historical Background

The conversion of Cornelius marks the most pivotal transition in Acts — the first unequivocal inclusion of uncircumcised Gentiles into the Christian fellowship. Cornelius was a centurion of the Italian Cohort stationed in Caesarea Maritima, Rome's provincial capital of Judea. As a 'devout God-fearer' who prayed regularly and gave alms, he represented the many Gentiles attracted to Jewish monotheism and ethics but unwilling to fully convert. Caesarea was a thoroughly Roman city, built by Herod the Great with a massive artificial harbor, a hippodrome, and a temple to Caesar. Peter's vision of the sheet with unclean animals (Acts 10:9-16) directly challenged the kosher laws that had defined Jewish identity for centuries. The Holy Spirit's falling on the Gentiles 'while Peter was still speaking' (Acts 10:44) left no doubt that God was doing a new thing — the Gentile Pentecost had arrived.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

This episode is the turning point Paul later describes in Ephesians 2:11-22 — Christ is our peace who has broken down the dividing wall of hostility between Jew and Gentile. Peter's declaration that 'God shows no partiality' (Acts 10:34) echoes Deuteronomy 10:17 and becomes a cornerstone of New Testament ecclesiology. The Jerusalem Council in Acts 15 settles the doctrinal question this event raises: Gentiles are saved through grace alone, not by becoming Jews. The language of 'remission of sins' (aphesis hamartion) ties directly to Jesus' promise in Luke 24:47 — 'repentance and remission of sins should be preached in His name among all nations.' Galatians 3:28 declares the unity of all believers in Christ, which this passage inaugurates.

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith saw Cornelius' conversion as proof that God is actively working in every culture and people group, preparing hearts long before the missionary arrives. Cornelius' prayers and alms 'came up for a memorial before God' (Acts 10:4) — a reminder that God honors sincere seekers. Smith often preached that the Holy Spirit's falling on Gentiles before their water baptism demonstrates that the Spirit is sovereign and cannot be contained by human religious systems. The lesson for believers is that God's salvation plan is bigger than any one culture, denomination, or tradition — the remission of sins through faith in Christ is offered freely to all who believe, regardless of background.
