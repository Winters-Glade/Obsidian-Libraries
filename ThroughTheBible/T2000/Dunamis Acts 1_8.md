# 190 Dunamis — Acts 1:8

> **Series:** T2000 Topical Series | **Pastor:** Chuck Smith | **Video:** [#190](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FxHCxOJYdjQ)

## Overview

Jesus' final words before His ascension. Acts 1:8 is the structural outline for the entire book: the gospel spreads concentrically from Jerusalem (Acts 1-7), to Judea and Samaria (Acts 8-12), and to the ends of the earth (Acts 13-28). The Greek word 'dunamis' means inherent power, ability, or capacity — the same root as 'dynamite.'

## 📜 Historical Background

Jesus' final commission in Acts 1:8 was given on the Mount of Olives (Acts 1:12), just east of Jerusalem, forty days after His resurrection. The disciples were gathered during the lead-up to the Jewish feast of Pentecost (Shavuot), one of three pilgrimage festivals when Jerusalem's population swelled. The promise of the Holy Spirit's power ('dunamis') came against the backdrop of Roman occupation — a world where power meant legions, governors, and the imperial cult. Jesus redefined power not as political force but as spiritual enablement for worldwide witness. The geographical markers in this verse — Jerusalem, Judea, Samaria, and the ends of the earth — form the structural outline for the entire book of Acts and trace the gospel's expansion from a Jewish sect to a global movement.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

The 'dunamis' promised here is the same power at work throughout the New Testament: the power that raised Christ from the dead (Romans 1:4, Ephesians 1:19-20), the power Paul prayed believers would know (Ephesians 3:16-20), and the power that transforms sinners into saints (2 Corinthians 3:18). Luke 24:49 records Jesus' parallel instruction to 'wait for the promise of the Father,' fulfilled dramatically at Pentecost in Acts 2. The concentric-circle pattern of Acts 1:8 also mirrors the Great Commission in Matthew 28:18-20, where Jesus' authority and presence accompany the disciple-making mission to all nations.

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith emphasized that the 'dunamis' of Acts 1:8 is not reserved for a special class of believers but is the birthright of every follower of Christ. The same Holy Spirit who empowered a handful of fearful disciples to turn the Roman Empire upside down is available today. Smith often said that the church does not need more programs or strategies — it needs the power of the Holy Spirit. The geographical progression reminds us that witness begins exactly where we are (Jerusalem — our home and community) and extends outward as God opens doors. Power is not given for personal display but for testimony.
