## Summary

'For as the rain cometh down, and the snow from heaven, and returneth not thither, but watereth the earth... so shall my word be that goeth forth out of my mouth: it shall not return unto me void.' Pastor Chuck explores the power and reliability of God's Word — it always accomplishes its purpose. This [[Prophecy]] of [[Restoration]] affirms that God's word of promise will never fail.

## 📜 Historical Background

[[Isaiah 55.md]] is one of the most beautiful invitations in the Old Testament — a free offer of salvation to all who thirst. The chapter opens with 'Ho, every one that thirsteth, come ye to the waters.' The rain-and-snow analogy draws from the agricultural cycle of ancient Israel. Rain falls from heaven, waters the earth, produces seed and bread, and then returns — it doesn't come back empty. God's Word operates on the same principle: it goes out with divine purpose and unfailingly accomplishes what God intends. This passage comes near the end of the 'Book of Comfort' (chapters 40-55) and anticipates the new covenant. The [[Prophecy|prophetic]] voices of [[Jeremiah]], [[Ezekiel]], and [[Daniel]] similarly proclaimed the enduring power of God's word through seasons of [[Judgment]] and [[Restoration]].

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

Jesus identifies Himself as the Word made flesh in [[John 1_1-14]]. The parable of the sower ([[Matthew 13_1-23.md]]) illustrates how God's Word, like seed, produces fruit when it falls on good ground. [[Hebrews 4_12.md]] declares that 'the word of God is quick, and powerful.' Paul affirms in [[Romans 10_14-17.md]] that faith comes by hearing the Word. The Great Commission ([[Matthew 28_18-20.md]]) sends disciples out with the assurance that Christ's word will not return void — it will produce fruit to the ends of the earth.

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith often said that we don't need to defend the Word — we just need to proclaim it. Like rain, it does its own work. Our job is faithfulness in sowing; the harvest belongs to God. When we share God's Word, we can trust that it will accomplish exactly what He intends, even if we don't see immediate results. The same Word that created the universe ([[_Bible/Genesis/Genesis 1.md]]) is the Word that transforms hearts today.
