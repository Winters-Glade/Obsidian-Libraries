## Summary

Isaiah contrasts the idols of Babylon — which must be carried by their worshippers — with the God of Israel, who carries His people. 'I have made, and I will bear; even I will carry, and will deliver you.' Pastor Chuck exposes the foolishness of [[idolatry]] and the comfort of a God who supports us rather than needing our support. This [[Prophecy]] pronounces [[Judgment]] on Babylon's false gods while promising [[Restoration]] to God's people.

## 📜 Historical Background

[[Isaiah 46.md]] is a devastating satire against Babylonian [[idolatry]]. The gods Bel (Marduk) and Nebo were the chief deities of Babylon — their statues were carried in religious processions. Isaiah points out the absurdity: these gods have to be carried by animals and carts; they bow down and cannot save themselves. In contrast, Yahweh carries His people from birth to old age. The contrast could not be starker: idols must be made, fed, carried, and protected by people; the living God makes, feeds, carries, and protects His people. This passage was written before Babylon's rise to power, demonstrating God's sovereign foreknowledge — a [[Prophecy|prophetic]] theme also developed by [[Jeremiah]], [[Ezekiel]], and [[Daniel]] in their own confrontations with Babylonian idolatry.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

Jesus extends this imagery in [[_Bible/Matthew/Matthew 11_28-30.md]], inviting those who are 'heavy laden' to come to Him. The early church rejected [[idolatry]] as incompatible with faith in Christ ([[1 Thessalonians 1_9.md]], [[1 John]]). Paul describes the living God in [[Acts 17_24-25.md]] as one who 'is not worshipped with men's hands, as though He needed any thing, seeing He giveth to all life, and breath, and all things' — directly echoing Isaiah's contrast.

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith noted that we tend to create 'idols' in our own image — things we think we need to support or maintain for God. But the gospel is the opposite: God supports us. When we try to prop up our religion, our reputation, or our righteousness, we're acting like we have to carry a dead idol. True faith rests in a God who carries us from the womb to old age and beyond.
