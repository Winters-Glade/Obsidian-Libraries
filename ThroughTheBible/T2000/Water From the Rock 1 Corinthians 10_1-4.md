# 218 Water From the Rock — 1 Corinthians 10:1-4

> **Series:** T2000 Topical Series | **Pastor:** Chuck Smith | **Video:** [#218](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XfOFcxCk_28)

## Overview

Paul draws a typological lesson from Israel's wilderness journey. The fathers were under the cloud, passed through the sea, and drank from the same spiritual Rock that followed them — and that Rock was Christ. Their experience serves as a warning and example for believers. Paul reads the Exodus narrative Christologically, seeing Christ as the pre-existent agent of Israel's deliverance and provision. The typology is both encouraging (Christ provides for His people) and warning (Israel's failures are examples for us).

## Pauline Context

1 Corinthians 10:1-13 is Paul's most extended typological reading of the Exodus, drawing lessons for the Corinthian church facing temptation and idolatry. The key phrase "that Rock was Christ" is Paul's most explicit identification of Christ with Yahweh's presence in the wilderness. The Jewish tradition of the "following rock" (a mobile source of water that accompanied Israel) is used by Paul to demonstrate Christ's continuous presence with His people. The catalog of Israel's sins — idolatry, sexual immorality, testing the Lord, grumbling — corresponds to the temptations facing the Corinthians. Paul connects this typology to the Eucharist ("spiritual food" and "spiritual drink"), showing that participating in Christ does not guarantee faithfulness.
