# 231 His Workmanship — Ephesians 2:8-10

> **Series:** T2000 Topical Series | **Pastor:** Chuck Smith | **Video:** [#231](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0r51IO8Qz4Y)

## Overview

"For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God: not of works, lest any man should boast. For we are his workmanship, created in Christ Jesus unto good works." Paul presents the clearest summary of salvation in all of Scripture — saved by grace through faith, not by works, yet created for good works. Believers are God's workmanship (poiema — His masterpiece), recreated in Christ for a life of purpose.

Ephesians 2:8-10 is the theological hinge of the letter. Paul has just described humanity's desperate state — dead in trespasses and sins, following the course of this world, by nature children of wrath (2:1-3). Then comes the great "But God" (2:4) — the turning point of the gospel. Salvation is entirely God's initiative from start to finish. The Greek word poiema ("workmanship") is the same root as our English "poem" — believers are God's own work of art, created in Christ for a life of good works that God prepared beforehand. This completely reframes the role of good works: they are not the root of salvation but the fruit.

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Grace | Undeserved favor — God's Riches At Christ's Expense — the sole basis of salvation from sin's death and dominion |
| Salvation | Complete deliverance from sin's penalty ([[justification]]), power ([[Sanctification]]), and eventually presence (glorification) |
| Faith | Not a meritorious work but the empty hand that receives the gift; faith itself is God's gift, excluding all boasting |
| Good Works | Not the means of salvation but its necessary evidence — prepared beforehand by God as the believer's walk of purpose |
| God's Workmanship | Every believer is a unique masterpiece (poiema) of God's creative grace, recreated in Christ for eternal purpose |

## Background & Context

Ephesians was written from Roman imprisonment (c. AD 60-62), likely sent with Tychicus to the churches of Asia Minor. The letter moves from the highest theology (chapters 1-3: election, redemption, the mystery of the [[church]]) to the most practical application (chapters 4-6: walk, relationships, spiritual warfare). Verse 8-10 sits at the transition — the summary of all Paul has taught about salvation and the foundation for all he will command. The phrase "not of yourselves" (ouk ex hymōn) is emphatic in the Greek — salvation in no way originates from within the human will or effort. The Reformation rallying cry of sola gratia (grace alone) and sola fide (faith alone) finds its definitive scriptural foundation here.
