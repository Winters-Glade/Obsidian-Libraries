## Summary

Proverbs 1 introduces the book's purpose: to impart [[wisdom]], instruction, and [[_Concepts/Doctrines/understanding]]. 'The [[fear of the Lord]] is the beginning of knowledge' establishes the foundation for all true [[wisdom]]. This foundational principle undergirds all [[Righteousness|righteous living]].

## 📜 Historical Background

Proverbs 1 serves as the introduction to the entire book. Solomon states the purpose: to know [[wisdom]] and receive instruction. [[Wisdom]] is personified as a woman calling out in the streets. The [[fear of the Lord]] is presented as reverent awe and humble dependence on God.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

James writes 'if any of you lack [[wisdom]], let him ask of God' (James 1:5). Paul speaks of Christ as 'the [[wisdom]] of God' (1 Corinthians 1:24). Jesus is [[Wisdom]] incarnate.

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith taught that the fear of the Lord is not cowering terror but reverent awe. It is the foundation upon which all true [[wisdom]] is built. Without it, knowledge becomes pride.

## 📚 Hebrew Roots & Wisdom Context

Proverbs 1 introduces **chokmah** (חָכְמָה), the Hebrew word for "[[wisdom]]" — meaning skill in living (the same root as the craftsman Bezalel in Exodus 31:3). The "fear of the Lord" (*yirat YHWH*, יִרְאַת יְהוָה) is reverent awe and humble dependence — the foundation of all [[wisdom]]. The father-to-son lectures in chapters 1-9 establish the **family setting** of [[wisdom]] transmission. [[Wisdom]] is personified as a woman calling in the streets, reaching its climax in Proverbs 8 where [[Wisdom]] is present at creation itself.

## 📖 Research Context

### The Purpose of Proverbs — Hebrew Mashal

The Hebrew **mashal** (מָשָׁל) — "proverb, saying, parable" — is broader than the English "proverb." A *mashal* is a comparison that illuminates truth by bringing two things together. The root means "to be like, to compare." Proverbs are packed comparisons that invite meditation.

The opening (1:1-7) serves as the **superscription** for the entire book:
1. **Purpose** — To know [[wisdom]] (*chokmah*), instruction (*musar*), [[_Concepts/Doctrines/understanding]] (*binah*)
2. **Method** — Through proverbs, parables, riddles (*chidah*)
3. **Foundation** — "The fear of the Lord is the beginning of knowledge"
4. **Negation** — "Fools despise wisdom and instruction"

### The Fear of the Lord — Yir'at YHWH

This is the **motto of the entire Wisdom Literature**. *Yir'ah* (יִרְאָה) encompasses reverence, awe, and humble dependence. The "beginning" (*reshit* רֵאשִׁית) is not just the first step but the foundation principle of all true knowledge. The New Testament does not abandon this: "Work out your own salvation with fear and trembling" (Philippians 2:12).

### Wisdom Personified

Proverbs 1 introduces Wisdom as a woman calling in the streets (vv. 20-33). This personification reaches its fullest development in Proverbs 8-9 and is a forerunner of the Logos theology of John 1 and Paul's identification of Christ as "the wisdom of God" (1 Corinthians 1:24).
