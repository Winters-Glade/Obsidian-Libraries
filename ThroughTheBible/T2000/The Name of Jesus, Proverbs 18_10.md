## Summary

Proverbs 18:10 declares: 'The name of the Lord is a strong tower: the righteous runneth into it, and is safe.' The name of the Lord is our refuge in times of trouble. This proverb points to the [[Messiah|Messianic]] fulfillment in Jesus, whose name is above every name.

## 📜 Historical Background

In the ancient Near East, a strong tower was a defensive structure within city walls. The 'name of the Lord' represents His character, His reputation — all that He has revealed Himself to be.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

Peter declares 'there is none other name under heaven... whereby we must be saved' (Acts 4:12). Paul writes that God has given Jesus a name 'above every name' (Philippians 2:9-10).

## 💡 Pastoral Application

When the enemy attacks, we don't need to defend ourselves — we run into the strong tower of His name. The name of Jesus is still a strong tower for all who [[trust]] in Him.

## 📚 Hebrew Roots & Wisdom Context

In Hebrew thought, a **name** (*shem*, שֵׁם) reveals character and identity. The "name of the Lord" represents all that God has revealed about Himself. The "strong tower" (*migdal oz*, מִגְדַּל־עֹז) was a familiar image in ancient Israel — a fortified tower within city walls (Judges 9:51; 2 Chronicles 26:9-10). This proverb uses **synthetic parallelism** where the second line advances the thought. The Hebrew *shagav* (שָׂגַב, "safe") means placed beyond reach, inaccessible to harm.

## 📖 Research Context

### Strong Tower — Migdal Oz

Proverbs 18:10 uses the Hebrew **migdal oz** (מִגְדַּל־עֹז) — literally "tower of strength." In ancient Israel, towers (*migdalim*) were fortified structures within city walls where defenders took refuge during attack. The image is of an impregnable high place offering a commanding view.

In the Psalms, God is repeatedly called a "strong tower" and "refuge" (Psalm 9:9; 18:2; 46:1, 7, 11; 59:9, 16-17; 61:3; 62:2-3, 7; 94:22; 144:2).

### The Name of the Lord — Shem YHWH

In Hebrew thought, a **name** (*shem* שֵׁם) is not a label but an expression of character. The "name of the Lord" embodies His revealed attributes — power, faithfulness, mercy, covenant-keeping. To call on His name (Genesis 12:8; Psalm 116:4, 13) is to invoke His character and [[trust]] His revelation.

### New Testament Application

The New Testament expands this: "God also hath highly exalted Him, and given Him a name which is above every name" (Philippians 2:9-10). Jesus is the name in whom God's full character is revealed — the strong tower for all who run to Him.
