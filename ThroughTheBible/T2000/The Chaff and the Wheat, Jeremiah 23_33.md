## Summary

[[Jeremiah 23_33.md]] poses a stark contrast: 'What is the chaff to the wheat?' God declares through Jeremiah that false prophets who speak their own words rather than His are like worthless chaff that the wind drives away. Pastor Chuck explores the difference between authentic divine revelation and human invention, and why God's Word carries eternal weight while man's words are empty.

## 📜 Historical Background

[[Jeremiah 23.md]] contains a scathing indictment of the prophets and priests who were leading God's people astray. In Jeremiah's day, false prophets were popular because they spoke messages the people wanted to hear — peace, prosperity, and safety — while Jeremiah's warnings of [[Judgment]] were unwelcome. The contrast between chaff (the worthless husk) and wheat (the valuable grain) was a familiar agricultural image. At harvest time, grain was threshed and winnowed; the heavy wheat fell to the ground while the light chaff was blown away by the wind. The false prophets' words, however popular, were ultimately weightless.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

John the Baptist uses the same imagery in [[Matthew 3_12.md]], declaring that Christ will 'thoroughly purge His floor, and gather His wheat into the garner; but He will burn up the chaff with unquenchable fire.' Jesus warns in [[Matthew 7_15-20.md]] about false prophets who come in sheep's clothing but inwardly are ravenous wolves — you will know them by their fruits. In [[Galatians 1_8-9.md]], Paul pronounces a curse on anyone who preaches a different gospel. The warning is timeless: not everyone who speaks in God's name speaks from God.

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith emphasized the critical importance of knowing God's Word so intimately that we can distinguish the true from the counterfeit. Just as a banker studies real currency to recognize counterfeits, believers must be saturated in Scripture to recognize false teaching. The popular message is not always the true message. Jeremiah's unpopular warnings were from God; the popular words of peace from the false prophets were empty chaff. We must measure every teaching against the revealed Word of God.
