# 215 The Temple of God — 1 Corinthians 3:16-17

> **Series:** T2000 Topical Series | **Pastor:** Chuck Smith | **Video:** [#215](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=83aCSruCCo4)

## Overview

Believers collectively are the temple of God, and the Spirit of God dwells in them. Paul warns that if any man defiles the temple of God, God will destroy him. The church corporately is the dwelling place of the Holy Spirit, called to holiness and set apart for God's presence. This passage comes in the context of Paul's rebuke of divisions in the Corinthian church — the church that boasts in human leaders (Apollos, Paul, Cephas) is actually the temple of God, and factionalism defiles that temple. The indwelling Spirit is not a possession of individual believers only but the mark of the corporate body as God's dwelling place.

## Pauline Context

The temple metaphor is one of Paul's most powerful descriptions of the church. In 1 Corinthians 3, Paul warns those who would destroy the church through factionalism that they are defiling God's temple. The image appears again in 2 Corinthians 6:16, where Paul cites Leviticus 26:12 and Ezekiel 37:27 — "I will dwell in them and walk among them." In Ephesians 2:21-22, Paul develops the temple metaphor further, describing the church as a building growing into a holy temple in the Lord, with Christ as the cornerstone and the apostles and prophets as the foundation. The temple imagery connects Paul's ecclesiology (doctrine of the church) to his pneumatology (doctrine of the Spirit) — the Spirit's indwelling presence is what makes the church holy and distinct.
