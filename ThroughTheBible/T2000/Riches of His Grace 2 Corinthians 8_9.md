# 225 Riches of His Grace — 2 Corinthians 8:9

> **Series:** T2000 Topical Series | **Pastor:** Chuck Smith | **Video:** [#225](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4Dfu6HBhNcI)

## Overview

Paul holds up the grace of the Lord Jesus Christ as the supreme example of generosity: though He was rich, yet for our sakes He became poor, that we through His poverty might be rich. This becomes the foundation for Paul's appeal to the Corinthians to give generously to the saints in need. The verse encapsulates the entire gospel in one sentence — the incarnation, atonement, and exaltation of Christ are compressed into a pattern of self-emptying love that becomes the model for Christian giving.

| Theme | Description |

## Pauline Context

2 Corinthians 8-9 is Paul's most extended teaching on Christian giving. He introduces the Macedonians as an example: though in deep poverty, they gave generously and joyfully because they first gave themselves to the Lord. The theological center of Paul's appeal is 8:9 — the grace of Christ who became poor to make us rich. This verse is a summary of Paul's Christology, echoing the self-emptying described in Philippians 2:5-11. The "poverty" of Christ refers to the incarnation, and the "riches" we receive include [[justification]], adoption, and eternal glory. Paul's argument is that generous giving is not a legal obligation but a grace-response — it flows from the recognition that we have received everything from Christ.
