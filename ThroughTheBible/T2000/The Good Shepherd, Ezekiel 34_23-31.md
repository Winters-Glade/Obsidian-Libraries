## Summary

Ezekiel 34:23-31 is a messianic promise that God Himself will raise up a Shepherd for His scattered flock — 'My servant David' — who will feed them and be their shepherd. Pastor Chuck explores this as a clear [[Prophecy]] of Jesus Christ, the Good Shepherd, who gathers, feeds, and protects God's people under a covenant of peace.

## 📜 Historical Background

Ezekiel 34 is a powerful contrast between Israel's failed shepherds (the religious and political leaders who exploited the flock) and God's promise to shepherd His people Himself. The chapter begins with a scathing indictment of the shepherds of Israel who 'feed themselves' but do not feed the flock (vv. 1-10). God declares He will remove them and become the Shepherd. Then in verse 23, a more specific promise emerges: 'And I will set up one shepherd over them, and he shall feed them, even my servant David.' Since David had been dead for centuries, this is clearly a [[Messiah|Messianic]] prophecy pointing to the greater Son of David — Jesus Christ. The title 'My servant David' echoes the [[Servant of the Lord]] theme found throughout the prophets.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

Jesus explicitly identifies Himself as the Good Shepherd in [[John 10_11-16.md]], declaring that He lays down His life for the sheep. He is the fulfillment of Ezekiel's 'my servant David.' The covenant of peace (Ezekiel 34:25) is realized in the New Covenant sealed by Christ's blood ([[Luke 22_20]]). [[Hebrews 13_20.md]] calls Jesus 'that great shepherd of the sheep' brought again from the dead. [[1 Peter 5_4]] refers to Him as the 'chief Shepherd' who will appear in glory.

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith often emphasized that the Good Shepherd is not just a title but a relationship. Unlike the hireling who flees when danger comes, Jesus knows His sheep by name and lays down His life for them. The 'covenant of peace' means that those who are under the Shepherd's care have nothing to fear — He is able to protect, provide, and lead His flock safely home.
