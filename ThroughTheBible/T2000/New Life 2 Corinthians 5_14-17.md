# 224 New Life — 2 Corinthians 5:14-17

> **Series:** T2000 Topical Series | **Pastor:** Chuck Smith | **Video:** [#224](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Usa69zRk-Eg)

## Overview

The love of Christ constrains us to live no longer for ourselves but for Him who died and rose again. In Christ, we are a [[New Creation]] — old things have passed away and all things have become new. This new identity transforms every aspect of life, including our relationships and priorities. Paul's declaration of [[New Creation]] is one of the most powerful statements of Christian identity in all of Scripture. The [[New Creation]] is not merely a renovated version of the old but an entirely new reality — the old order (death under the Law, life lived for self) has been superseded by the new (life in Christ, life lived for Him).

| Theme | Description |

## Pauline Context

2 Corinthians 5:14-21 is the heart of Paul's theology of reconciliation and [[New Creation]]. The phrase "in Christ" (en Christō) is Paul's most characteristic expression for union with Christ, appearing over 160 times in his letters. The "love of Christ constrains us" (syn-echei hēmas) suggests both compulsion and containment — Christ's love both drives and directs the believer's life. Paul's logic is striking: Christ died for all, therefore all died — the believer's old self died with Christ, and the life the believer now lives is lived for Christ. The ministry of reconciliation given to believers (v. 18-20) extends the apostolic mission to the [[church]], making every believer an ambassador of the [[New Creation]]. This passage connects to Paul's Adam-Christ typology in Romans 5:12-21 and 1 Corinthians 15:45-49.
