# 232 Comprehending the Uncomprehending — Ephesians 3:14-20

> **Series:** T2000 Topical Series | **Pastor:** Chuck Smith | **Video:** [#232](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M9Q8iuYG6hU)

## Overview

Paul prays that believers would be strengthened with power through the Spirit in the inner man, that Christ may dwell in their hearts by faith, and that they would be rooted and grounded in love — able to comprehend the breadth, length, depth, and height of the love of Christ which surpasses knowledge. This is Paul's great doxological prayer, celebrating a love so vast it can be experienced but never fully grasped.

This is one of the most remarkable prayers in all of Scripture. Paul prays not for physical needs or circumstantial changes but for spiritual perception — that believers would have the inward strength to grasp the infinite dimensions of Christ's love. The paradox is intentional: to "comprehend the uncomprehending" — to know a love that passes knowledge. Paul piles phrase upon phrase: "strengthened with might," "Christ dwelling in your hearts," "rooted and grounded in love," "able to comprehend," "to know the unknowable," "filled with all the fullness of God." Each phrase builds on the last, culminating in a doxology to the God "who is able to do exceeding abundantly above all that we ask or think."

## Key Themes

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Love of God | Christ's love has four dimensions (breadth, length, depth, height) — vast enough to encompass all people yet intimate enough to fill the individual heart |
| Prayer | Paul's model prayer prioritizes spiritual depth over material need — asking for inner strength, Christ's indwelling, and love's comprehension |
| Holy Spirit | The Spirit strengthens the "inner man" — the believer's spirit — enabling what human effort cannot achieve |
| Spiritual Strength | Not physical or emotional stamina but the power to grasp supernatural truth and live in the reality of God's love |
| Comprehension of Christ's Love | The goal of Christian experience — not mere intellectual knowledge but experiential, relational knowing that surpasses cognitive understanding |

## Background & Context

This prayer follows Paul's explanation of the "mystery of Christ" — that Gentiles are fellow heirs, members of the same body, and partakers of the promise in Christ (Ephesians 3:6). Paul describes himself as a prisoner of Christ Jesus for the sake of the Gentiles, entrusted with revealing this mystery. The prayer is thus deeply connected to Paul's apostolic mission: that those who were once far off might know the full dimensions of the love that brought them near. The doxology (3:20-21) is one of the richest in Scripture — God is able to do "hyper ek perissou" (exceedingly abundantly) above all we ask or think, literally "superabundantly beyond measure."
