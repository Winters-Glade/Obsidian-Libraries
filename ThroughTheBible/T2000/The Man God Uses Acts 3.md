# 191 The Man God Uses — Acts 3

> **Series:** T2000 Topical Series | **Pastor:** Chuck Smith | **Video:** [#191](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QDJaKwSVvho)

## Overview

The healing of the lame man at the Beautiful Gate of the temple serves as the occasion for Peter's second major sermon. This chapter marks the first apostolic miracle after Pentecost and demonstrates the power of Jesus' name. Peter uses the miracle as a platform to call Israel to repentance and announce the coming 'times of refreshing.'

## 📜 Historical Background

The healing at the Beautiful Gate (likely the Nicanor Gate, the Corinthian bronze entrance to the Court of Women) occurred at the ninth hour (3:00 PM), the Jewish time of afternoon prayer — one of three daily prayer times in Second Temple Judaism. The lame man, over forty years old (Acts 4:22), had been carried to this gate daily, begging from those entering the temple. In the first-century Jewish world, physical disability was often associated with sin (John 9:2), making the man's condition both physical and social. The temple itself was Herod's magnificent renovation, renowned throughout the Roman world for its size and splendor. Peter and John's boldness is remarkable given that just weeks earlier Peter had denied Jesus before a servant girl. The 'times of refreshing' Peter mentions (Acts 3:19) echoes the prophetic hope of Israel's restoration (Isaiah 44:3, Ezekiel 36:25-27).

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

The healing in Jesus' name ('In the name of Jesus Christ of Nazareth, rise up and walk!') demonstrates the apostolic authority Jesus delegated to His followers (Mark 16:17-18). This miracle fulfills Isaiah 35:6 — 'the lame shall leap like a deer' — showing that the messianic age prophesied in the Old Testament had arrived. Peter's sermon explicitly connects the miracle to faith in the name of Jesus (Acts 3:16), establishing the pattern that apostolic signs authenticate the gospel message (Hebrews 2:3-4). The call to repentance and the promise of 'times of refreshing' (Acts 3:19) echo into the New Covenant promise of the Holy Spirit as the down payment of our inheritance (Ephesians 1:13-14).

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith often noted that God uses imperfect people — Peter, who had denied Christ, became the instrument of this powerful healing. The man at the gate asked for money but received something far greater: healing and wholeness in Jesus' name. This illustrates how God's gifts often exceed our requests. Smith emphasized that the name of Jesus is not a magical formula but the authority of a living Person working through yielded vessels. The miracle was not about Peter's faith but about the faithfulness of Jesus, whose name carries the same power today.
