## Summary

'So shall they fear the name of the Lord from the west, and his glory from the rising of the sun. When the enemy shall come in like a flood, the Spirit of the Lord shall lift up a standard against him.' Pastor Chuck examines divine defense — God's promise to protect His people when the enemy overwhelms. This [[Prophecy]] weaves together themes of [[Judgment]], [[Restoration]], and the coming [[Messiah|Redeemer]].

## 📜 Historical Background

[[Isaiah 59.md]] is a chapter of stark contrast. It opens with a declaration that God's hand is not shortened that it cannot save — the problem is not God's power but the people's sin. The chapter catalogs Israel's transgressions: lying, violence, injustice. Yet in verses 16-21, the tone shifts dramatically. God Himself puts on righteousness as a breastplate and salvation as a helmet (passages Paul later adapts in [[Ephesians 6.md]]). The promise that 'the Spirit of the Lord shall lift up a standard' against the enemy pictures a banner raised in battle — a rallying point that signals God's intervention and ultimate victory. This [[Prophecy]] of [[Judgment]] and [[Restoration]] echoes the messages of [[Jeremiah]], [[Ezekiel]], and [[Daniel]], who also proclaimed God's ultimate triumph over evil.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

Paul directly borrows the armor imagery from [[Isaiah 59_17.md]] in [[Ephesians 6_13-17.md]], describing the 'whole armor of God.' The 'Redeemer shall come to Zion' ([[_Bible/Isaiah/Isaiah 59_20.md]]) is quoted by Paul in [[Romans 11_26-27.md]] in the context of Israel's future salvation. The promise that God's Spirit and words would remain with His people 'from henceforth and forever' points to the permanent indwelling of the Holy Spirit in the New Covenant ([[_Bible/John/John 14_16-17.md]]).

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith taught that when the enemy attacks like a flood — overwhelming and relentless — we cannot defend ourselves in our own strength. But the Spirit raises a standard. Our defense is not our own resolve but the presence of the Spirit and the promises of God. The battle belongs to the Lord. When we feel overwhelmed, we look for the standard He raises.
