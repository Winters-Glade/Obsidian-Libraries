# 195 Stick to the Lord — Acts 11:23

> **Series:** T2000 Topical Series | **Pastor:** Chuck Smith | **Video:** [#195](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tHWwHXGto8U)

## Overview

When news of Gentiles believing reaches Jerusalem, they send Barnabas to Antioch. Verse 23 describes his response: 'When he came and had seen the grace of God, he was glad, and encouraged them all that with purpose of heart they should continue with the Lord.' This marks the beginning of the Antioch church, which becomes the launching pad for Paul's missionary journeys.

## 📜 Historical Background

Antioch on the Orontes (modern Antakya, Turkey) was the third-largest city of the Roman Empire after Rome and Alexandria, with a population of approximately 300,000. It was a cosmopolitan center of Greek culture, Roman administration, and significant Jewish presence, located on the Orontes River about 15 miles inland from the Mediterranean coast. When the scattered believers from Stephen's persecution first preached to Greeks in Antioch (Acts 11:20), a multi-ethnic church was born that would become the launching pad for all of Paul's missionary journeys. Barnabas, sent from Jerusalem to investigate, was known as the 'Son of Encouragement' — his leadership style emphasized building up rather than controlling. It was in Antioch that believers were first called 'Christians' (Acts 11:26), likely a term coined by the Roman authorities or local population to identify this growing movement.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

Barnabas' exhortation to 'stick to the Lord with purpose of heart' echoes through the New Testament as a call to perseverance. Paul uses similar language in Colossians 1:21-23 — 'if indeed you continue in the faith, grounded and steadfast.' The Greek word prosmenein (to continue with, to stick to) suggests an active, purposeful perseverance. Antioch becomes the model of a multi-ethnic, missional church that the Pauline epistles later describe: a body where 'there is neither Jew nor Greek' (Galatians 3:28). This church's practice of sending out missionaries (Acts 13:1-3) establishes the pattern for the Great Commission's fulfillment through intentional, Spirit-led church planting.

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith frequently pointed to Barnabas as the model encourager — a man who saw grace at work and celebrated it. Smith taught that the greatest need in the church is not more critics but more encouragers who, like Barnabas, can 'see the grace of God' in others and call them to persevere. The phrase 'purpose of heart' (prothesis kardias) implies a fixed, deliberate, settled orientation of one's whole being toward God — not a casual commitment but a life-defining decision. Smith emphasized that perseverance is not passive endurance but active 'sticking' — clinging to Christ through every season, recognizing that the Christian life is a marathon requiring sustained purpose.
