## Summary

## 📜 Historical Background

The Hebrew phrase 'in the way he should go' literally means 'according to his way' — suggesting training tailored to the child's unique temperament. The promise is a general principle that consistently applied training bears fruit over a lifetime.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

Paul instructs fathers to 'bring them up in the nurture and admonition of the Lord' (Ephesians 6:4). Timothy knew the Scriptures 'from a child' (2 Timothy 3:15).

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Training is more than teaching — it's modeling, discipling, and investing time. The promise is not that children won't wander but that the training will draw them back.

## 📚 Hebrew Roots & Wisdom Context

The Hebrew verb "train up" (*chanak*, חָנַךְ) means "to dedicate" or "to initiate" — the same word used for the dedication of Solomon's Temple (1 Kings 8:63). The phrase "in the way he should go" reads "according to his way" (*al pi darko*, עַל־פִּי דַּרְכּוֹ) — suggesting training tailored to the child's unique temperament and gifts. This reflects the distinctive **family-based [[wisdom]] tradition** of ancient Israel, where parents were the primary educators.

## 📖 Research Context

### Hebrew Word Study — Hanak

The Hebrew *hanak* (חָנַךְ) — "train, dedicate, inaugurate" — appears only five times in the Old Testament. It means **inauguration** — setting a child's feet in the right direction. The related Arabic word *hanaka* means "to rub the palate of a newborn with chewed dates" — an ancient initiation practice.

The phrase "when he is old" — Hebrew *b'ziqno* (בְּזִקְנוֹ) — describes the power of formative training. The character formed in childhood typically endures through life. As a general [[wisdom]] principle (not an absolute promise), early training in the [[fear of the Lord]] is the most important investment a parent can make.

### Egyptian Connection — Proverbs 22:17–24:22

This section shows a remarkable literary relationship with the Egyptian **Instructions of Amenemope** (c. 1100 BC):
- Both have 30 "chapters" (Proverbs 22:20)
- Both use a father instructing his son format
- Both command not to move boundary stones (Proverbs 22:28)

Israel's [[wisdom]] tradition was not isolated but engaged with international [[wisdom]], transformed by the foundational principle: "The [[fear of the Lord]]." The New Testament echoes: "Bring them up in the nurture and admonition of the Lord" (Ephesians 6:4).
