## Summary

Isaiah 9:6 is one of the most beloved Christmas passages in Scripture: 'For unto us a Child is born, unto us a Son is given.' Pastor Chuck explores the four-fold description of the coming Messiah — Wonderful Counselor, Mighty God, Everlasting Father, Prince of Peace — and how each title reveals a different facet of Christ's divine nature and redemptive work.

## 📜 Historical Background

Isaiah 9:6 is part of a [[Prophecy|prophetic]] section ([[Isaiah 7_7-12.md]]) dealing with the sign of [[_Concepts/Doctrines/Immanuel]]. Chapter 9 follows the [[Prophecy]] of a great light shining in [[Galilee of the Gentiles|Galilee]] ([[Isaiah 9_1-2.md]]) and the joy of deliverance from oppression. The four throne-names given to the Child are extraordinary: each is a divine title. 'Wonderful Counselor' suggests supernatural wisdom; 'Mighty God' (El Gibbor) explicitly calls the child divine; 'Everlasting Father' (Abi Ad) indicates eternity and paternal care; 'Prince of Peace' (Sar Shalom) promises a reign of wholeness and well-being. This was spoken in the context of the Syro-Ephraimite War (c. 734 BC) when [[Judah]] faced annihilation, yet the [[Prophet]] looked beyond the immediate crisis to a future [[David|Davidic King]]. This [[Messiah|Messianic]] [[Prophecy]] points forward to the [[Restoration]] of Israel under the [[servant|Servant of the Lord]]. Like the later prophets [[Jeremiah]] and [[Ezekiel]], Isaiah saw beyond [[Judgment]] to the coming [[Remnant]] that would return to the Lord. The [[Messiah]] foretold here would fulfill what [[Daniel]]'s visions later detailed — an everlasting kingdom.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

[[Matthew 4_15-16.md]] directly quotes [[Isaiah 9_1-2.md]] as fulfilled in Jesus' Galilean ministry. The titles are realized in Christ: He is our Wonderful Counselor ([[_Bible/Colossians/Colossians 2_3.md]]), Mighty God ([[John 1_1-3]], [[Colossians 1_15-17.md]]), Everlasting Father ([[John 14_9]] — 'He who has seen me has seen the Father'), and Prince of Peace ([[Ephesians 2_14.md]] — 'He is our peace'). The angel's announcement to Mary in [[Luke 1_32-33.md]] echoes the promise of an eternal Davidic kingdom.

## 💡 Pastoral Application
Chuck Smith often used this verse at Christmas to emphasize that the baby in the manger was no ordinary child — He was God incarnate. 'Unto us' highlights the personal nature of the gift: the Son was given for us, to us. The four titles invite us to know Christ more deeply — as the source of wisdom, the strength of God, the compassionate Father, and the giver of [[Philippians 4_7.md]].
