## Summary

God appears to [[Solomon]] at [[Gibeon]] in a dream, offering to give him whatever he asks. Solomon's humble request for an [[discernment|understanding heart]] to govern [[Gods People|God's people]] well — rather than [[Long Life|long life]], [[Riches|riches]], or the death of his enemies — pleases God so deeply that He grants [[wisdom]] plus all the things Solomon did not ask for. This teaching explores the priority of seeking spiritual wisdom over material blessings, the nature of [[Leadership|godly leadership]], and the principle that when we seek first the [[Kingdom of God]], all other things are added to us. [[Pastor Chuck Smith|Pastor Chuck]] encourages believers to ask for the 'much more' that God desires to give.

## 📜 Historical Background

[[Solomon]]'s reign began around 970 BC, inheriting a [[United Monarchy|united kingdom]] at the height of its territorial extent — from the [[Euphrates River]] to the border of [[Egypt]]. Early in his kingship, Solomon traveled to [[Gibeon]], the primary high place where the bronze altar from the [[Tabernacle]] (crafted under [[Moses]] at [[Sinai]]) still stood in the absence of a permanent [[Temple in Jerusalem|Temple]]. Offering a thousand burnt offerings — an extravagant act of devotion matching the scale of his new responsibilities — Solomon positioned himself in the tradition of [[Patriarchs|patriarchal worship]] at altars. That night, God appeared to him in a dream with an open-ended offer. Solomon's choice of a 'listening heart' (Hebrew *lev shomea* — a hearing, discerning heart) over wealth, long life, or military victory reveals a profound sense of inadequacy for the monumental task of shepherding God's [[Covenant People|covenant people]]. God's delighted response — granting unparalleled [[wisdom]] plus the [[Riches|riches]] and honor Solomon did not seek — establishes a foundational biblical principle: when God's kingdom purposes take priority, [[Divine Providence|divine provision]] follows. This moment also casts a shadow across the united monarchy: Solomon's later failure to maintain these priorities would ultimately fracture the nation [[David|his father]] unified, setting the stage for the [[divided kingdom|divided kingdom]] chronicled in the rest of 1 and 2 Kings.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

[[Jesus Christ|Jesus]] is described as the embodiment of divine [[wisdom]] — 'Christ the power of God and the wisdom of God' ([[_Bible/1 Corinthians/1 Corinthians 1_24.md]]). [[James]] promises that 'if any of you lacks wisdom, let him ask of God, who gives to all liberally.' [[Jesus Christ|Jesus]] taught that seeking first the [[Kingdom of God]] brings all other things as additions ([[Matthew 6_33.md]]). Solomon's request for wisdom prefigures [[Jesus Christ|Christ]] who, though rich, became poor for our sakes ([[_Bible/2 Corinthians/2 Corinthians 8_9.md]]).

## 💡 Pastoral Application

When God offers you a blank check, what do you ask for? Solomon's request reveals his heart: not [[Riches|riches]], honor, or long life, but [[wisdom]] to serve God's people well. [[Pastor Chuck Smith|Chuck Smith]] applied this by asking believers to examine their [[Prayer|prayers]]: What are you asking God for? Do your requests reflect [[Kingdom of God|kingdom]] priorities or personal comfort? [[Jesus Christ|Jesus]]' promise that 'seek first the kingdom of God and His righteousness, and all these things shall be added to you' ([[Matthew 6_33.md]]) captures the heart of Solomon's story. God still gives 'much more' to those who prioritize His purposes.

## 📖 Research Flavor

**Gibeon's High Place and the Bronze Altar.** The bronze altar made by [[Bezalel]] ([[Exodus 38.md]]) stood at Gibeon because the [[Tabernacle]] was still there in Solomon's early reign. This altar, approximately 7.5 ft square and 4.5 ft high ([[Exodus 27_1-2.md]]), was the central place of sacrifice until the [[Temple in Jerusalem|Temple]] was built. Archaeological excavations at el-Jib (ancient Gibeon) have uncovered a remarkable pool-and-staircase system 37 ft in diameter and 35 ft deep, hewn from solid rock — likely the 'pool of Gibeon' mentioned in [[2 Samuel 2_13.md]]. The site also contained extensive wine cellars, confirming Gibeon's reputation for viticulture in antiquity.

**ANE Wisdom Parallels.** Solomon's dream-request echoes a genre of [[Ancient Near East|ancient Near Eastern]] wisdom literature where kings ask gods for discernment. The [[Egypt|Egyptian]] 'Instructions of Amenemope' (c. 1100 BC) advises, 'Better is bread with a happy heart than riches with contention.' The Babylonian king [[Nabonidus]] claimed the gods gave him wisdom in a dream. Yet Solomon's request stands apart from its ANE counterparts: he asks not for military success, divination power, or personal prosperity, but for *judicial wisdom* to serve God's people — a radical inversion of expected royal priorities that reflects [[Israel|Israel's]] [[Covenant People|covenant identity]] as a people called to reflect God's justice.

**The 'Much More' Principle in Biblical Theology.** The Greek *mallon* in [[Jesus Christ|Jesus]]' teaching ('how much more,' [[Matthew 6_30.md]]; [[Matthew 7_11.md]]) echoes the Hebrew *gam* ('also') in God's response to Solomon. Where Solomon asked for one thing (wisdom), God gave him 'also' [[Riches|riches]], honor, and long life — a pattern of superabundant [[Grace|grace]] that [[Jesus Christ|Jesus]] amplified: 'Seek first the [[Kingdom of God]] and His righteousness, and all these things shall be added to you' ([[Matthew 6_33.md]]). This 'much more' principle runs throughout Scripture: God exceeds our requests because His nature is generous beyond measure.
