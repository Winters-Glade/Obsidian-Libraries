## Summary

'Fear thou not; for I am with thee: be not dismayed; for I am thy God: I will strengthen thee; yea, I will help thee; yea, I will uphold thee with the right hand of my righteousness.' One of Scripture's most comforting promises, Pastor Chuck examines the basis for courage in the character and promises of God. This [[Prophecy]] of reassurance speaks into the [[Judgment|exile]] and points toward future [[Restoration]].

## 📜 Historical Background

Isaiah 41 begins a series of 'fear not' declarations addressed to Israel in exile. God introduces Himself as the one who holds history in His hands — He calls Cyrus by name before he is born (Isaiah 45:1) and controls the rise and fall of nations. The phrase 'Fear thou not' appears repeatedly because God knows the natural human response to overwhelming circumstances. Each reassurance is anchored in a different aspect of God's character: His presence ('I am with thee'), His identity ('I am thy God'), His strength ('I will strengthen thee'), His help ('I will help thee'), and His righteousness ('I will uphold thee').

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

The assurance 'I am with thee' is fulfilled supremely in Jesus Christ, who is called 'Immanuel — God with us' ([[_Bible/Matthew/Matthew 1_23.md]]). Jesus repeatedly tells His disciples 'Fear not' ([[Matthew 14_27.md]], [[Luke 12_32]]). The promise 'I will strengthen thee' finds its New Testament counterpart in [[_Bible/Philippians/Philippians 4_13.md]]: 'I can do all things through Christ which strengtheneth me.' The right hand of God's righteousness is ultimately Christ Himself, seated at the right hand of God ([[Romans 8_3]]).

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith emphasized that God doesn't just tell us not to be afraid — He gives us five solid reasons. Each 'fear not' is followed by a promise we can stand on. The progression is beautiful: presence, relationship, strength, help, and support. When we feel weak, God's strength is made perfect. The 'right hand of righteousness' suggests God doesn't just help us survive — He upholds us in a way that honors Him.
