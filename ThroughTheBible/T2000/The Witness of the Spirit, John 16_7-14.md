## Summary

John 16:7-14 records Jesus' promise of the Holy Spirit — the Comforter, the Spirit of Truth. Jesus said it was 'expedient' (profitable, advantageous) for Him to go away so that the Spirit could come. Pastor Chuck explores the Spirit's work: convicting the world of sin, righteousness, and judgment; guiding believers into all truth; and glorifying Christ by taking what is His and declaring it to us. The Spirit's ministry is essential to the life of the Church.

## 📖 Research Context

This passage is the culmination of Jesus' teaching on the Holy Spirit in the Upper Room, following the first promise of the Paraklētos in John 14:16-17, 26. The term 'Comforter' (Paraklētos) is a legal term meaning 'one called alongside' — an advocate, counselor, or defense attorney. Jesus is the first Paraklētos (1 John 2:1 — 'we have an Advocate with the Father, Jesus Christ the righteous'); the Spirit is the second. The disciples' sorrow at Jesus' departure reflects their failure to understand that the Incarnation was always temporary — the Spirit's coming would be permanent and universal. Jesus says it is 'expedient' (sympherei — 'profitable, advantageous, better') for Him to go. The Spirit's threefold work of conviction (elenchō) — sin, righteousness, and judgment — forms a complete soteriological framework: sin reveals our need, righteousness reveals God's provision in Christ, judgment reveals the consequence of rejection. The phrase 'He will not speak on His own authority' (ou gar lalēsei aph' heautou) underscores the Spirit's role as the one who points to Christ — the Spirit never draws attention to Himself but always to Jesus.

Key Old Testament cross-references: Joel 2:28-32 (the universal outpouring), Jeremiah 31:31-34 (New Covenant — the law written on hearts), Ezekiel 36:26-27 (a new heart and a new spirit), Isaiah 11:2 (the Spirit of the Lord shall rest upon Him).

## 📜 Historical Background

The disciples were deeply troubled by Jesus' announcement of His departure. They couldn't understand how His leaving could be good. Jesus explains that the Spirit's coming depends on His going. In Jewish thought, the Spirit was the voice of prophecy that had been silent for 400 years between Malachi and John the Baptist. The promise of the Spirit's permanent indwelling was revolutionary. The Spirit's threefold conviction — of sin (the root problem), righteousness (the solution in Christ), and judgment (the consequence of rejecting Christ) — is the basis of evangelism.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

Acts 1:8 — you shall receive power when the Holy Spirit comes upon you. Acts 2 — Pentecost: the fulfillment of this promise. Romans 8:26 — the Spirit helps our weaknesses and intercedes for us. 1 Corinthians 2:10-14 — the Spirit searches the deep things of God and reveals them. Galatians 5:22-23 — the fruit of the Spirit. Ephesians 1:13-14 — the Spirit is the seal and guarantee of our inheritance. Romans 8:16 — the Spirit bears witness with our spirit. Romans 8:14-17 — led by the Spirit, we cry Abba, Father. 1 Corinthians 12:3 — no one can say Jesus is Lord except by the Holy Spirit.

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith taught that the Spirit's primary work is not to draw attention to Himself but to glorify Christ. Any genuine work of the Spirit will center on Jesus. The Spirit convicts — not to condemn but to convince. He opens blind eyes to see sin for what it is, righteousness as a gift in Christ, and judgment as certain for those who reject God's provision. The Spirit also guides believers — not just once but continually — into all truth. We don't need to fear being led astray if we are walking by the Spirit.
