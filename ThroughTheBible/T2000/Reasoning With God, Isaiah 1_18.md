## Summary

|[[Isaiah 1_18]] is one of the most gracious invitations in all of Scripture: 'Come now, and let us reason together, saith the Lord: though your sins be as scarlet, they shall be as white as snow; though they be red like crimson, they shall be as wool.' Pastor Chuck explores the heart of God's invitation to [[Repentance]] and the promise of complete [[forgiveness]].

## 📜 Historical Background

|[[Isaiah 1]] sets the stage for the entire book as a courtroom scene. God brings a '[[Covenant]] lawsuit' (rîb) against His people. [[Israel]] had rebelled, and their [[Worship]] had become empty ritual. Despite their unfaithfulness, God extends an offer of cleansing — scarlet sins made white as snow. This chapter introduces the themes of [[Judgment]] and [[Redemption]] that permeate Isaiah's [[Prophecy]]. The reference to 'scarlet' and 'crimson' evokes the deep, permanent stains of sin that only divine [[Grace]] can remove.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

|The invitation of [[Isaiah 1_18]] finds its ultimate fulfillment in the atoning work of [[Christ]]. [[_Bible/1 Peter/1 Peter 1_18-19.md]] speaks of [[Redemption]] 'not with corruptible things, but with the precious blood of Christ.' [[Romans 5_8-9.md]] declares that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us, and we are now 'justified by His blood.' The imagery of scarlet sins becoming white as snow prefigures the washing of regeneration ([[Titus 3_5-7]]).

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith often emphasized that God doesn't bargain with us — He invites us to reason together. This verse shows that true repentance isn't about God meeting us halfway; it's about coming to God on His terms and receiving His complete, unconditional forgiveness. The offer is extended to all, regardless of the depth or color of their sin.
