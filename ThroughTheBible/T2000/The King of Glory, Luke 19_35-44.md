## Summary

The [[Triumphal Entry]] — [[Jesus]] rides into [[Jerusalem]] on a donkey as crowds spread their cloaks and cry 'Blessed be the King that cometh in the name of the Lord!' Yet Jesus weeps over the city, knowing that within a week He will be crucified and that [[Jerusalem]] will face destruction.

## 📜 Historical Background

The [[Triumphal Entry]] occurred on the Sunday before Jesus' crucifixion (Palm Sunday). The donkey was a deliberate fulfillment of [[Zechariah 9_9.md]] — a symbol of [[peace]], not war. A king riding a horse came to conquer; a king riding a donkey came in [[peace]]. The crowds shouted 'Hosanna' (meaning 'Save now'), expecting political deliverance from Rome. But Jesus came as a different kind of King — one who would conquer sin and death through His own sacrifice.

## ✝️ New Testament Connection

[[Zechariah 9_9.md]] is directly fulfilled: 'Rejoice greatly, O daughter of [[Zion]]... behold, thy King cometh unto thee... lowly, and riding upon an ass.' The same crowd that cried 'Hosanna' on Sunday would cry 'Crucify Him' by Friday — revealing the fickleness of human acclaim. In the book of [[revelation]], Jesus is revealed as 'King of kings and Lord of lords' ([[Revelation 19_16.md]]), the ultimate fulfillment of the King of [[_Concepts/Doctrines/glory]].

## 💡 Pastoral Application

Chuck Smith observed that Jesus wept over Jerusalem because He knew what they were missing — the very [[peace]] they cried for was standing before them, but they couldn't see it. The lesson for us is that we can be religiously fervent yet spiritually blind. True worship recognizes who Jesus really is, not just who we want Him to be.
