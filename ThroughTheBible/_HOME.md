# Through the Bible with Pastor Chuck Smith

> Complete verse-by-verse teaching series by **Pastor Chuck Smith**:

> complemented by the **T2000 Topical Series**.

---

## About Pastor Chuck Smith

Pastor Chuck Smith (1927–2013) was the founder of Calvary Chapel Costa Mesa and a leading figure in the Jesus Movement of the 1960s and 1970s. His verse-by-verse teaching style, warm pastoral heart, and commitment to the authority of Scripture have influenced millions worldwide through the Calvary Chapel movement.

## Series Overview

### C2000 Verse-by-Verse Series

The C2000 Series is Pastor Chuck Smith's complete verse-by-verse exposition of the entire Bible, originally broadcast on radio. These are structured by book and chapter.

- **Location:** `C2000/Notes/` (study notes by book), `C2000/Transcripts/` (transcripts by book)
- **Notes:** Comprehensive chapter-by-chapter study notes
- **Transcripts:** Full teaching transcripts organized by Bible book

### T2000 Topical Series

The T2000 Series is Pastor Chuck Smith's topical teaching series, originally divided into 14 batches covering the entire Bible. Each batch focuses on a specific section or theme.

- **Location:** `T2000/`
- **Format:** Standalone topical study notes

## About This Vault

This vault is a comprehensive Obsidian knowledge base of Pastor Chuck Smith's teaching ministry. It combines verse-by-verse exposition, topical studies, transcripts, concept notes, and Bible verse references into a fully linked, navigable system.

### Directory Structure

| Directory | Contents |
|-----------|----------|
| **C2000/Notes/** | 342 chapter-by-chapter study notes from the C2000 Verse-by-Verse Series, organized by Bible book (65 books) |
| **C2000/Transcripts/** | 321 full teaching transcripts from the C2000 radio broadcasts, organized by Bible book |
| **T2000/** | 270 standalone topical study notes from the T2000 Topical Series |
| **_transcripts/** | 272 YouTube video transcripts of Pastor Chuck Smith teachings |
| **_Concepts/** | 2,200 concept notes across 6 categories: Doctrines (72), Events (14), People (382), Places (320), Terms (1,412), and Biblical References |
| **_Bible/** | 3,198 Bible verse notes, one per referenced chapter of each book (66 books, 67 directories) |
| **_MOCs/** | 32 Maps of Content that aggregate and cross-link notes by book, theme, people, concepts, and Bible section |
| **Root files** | `_HOME.md`, `_INDEX.md` (alphabetical index), `_BIBLE_INDEX.md` (verse reference index) |

### How Navigation Works

1. **Start here** — `_HOME.md` is the vault home page with Quick Navigation links to all major entry points
2. **Maps of Content (MOCs)** — Located in `_MOCs/`, these serve as curated entry points:
   - **By Bible Book** → [[_MOC_By_Book]] — canonical book order
   - **By Theme** → [[_MOC_by_Theme]] — thematic categories
   - **By People** → [[_MOC_People]] — biblical figures
   - **By Concepts** → [[_MOC_Concepts]] — doctrines, people, places, events
   - **Section MOCs** — 14 section-specific MOCs covering the Bible chronologically
   - **Batch MOCs** — 14 T2000 batch MOCs aligned with the teaching series
3. **Alphabetical Index** → [[_INDEX]] — every note in the vault listed A–Z
4. **Bible Verse Index** → [[_BIBLE_INDEX]] — every Bible verse referenced, organized by book

### Conventions

- **Wikilinks:** All internal cross-references use `[[Note Name]]` (Obsidian wikilinks) for automatic graph traversal and backlinking
- **YAML Frontmatter:** Every note includes `title`, `tags`, and where applicable `aliases` and `created` dates, enabling Obsidian's native search, Dataview queries, and tag-based aggregation
- **Bible Reference Format:** Chapter-level verse notes use the naming convention `Bible: Book Chapter:Verse` (e.g., `Bible: Romans 8:38.md`) and are stored in `_Bible/` by book directory
- **File Naming:** Spaces in file names are replaced with underscores for reliable cross-platform compatibility. Display titles use the `title` field in frontmatter
- **Concept Notes:** Located in `_Concepts/` with subdirectories for Doctrines, Events, People, Places, and Terms. Each concept note includes `aliases` for variant spellings and alternative names
- **MOC Naming:** All MOC files are prefixed `_MOC_` for easy identification and sorted predictably in file browsers
