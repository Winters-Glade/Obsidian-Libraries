## Historical & Cultural Context

> [!NOTE] QT Verification
> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### The Mission of the Twelve
The sending of the Twelve (Luke 9:1-6) parallels Matthew 10 and Mark 6:7-13. Jesus gives them "power and authority" over demons and diseases — a dual emphasis on healing and exorcism characteristic of Luke's attention to physical and spiritual liberation.

### Feeding the 5000
The feeding of the 5,000 (Luke 9:10-17) is the only miracle recorded in all four Gospels ([[Matthew 14_13-21.md]], [[Mark 6_30-44.md]], [[John 6_1-14]]). Luke records that the location was [[Bethsaida]] — "House of Fishing" — fittingly on the northern shore of the [[Sea of Galilee]].

### Peter's Confession and the Transfiguration
Peter's confession (Luke 9:18-20) and the [[Transfiguration]] (Luke 9:28-36) are central turning points. Luke uniquely notes that Jesus was praying at both events and that Moses and Elijah "spoke of his departure (*exodos*) which he was about to accomplish at [[Jerusalem]]" (Luke 9:31) — a key term tying Jesus' death and resurrection to the Old Testament exodus.

### The Mission of the Seventy(-Two)
The sending of the [[Seventy Weeks Prophecy]] (Luke 10:1-24) is unique to Luke. The number 70 (or 72 in some manuscripts) echoes the 70 nations of the world in [[Genesis 10.md]] and the 70 elders appointed by Moses ([[Numbers 11_16-17.md]]), symbolizing the universal scope of the Gospel mission.

### The Good Samaritan
The parable of the [[Samaritans.md]] (Luke 10:25-37) is unique to Luke and one of the most famous stories in the Bible. The road from [[Jerusalem]] to [[Jericho]] was notoriously dangerous — a 17-mile descent through rocky terrain descending 3,000 feet. The Samaritans were despised by Jews as heretics and half-breeds. By making a Samaritan the hero, Jesus subverts ethnic prejudice.

### Mary and Martha
The visit to [[Martha|Mary]] and [[Martha]] in [[Bethany]] (Luke 10:38-42) contrasts the active life (service) with the contemplative life (listening to Jesus). Bethany, about 2 miles from [[Jerusalem]] on the eastern slope of the [[Mount of Olives]], was the home of Jesus' close friends.
