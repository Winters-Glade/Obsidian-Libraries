## Historical & Cultural Context

> [!NOTE] QT Verification
> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### Authority Questioned
The challenge to Jesus' authority (Luke 20:1-8) by the chief priests, scribes, and elders occurs in the Temple precincts during the final week. Jesus' counter-question about John's baptism exposes their unwillingness to commit to the truth.

### The Parable of the Tenants
The parable of the wicked tenants (Luke 20:9-19) echoes [[Isaiah 5_1-7.md]] and was clearly understood by the leaders as directed against them (Luke 20:19). The vineyard allegory — the tenants representing Israel's leaders, the servants representing the prophets, and the "beloved son" representing Jesus — is unmistakable.

### Tribute to Caesar
The question about tribute (Luke 20:20-26) was intended to trap Jesus into either losing popular support (if he endorsed the tax) or being reported to Rome (if he rejected it). The [[Darius.md]] bore the image of [[Julius Caesar.md]] with the inscription "Tiberius Caesar, son of the Divine Augustus" — an idolatrous claim to divinity for Jewish ears. Jesus' answer transcends the trap.

### The Resurrection Question
The [[Sadducees]]' question about the woman with seven husbands (Luke 20:27-40) was a classic argument against the resurrection. Jesus affirms resurrection, citing [[Exodus 3_6]] and describing the resurrected state as being "like angels" — neither marrying nor given in marriage.

### The Destruction of the Temple
The prophecy of the Temple's destruction (Luke 21:5-6) was fulfilled in AD 70. The siege of [[Jerusalem]] by [[Titus]] was devastating: the historian [[Josephus]] (a contemporary eyewitness) records that 1.1 million people died and 97,000 were taken captive. The [[Titus]] in Rome commemorates the victory and depicts Roman soldiers carrying the Temple treasures, including the [[Menorah]].

### The Widow's Offering
The widow's two mites (Luke 21:1-4) — a tiny offering — is praised by Jesus as the greatest gift, because she gave "all the living she had."
