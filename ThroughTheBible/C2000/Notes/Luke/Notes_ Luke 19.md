## Historical & Cultural Context

> [!NOTE] QT Verification
> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### Zacchaeus the Tax Collector
The story of [[Zacchaeus]] (Luke 19:1-10) is unique to Luke and exemplifies the Gospel's emphasis on salvation reaching the outcast. Zacchaeus was "chief tax collector" (*architelōnēs*) — a senior figure who oversaw a network of tax collectors in the rich agricultural area around [[Jericho]]. Jericho was a center of balsam and date production, making it a lucrative tax district. The sycamore-fig tree (*Ficus sycomorus*) Zacchaeus climbed was a common tree in the Jericho region, with low branches ideal for climbing.

### The Parable of the Ten Minas
The parable of the ten minas (Luke 19:11-27) — similar to the parable of the talents in [[Matthew 25_14-30.md]] — is set in the context of a nobleman going to receive a kingdom. This may allude to [[Berenice (daughter of Herod Agrippa)]], who traveled to Rome in 4 BC to be confirmed as king (and was opposed by a delegation of Jews). The parallel would have resonated with Luke's audience.

### The Triumphal Entry
The [[Triumphal Entry]] (Luke 19:28-44) contains unique details: Jesus weeps over [[Jerusalem]] (Luke 19:41-44) and predicts the city's destruction by an enemy who will "surround you and hem you in on every side" — precisely fulfilled in AD 70 when [[Titus]]' Roman legions besieged [[Jerusalem]]. The descent from the [[Mount of Olives]] to the city would have provided a panoramic view.

### Cleansing the Temple
The cleansing of the Temple (Luke 19:45-48) interrupts the money-changing and animal-selling in the Court of the Gentiles. Daily teaching in the Temple became Jesus' pattern for the remainder of his ministry.
