## Historical & Cultural Context

> [!NOTE] QT Verification
> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### Historical Anchoring
Luke's precise dating of [[John the Baptist]]'s ministry (Luke 3:1-2) using six contemporary rulers — [[Julius Caesar.md]], [[Pontius Pilate]], [[Herod Agrippa.md]], [[Philip the Evangelist.md]], [[Lycaonia.md]], and the high priests [[Annals.md]] and [[Caiaphas]] — reflects his historical methodology. This dates the beginning of John's ministry to approximately AD 28–29.

### Genealogy of Jesus
Luke's genealogy (Luke 3:23-38) traces Jesus back to [[Adam]], "the son of God" — emphasizing Jesus as the universal Savior for all humanity. This contrasts with Matthew's genealogy, which traces Jesus through [[Abraham]] and David, emphasizing his Jewish messianic credentials. Key differences: Luke's genealogy goes backward (not forward) and has different names in the David-to-Joseph segment.

### The Temptation
The temptation (Luke 4:1-13) is more detailed than Mark's account (Mark 1:12-13) and differs from Matthew's order (Luke places the [[Jerusalem]] temptation second, the mountain temptation third). Jesus' responses from [[Deuteronomy 7_6-8.md]] emphasize obedience to God's word.

### [[Nazareth]] Rejection
Jesus' sermon in [[Nazareth]] (Luke 4:16-30) — reading from [[Isaiah 61_1-2.md]] in the synagogue — is the programmatic statement for Luke's Gospel. The synagogue service of the period included the [[Shema]], prayers, a reading from the Law, a reading from the Prophets (the [[Zarah]]), and a sermon. Jesus' reference to [[Elijah]] and [[Elisha]] ministering to Gentiles (the widow of [[Zarah.md]] and [[Ephrem the Syrian.md]]) foreshadows the Gentile mission. The attempt to throw Jesus off a cliff reflects [[Nazareth]]'s location on a hillside.

### [[Capernaum]] Ministry
The healing of the demoniac in the [[Capernaum]] synagogue (Luke 4:31-37) and Peter's mother-in-law (Luke 4:38-44) demonstrate Jesus' authority over both the spirit world and physical illness.
