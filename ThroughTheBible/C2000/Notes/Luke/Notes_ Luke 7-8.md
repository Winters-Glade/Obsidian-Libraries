## Historical & Cultural Context

> [!NOTE] QT Verification
> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### The Centurion's Servant
The healing of the centurion's servant (Luke 7:1-10) highlights a Gentile's remarkable faith. The centurion had even built a synagogue for the Jewish community — a practice attested archaeologically at [[Capernaum]] (the synagogue there, though later, was built on earlier foundations). The Roman military presence in [[Galilee]] under [[Herod Agrippa.md]] included centurions stationed at key towns.

### Raising the Widow's Son at Nain
The raising of the widow's son at [[Cain.md]] (Luke 7:11-17) is unique to Luke. Nain (modern Nein) is a village on the northwestern slope of [[Mount Moriah.md]], about 25 miles from [[Capernaum]]. The widow losing her only son meant she would be left destitute (no one to support her). Jesus' compassion — "he had compassion on her" — before even being asked is characteristic of Luke's portrayal of Jesus. This miracle echoes [[Elijah]] raising the widow's son at [[Zarah.md]] ([[1 Kings 17_17-24.md]]) and [[Elisha]] raising the Shunammite's son ([[2 Kings 4_32-37.md]]).

### [[John the Baptist]]'s Question
John's question from prison (Luke 7:18-35) — "Are you the one who is to come?" — reveals the tension between John's expectation of a judgment-bringing [[Messiah]] and Jesus' ministry of healing and mercy. Jesus' response alludes to [[Isaiah 35_5-6.md]] and [[Isaiah 61_1]].

### The Sinful Woman
The anointing by the sinful woman at the house of [[The Pharisees.md]] (Luke 7:36-50) is a different event from the anointing at Bethany (Matthew 26:6-13). The contrast between the Pharisee's lack of hospitality and the woman's extravagant love illustrates forgiveness and gratitude.

### Parables and Miracles
The parable of the sower (Luke 8:4-15), the calming of the storm (Luke 8:22-25), the Gadarene demoniac (Luke 8:26-39), and the raising of Jairus' daughter (Luke 8:40-56) all have Synoptic parallels (cf. [[Matthew 15_8-9.md]], [[Mark 6_45.md]]).
