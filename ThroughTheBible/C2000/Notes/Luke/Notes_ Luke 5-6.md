## Historical & Cultural Context

> [!NOTE] QT Verification
> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### Calling the First Disciples
The miraculous catch of fish (Luke 5:1-11) is unique to Luke's account of the calling of [[Peter]], [[Zebedee|James]], and [[John the Apostle|John]]. The [[Sea of Galilee]]]] was known for abundant fish — the historian Josephus records that the lake supported a thriving fishing industry with over 230 boats. The fish were likely [[Tilapia|St. Peter's fish]] (tilapia) and sardines.

### Healing the Leper and the Paralytic
The healing of the leper (Luke 5:12-16) and the paralytic (Luke 5:17-26) demonstrate Jesus' authority over impurity and sin. The Pharisees and teachers present "from every village of [[Galilee]] and [[Judea]] and from [[Jerusalem]]" (Luke 5:17) show that Jesus' reputation had spread widely, bringing scrutiny from religious authorities.

### Calling Levi
The call of [[Levi]] (Luke 5:27-32) and the feast at his house highlight Luke's theme of Jesus' association with tax collectors and sinners. The Greek word *hamartōloi* ("sinners") refers to those who did not observe the Pharisaic purity regulations.

### Sabbath Controversies
The controversies over plucking grain on the Sabbath and the healing of the withered hand (Luke 6:1-11) reflect the growing opposition from the Pharisees.

### The Sermon on the Plain
Luke 6:17-49 (the [[Sermon on the Mount.md]]) is Luke's parallel to Matthew's [[Sermon on the Mount]] (Matthew 5-7). Luke's version is shorter, delivered on a "level place" (not a mountain), and includes only four beatitudes with corresponding woes. The emphasis on poverty, hunger, and persecution reflects Luke's concern for the poor. The "Golden Rule" (Luke 6:31) is stated positively here, as in Matthew 7:12.
