## Historical & Cultural Context

> [!NOTE] QT Verification
> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### Trial Before Pilate and Herod
Jesus' trial before [[Pontius Pilate]] (Luke 23:1-25) includes the unique detail of Pilate sending Jesus to [[Herod Agrippa.md]] (Luke 23:6-12). Herod was in [[Jerusalem]] for the Passover, and his curiosity about Jesus (he had wanted to see a miracle) went unsatisfied. The mockery by Herod and his soldiers (Luke 23:11) fulfills the rejection of the [[Messiah]] by both Jewish and Gentile authorities (cf. [[Psalm 2_2]]). The release of [[Barabbas]] (a rebel and murderer, Luke 23:18-19) creates the ironic substitution of the guilty for the innocent.

### The Crucifixion
The crucifixion (Luke 23:26-49) includes unique details: Jesus' words to the "daughters of [[Jerusalem]]" (Luke 23:27-31, referencing [[Hosea 10_8]]), his prayer for forgiveness — "Father, forgive them, for they know not what they do" (Luke 23:34, absent in some early manuscripts), the penitent thief (Luke 23:39-43), and Jesus' final cry — "Father, into your hands I commit my spirit" (Luke 23:46, quoting [[Psalm 31_5]]).

### The Darkening and the Torn Veil
The darkness over the land (Luke 23:44-45) and the tearing of the Temple veil (the *parochet*, a massive curtain separating the [[Holy of Holies]] from the Holy Place) symbolize cosmic judgment and the opening of access to God.

### The Burial
[[Joseph of Arimathea]]'s burial of Jesus (Luke 23:50-56) fulfills [[Isaiah 53_9]]. The women prepare spices and ointments — they would return after the Sabbath to complete the anointing.

### The Resurrection
The resurrection account (Luke 24:1-12) begins with the women at the empty tomb. Two angels (not one, as in Matthew and Mark) announce the resurrection. The [[_MOC_by_Theme.md]] (Luke 24:13-35) — unique to Luke — is one of the most beautiful narratives in the Gospels. The village of Emmaus, about 7 miles (60 stadia) from [[Jerusalem]], is identified with several possible sites ([[Motza]], [[Tell Abu Hureira]], [[Emmaus]]). The exposition of the Scriptures — beginning with [[Moses]] and the [[Prophets]] — shows that the suffering of the Christ was prophesied throughout the Old Testament.

### The [[Great Commission]] (Luke's Version)
Jesus' final commission (Luke 24:44-49) — "that repentance and forgiveness of sins should be proclaimed in his name to all nations" — emphasizes the worldwide scope of the mission, beginning from [[Jerusalem]]. The [[Ascension]] (Luke 24:50-53) concludes Luke's Gospel and provides the transition to the [[Book of Enoch.md]].
