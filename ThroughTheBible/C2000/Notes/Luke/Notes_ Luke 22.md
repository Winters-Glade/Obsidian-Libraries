## Historical & Cultural Context

> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### The Plot and the Passover
The conspiracy to kill Jesus (Luke 22:1-6) involved the chief priests and scribes, with [[Judas Iscariot]] offering to betray him. The [[Feast of Unleavened Bread]] (Passover) was the setting — commemorating Israel's deliverance from Egypt ([[Exodus 12.md]]). The "sacrifice of the Passover lamb" in the Temple was a central ritual, with families bringing lambs to be slaughtered by the priests.

### The Last Supper
The [[Last Supper]] (Luke 22:7-38) contains Luke's distinctive version of the institution. Luke uniquely records a second cup (Luke 22:17-18) before the bread, and the dispute among the disciples about who is greatest (Luke 22:24-30), which Jesus transforms into a teaching about servant leadership. The prediction of Peter's denial (Luke 22:31-34) is framed as [[Satan]]'s demand to "sift" the disciples like wheat — an agricultural image of testing.

### The Garden of [[Gethsemane]]
Jesus' agony in [[Gethsemane]] (Luke 22:39-46) is described with unique details: the sweat like "great drops of blood" and the strengthening angel. [[Gethsemane]] (from Aramaic "oil press") was on the [[Mount of Olives]]. The disciples' sleep from "sorrow" reflects their exhaustion and grief.

### The Arrest and Peter's Denial
The arrest (Luke 22:47-53) includes the healing of the servant's ear (only in Luke). Peter's denial (Luke 22:54-62) — three times before the rooster crows — is one of the most poignant moments in the Gospels. The cockcrow likely refers to the third watch of the night (midnight to 3 AM), when a rooster's crow signaled morning.

### The Mocking and Trial
The guards mock Jesus (Luke 22:63-65), and the morning trial before the [[Sanhedrin]] (Luke 22:66-71) elicits Jesus' clear confession: "You say that I am" — the divine name allusion (echoing [[Exodus 3_14]]).
