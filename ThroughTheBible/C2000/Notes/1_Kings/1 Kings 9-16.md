## Overview

[[1 Kings 9_1-9.md]], as we begin our study of the Word this evening. At the beginning of [[Solomon]]'s reign, the Lord appeared to him while he was in Gibeah, there offering sacrifices unto God. And the Lord basically said to [[Solomon]], ask me whatever you want ([[1 Kings 3_5]]). And so [[Solomon]] asked that the Lord would give him wisdom and understanding that he might govern over this glorious people of God. And God was pleased with the request that [[Solomon]] made because he didn't ask for riches, for the life of his enemies, or for fame. The Lord said, because you have asked that you might just have wisdom and understanding, I'm going to give you what you have asked. But I'm going to give you even more than that. I'm going to give you honor and fame and riches and all in abundance ([[1 Kings 3_11-13.md]]). So, as we get to the 9th chapter, we find the Lord appearing to [[Solomon]] the second time. [[Solomon]] has now completed the [[Temple|temple]], which took him 7 years to build ([[1 Kings 6_38.md]]). And he has also completed his own palace, which took him 13 years to build ([[1 Kings 7_1]]). So, the 20-year building project is over ([[1 Kings 9_10.md]]). And the Lord now appears to [[Solomon]], who has, of course, gained in fame and stature and notoriety through the world for his marvelous wisdom and the glories of the kingdom that he has established ([[1 Kings 10_1-13.md]]). And so, when he was finished the building, the Lord came to him the second time and warned him about obedience and warned him about turning aside to follow other gods ([[1 Kings 9_4-9.md]]). But sadly, [[Solomon]]'s heart was turned away by his foreign wives, and he indulged in [[idolatry|idolatry]] ([[1 Kings 11_1-8.md]]), setting the stage for the division of the [[monarchy|monarchy]] after his death. The kingdom was torn in two under [[Rehoboam]] and [[Jeroboam]] ([[1 Kings 12_1-24.md]]), and [[Jeroboam]] established rival worship centers at [[Bethel]] and [[Dan]] with golden calves ([[1 Kings 12_25-33.md]]), leading Israel into [[idolatry|idolatry]] and [[Prophecy|prophetic]] condemnation.

## Historical Context

> [!qt-verified] Historical Context

- The Divided Kingdom began ca. 930 BC after Rehoboam's harsh response to the northern tribes' request for lighter burdens (1 Kings 12)
- Jeroboam I established rival worship centers at Dan and Bethel with golden calves, creating a lasting religious schism (1 Kings 12:26–33)
- Solomon's later apostasy (1 Kings 11) — driven by his foreign wives who turned his heart after other gods — set the stage for the kingdom's division
- Archaeological findings that illuminate this period:
  - The **Tel Dan Stele** (ca. 840 BC) mentions the "House of David," providing extra-biblical confirmation of the Davidic dynasty
  - The Gezer gate, along with gates at Hazor and Megiddo, is attributed to Solomonic construction (1 Kings 9:15)
  - The Moabite Stone (Mesha Stele, ca. 840 BC) records the rebellion of Mesha of Moab against Israel, corresponding to 2 Kings 3
  - [[Samaria]] excavations have uncovered the royal palaces of the Omride dynasty (Ahab and Jezebel), including Phoenician-style ivory inlays
- The prophetic voices during this period — Ahijah ([[1 Kings 11_29-39.md]], [[1 Kings 14_1-18.md]]), Shemaiah ([[1 Kings 12_22-24.md]]), and unnamed prophets — served as God's conscience to the kings
