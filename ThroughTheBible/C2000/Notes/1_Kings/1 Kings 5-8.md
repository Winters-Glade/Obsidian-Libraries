## Overview

So [[Hiram]], the king of [[Tyre]], when he heard that [[Solomon]] was upon the throne in place of his father [[David]], for [[Hiram]] was always a great admirer of [[David]]. And [[Solomon]] sent to [[Hiram]] and he said, You know how that [[David]], my father, could not build a house unto the name of the Lord his God for the wars which were about him on every side until the Lord put them under the soles of his feet. But now the Lord my God hath given me rest on every side so that there is neither adversary nor evil occurrent ([[1 Kings 5_3-4.md]]). Behold, I purpose to build a house unto the name of the Lord my God ([[1 Kings 5_5]]). As the Lord spake to [[David]], my father, saying, Thy son, whom I will set upon the throne in your place, he will build a house unto my name. Now therefore, command that they hew me cedar trees out of Lebanon and my servants shall be able to build a house unto my name ([[1 Kings 5_6]]). And I shall be with thy servants. And unto thee will I give hire for thy servants according to all that you shall appoint. For thou knowest that there is not among any of us those that have the skill in cutting timber like those of Sidon. So it came to pass, and [[Hiram]] heard the words of [[Solomon]], that he rejoiced greatly and said, Blessed be the Lord this day which hath given unto [[David]] a wise son over this great people ([[1 Kings 5_7]]). So [[Hiram]] supplied [[Solomon]] with cedar and fir timber from Lebanon ([[1 Kings 5_10-11.md]]), and [[Solomon]] began the construction of the [[Temple|temple]] in [[Jerusalem]]. The [[Temple|temple]] took seven years to build ([[1 Kings 6_38.md]]), and when it was completed, the glory of the Lord filled the house ([[_Bible/1 Kings/1 Kings 8_10-11.md]]). [[Solomon]] then offered a magnificent prayer of dedication ([[1 Kings 8_22-53.md]]), asking the Lord to hear from heaven when His people prayed toward that place.

## Key Themes

- The construction of the [[Temple|temple]] as the central dwelling place of God among His people ([[1 Kings 5_1-18.md]], [[1 Kings 6_1-38.md]])
- The dedication of the [[Temple|temple]] and Solomon's prayer ([[1 Kings 8_1-66.md]])
- The partnership between [[Hiram]] of [[Tyre]] and [[Solomon]] as a model of international cooperation under God's blessing
- The [[Covenant|covenant]] relationship between God and Israel centered on the [[Temple|temple]] ([[1 Kings 8_22-53.md]])
- God's glory filling the temple as a sign of His presence and approval ([[_Bible/1 Kings/1 Kings 8_10-11.md]])
- The conditional nature of God's blessing upon Israel based on faithfulness ([[1 Kings 6_11-13.md]], [[1 Kings 8_25-26.md]], [[1 Kings 9_1-9.md]])

## Historical Context

> [!qt-verified] Historical Context

- The Temple was built ca. 966–959 BC, taking seven years to complete (1 Kings 6:38), with Solomon's palace taking another thirteen years
- This was the crowning achievement of Israel's united monarchy, centralizing worship in Jerusalem
- Archaeological evidence from the Ophel excavations has uncovered monumental architecture from the Solomonic era, including a large stone structure and a gate complex
- The Temple Mount in Jerusalem — though extensively rebuilt by Herod the Great — preserves the general location of Solomon's Temple
- Comparisons with other ancient Near Eastern temples such as Tell Tainat (ninth-century Syria) and Ain Dara (northern Syria) show shared architectural features: tripartite layout, side chambers, cherubim imagery
- The alliance with [[Hiram]] of Tyre brought Phoenician craftsmanship, cedar from Lebanon, and Mediterranean architectural expertise to Israel
- Solomon's international trade network encompassed Egyptian horses, Phoenician cedar, Arabian gold, and goods from Tarshish ([[1 Kings 10_22.md]])
