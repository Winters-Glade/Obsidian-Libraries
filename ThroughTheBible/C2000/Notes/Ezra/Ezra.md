## Overview

Let's turn now to the book of [[Ezra 8_20]]. The book of [[Ezra 8_20]], the first part, was probably written by [[Ezra 8_20]], but written from the records. Actually, at the time that [[Cyrus]] gave the decree to build the [[Temple|temple]], [[Ezra 8_20]] wasn't even alive. [[Ezra 8_20]] doesn't come into the story, into a first-hand account, until you get to chapter 7. The first six chapters deal with the first of the [[Restoration|repatriation]] of the nation Israel after the [[Exile|Babylonian captivity]]. Now, in the first year of [[Cyrus]], the king of [[Persia]], that the word of the Lord by the mouth of [[Jeremiah]] might be fulfilled, the Lord said, The Lord stirred up the spirit of [[Cyrus]], king of [[Persia]], that he made a proclamation throughout all of his kingdom, and put it also in writing, saying, Thus saith [[Cyrus]], the king of [[Persia]], the Lord God of heaven, hath given me all the kingdoms of the earth, and he hath charged me to build him a house at [[Jerusalem]], which is in Judah. Now, he mentions here, in order that the word of the prophecy of [[Jeremiah]], might be fulfilled. And as you turn to [[Jeremiah 25.md]], we find that the Lord had predicted that they would be in [[Exile|Babylonian captivity]] for 70 years. [[Jeremiah 25_11.md]]. And this whole land shall be a desolation and an astonishment. And these nations shall serve the king of [[Babylon]] for 70 years. And it shall come to pass, when 70 years are accomplished, that...

## Key Themes

- **Fulfillment of Prophecy**: The 70-year [[Exile|exile]] foretold by [[Jeremiah]] ([[Jeremiah 25_11-12.md]]; [[Jeremiah 29_10.md]]) sets the timeline for the [[Restoration|restoration]], demonstrating God's faithfulness to His word.
- **God's Sovereignty Over Nations**: [[Cyrus]], the pagan king of [[Persia]], is moved by God to decree the rebuilding of the [[Temple|temple]] ([[Ezra 1_1-4]]), and [[Isaiah]] prophesied his role by name 150 years beforehand ([[Isaiah 44_28-45_1.md]]).
- **The Priority of [[Temple]] Worship**: The first wave of returning [[Exile|exiles]] under [[Zerubbabel]] and [[Jeshua]] immediately rebuilds the altar and lays the foundation of the [[Temple|temple]], placing worship at the center of national life.
- **Opposition and Perseverance**: Work on the [[Temple|temple]] stalls due to opposition from [[Samaria|Samaritans]] and local officials, but the prophets [[Haggai]] and [[Zechariah]] spur the people to resume and complete the work ([[Ezra 5_1-2]]; [[Haggai 1_1-2]]; [[Zechariah 1_1-8.md]]).
- **Spiritual [[Repentance]] and Renewal**: Upon arrival, [[Ezra 8_20]] leads the people in confession and a [[Covenant|covenant]] to separate from mixed marriages ([[Ezra 9_9-10]]), highlighting the need for holiness and obedience to God's Law.
- **The Hand of God**: Repeatedly the narrative emphasizes that God's hand was upon the leaders and the people for good, enabling the [[Restoration|restoration]] against all odds.

## Connections

- **[[Nehemiah]]**: The book of [[Nehemiah]] continues the [[Restoration|restoration]] story, focusing on rebuilding Jerusalem's walls and further [[Covenant|covenant]] renewal — together with [[Ezra 8_20]], these books form a unified historical account of the post-exilic period.
- **[[Haggai]] and [[Zechariah]]**: These post-exilic prophets ministered during the period of Temple rebuilding (520-516 BC), calling the people to prioritize God's house and encouraging them with visions of future glory.
- **[[Malachi]]**: The last of the post-exilic prophets, addressing similar issues of corrupt priesthood, mixed marriages, and neglect of [[Temple|temple]] worship that [[Ezra 8_20]] confronted.
- **[[Daniel]]**: Daniel's prayer in [[Daniel 9]] and the 70-weeks prophecy are directly linked to Jeremiah's 70-year prophecy and the [[Restoration|restoration]] of Jerusalem.
- **[[Isaiah]]**: The prophecy of [[Cyrus]] by name ([[Isaiah 44_28-45_1.md]]) is one of the most remarkable messianic prophecies in the Old Testament, fulfilled in [[Ezra 8_20]].
- **[[Jeremiah]]**: Jeremiah's 70-year prophecy ([[Jeremiah 25_11-12.md]]; [[Jeremiah 29_10.md]]) provides the chronological framework for the entire [[Exile|exile]] and [[Restoration|restoration]] narrative.
- **New Testament**: The [[Temple|temple]] [[Restoration|restoration]] pointed forward to the ultimate Temple, Jesus Christ ([[John 2_19-21.md]]), and the spiritual [[Restoration|restoration]] of all who come to Him.

## Historical Context

> [!qt-verified] Historical Context

- **The Babylonian Exile (605-539 BC) and the Persian conquest of Babylon by Cyrus the Great (539 BC)**: The [[Exile|Babylonian captivity]] began with Nebuchadnezzar's siege of [[Jerusalem]], culminating in the destruction of the [[Temple|Temple]] in 586 BC. In 539 BC, [[Cyrus]] the Great conquered [[Babylon]], ushering in a new era for the exiled Judeans.
- **The Edict of Cyrus (538 BC)**: [[Cyrus]] issued a decree allowing the exiled peoples to return to their homelands and rebuild their temples. This policy of [[Restoration|repatriation]] and religious toleration is confirmed archaeologically by the **Cyrus Cylinder** (housed in the British Museum), which records [[Cyrus]]'s restoration of captured peoples and their cultic objects.
- **The three waves of return**: 
  - **[[Zerubbabel]] (538 BC, [[Ezra 1_1-6]])**: The first wave, led by [[Zerubbabel]] and [[Jeshua]], focused on rebuilding the [[Temple|Temple]].
  - **[[Ezra 8_20]] (458 BC, [[Ezra 7]])**: The second wave, led by [[Ezra 8_20]] the scribe, focused on teaching the Law and religious reform.
  - **[[Nehemiah]] (445 BC)**: The third wave, led by [[Nehemiah]], focused on rebuilding the walls of [[Jerusalem]] (recorded in the book of [[Nehemiah]]).
- **The rebuilding of the Temple (520-516 BC)**: After initial enthusiasm, work stalled due to opposition from [[Samaria|Samaritans]] and local officials. The prophets [[Haggai]] and [[Zechariah]] spurred the people to resume construction, and the [[Temple|Temple]] was completed and dedicated in 516 BC. This Samaritan-Jewish conflict over worship and legitimacy continued into Jesus' day ([[John 4_9]], [[John 4_20]]).
- **The role of the prophets Haggai and Zechariah**: [[Haggai]]'s messages (520 BC) called the people to prioritize God's house, while [[Zechariah]]'s visions encouraged them with promises of God's presence and future glory.
- **The religious reforms of Ezra**: Upon arriving in [[Jerusalem]], [[Ezra 8_20]] was horrified to learn that the people — including priests and Levites — had intermarried with foreign peoples. He led them in confession, prayer, and a [[Covenant|covenant]] to separate from these marriages ([[Ezra 9_9-10]]). He also publicly read and expounded the Law, sparking a revival ([[Nehemiah 8.md]]).
- **Persian imperial context**: Under [[Cyrus]] and his successors, the [[Persia|Persian Empire]] was organized into satrapies (provinces). Judah was part of the satrapy **"Beyond the River" (Abar-Nahara — the region west of the Euphrates)**. Official **Aramaic** served as the chancellery (administrative) language of the empire, explaining why portions of Ezra ([[Ezra 4_8-6_18]]; [[Ezra 7_12-26.md]]) are written in Aramaic.
