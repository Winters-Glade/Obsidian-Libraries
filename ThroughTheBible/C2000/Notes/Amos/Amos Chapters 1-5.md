# Amos — Chapters 1-5

## Overview

Teaching from **Amos chapters 1-5** by Pastor Chuck Smith, part of the **C2000 Series** verse-by-verse exposition of the Bible.

## Summary

Let's turn now to the book of Amos. In the first verse of Amos, he introduces himself. These are the words of Amos, who was among the herdmen, or a shepherd, at Tekoa. Now Tekoa is a little valley going down toward the Dead Sea from the area of Bethlehem. Actually, if you're standing on Herodion, that fortress that Herod built sort of east of Bethlehem, looking south, you are looking into the valley of Tekoa, the area from which Amos came. He was a herdman, and then in chapter 7, he tells us that he was a fruit picker. He picked the sycamore fruit, which was the fig. So he was not a prophet. He was not the son of a prophet. He was not from a line of ministers. He was just a common, ordinary person like all of us are. And yet, while he was there watching his sheep, God spoke to him to go up to the northern kingdom and to pray. He said, He said, He said, He said, He said, He said, He said, He said, He said, He said, He said, He said, So often we read at the beginning of a book, the word of the Lord, which came to the prophet, saying. But Amos doesn't say, this is the word of the Lord. He said, these are the words of Amos, who was among the herdmen of Tekoa, which he saw. So it puts, of course, the divine attestation upon it here, which he saw. So Amos saw these visions from the Lord. And he saw them concerning the northern kingdom of Israel.
