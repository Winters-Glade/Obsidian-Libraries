## Messianic Prophecies

This transcript covers significant Messianic prophecies including themes of the suffering servant, the reign of the coming Messiah, and the redemption of Israel. Key passages from Isaiah point to the coming of Jesus Christ and the establishment of His kingdom.

## 🏛️ Historical Context

> [!qt-verified] Historical Context

Isaiah's commissioning (chapter 6) occurred in the year King Uzziah died (c. 740 BC), marking a pivotal transition in Judah's history. The vision of God's holiness in the temple — with the seraphim crying "Holy, holy, holy" — establishes the theological foundation for the judgments and promises that follow.

During the Syro-Ephraimite War (735–734 BC), King Ahaz of Judah faced a coalition of Israel (Ephraim) and Syria (Damascus). Against Isaiah's counsel, Ahaz sought protection from Assyria rather than trusting the Lord. This period saw the famous **Immanuel prophecy** (Isaiah 7:14), the promise of a son born to a virgin, as a sign of God's presence with His people amid national crisis.

## 📜 Dead Sea Scrolls Connection

> [!qt-verified] Dead Sea Scrolls Connection

The **Great Isaiah Scroll (1QIsaᵃ)** preserves the vision of Isaiah 6 in Column 5, including the seraphim's thrice-holy declaration. Notably, 1QIsaᵃ reads "His glory fills the whole earth" with only minor orthographic differences from the Masoretic Text, confirming the stability of this pivotal passage over a millennium of transmission.

The Immanuel prophecy of Isaiah 7:14 appears in 1QIsaᵃ with the Hebrew word **עלמה** (*almah* — "young woman/virgin"), matching the Masoretic Text. When the Septuagint (Greek translation, c. 200 BC) translated this word as **παρθένος** (*parthenos* — "virgin"), it provided the textual basis for Matthew's citation (Matthew 1:23) in the virgin birth narrative.

The scroll is housed at the **Shrine of the Book**, Israel Museum, Jerusalem.

## ✝️ New Testament Fulfillment

**Isaiah 6:1–10** — The call of Isaiah is foundational for the New Testament. Jesus quotes Isaiah 6:9–10 (the hardening prophecy) in **Matthew 13:14–15** and **Mark 4:12** to explain why He teaches in parables. Paul cites the same passage in **Acts 28:26–27** regarding Israel's rejection of the gospel. John 12:39–41 explicitly states that Isaiah "saw His glory and spoke of Him," identifying the vision of the enthroned Lord with the pre-incarnate Christ.

**Isaiah 7:14** — The virgin birth prophecy is directly cited in **Matthew 1:22–23** as the fulfillment of Immanuel — "God with us."

**Isaiah 9:1–7** — The promise of a great light in Galilee is cited in **Matthew 4:14–16** as fulfilled in Jesus' ministry. The titles "Wonderful Counselor, Mighty God, Everlasting Father, Prince of Peace" (9:6) describe the Messiah's divine nature and eternal reign, echoed in **Luke 1:32–33**.
