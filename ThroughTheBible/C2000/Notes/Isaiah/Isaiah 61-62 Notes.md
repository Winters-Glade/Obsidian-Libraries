## Summary

Shall we turn in our Bibles to Isaiah 61? We are told in the New Testament that the Old Testament prophets many times wrote of things that they did not really understand. Earnestly desiring really to look into these things, but they wrote as the Spirit of God inspired them. And so we find that quite often the Old Testament prophets did not clearly understand the work of God in creating the body of Christ, the church, from among the Gentiles.

## 🏛️ Historical Context

> [!qt-verified] Historical Context

Isaiah 61–62 proclaims the year of the Lord's favor — the **Jubilee** — and the restoration of Zion. The Spirit of the Lord upon the Anointed One (61:1) brings good news to the poor, freedom to captives, and comfort to mourners. Chapter 62 depicts the Lord's passionate commitment to Zion: "For Zion's sake I will not keep silent" until her righteousness shines like the dawn.

## 📜 Dead Sea Scrolls Connection

> [!qt-verified] Dead Sea Scrolls Connection

The **Great Isaiah Scroll (1QIsaᵃ)** — one of the seven original Dead Sea Scrolls discovered at Qumran in 1947 — contains the complete Book of Isaiah and dates to approximately **125 BCE**, making it roughly **1,000 years older** than the oldest previously known Masoretic Hebrew manuscripts. It is preserved almost in its entirety across 54 columns on 17 sheets of parchment, measuring over 24 feet in length.

### Textual Significance

When compared to the Masoretic Text (MT), 1QIsaᵃ contains **more than 2,600 textual variants**, though the overall order and content remain consistent. A 2021 AI-assisted paleographic analysis by the University of Groningen determined that **two scribes** copied the scroll, with the division occurring at column 27 — precisely at the boundary scholars often mark between Proto-Isaiah (chapters 1–39) and Deutero-Isaiah (chapters 40–66).

The scroll is now housed at the **Shrine of the Book**, Israel Museum, Jerusalem, and has been fully digitized in 1,200 megapixel resolution.

## ✝️ New Testament Fulfillment

**Isaiah 61:1–3** — Jesus reads this passage in the synagogue at Nazareth and declares it fulfilled in Himself (**Luke 4:17–21**). In doing so, He identifies Himself as the Anointed One (Messiah) and announces the dawn of the Messianic Age. The "year of the Lord's favor" (Jubilee) becomes a picture of the gospel age of salvation.

The phrase "garment of praise for a spirit of despair" (61:3) finds its ultimate fulfillment in the transformation Christ brings — exchanging our ashes for beauty (see **Revelation 21:4**).
