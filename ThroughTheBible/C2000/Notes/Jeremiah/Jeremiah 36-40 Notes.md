## 🏛️ Historical Context

> [!qt-verified] Historical Context

Jeremiah 36 records the dramatic episode where **Baruch**, Jeremiah's scribe, read the scroll of Jeremiah's prophecies in the temple, and later King **Jehoiakim** cut the scroll into pieces and burned it in a brazier. Jeremiah dictated a second scroll with even more judgments.

Chapters 37–39 recount the fall of Jerusalem in **586 BC**. During the final siege, Jeremiah was arrested as a deserter, imprisoned, then rescued from a cistern by **Ebed-Melech** the Ethiopian eunuch — a Gentile who showed more faith and courage than Judah's leaders. The Babylonian capture of the city, the blinding of **Zedekiah**, the execution of his sons, and the burning of the Temple and palaces occurred in the summer of 586 BC.

### The Lachish Letters
The **Lachish Letters** (discovered 1935 at Tel Lachish) provide stunning archaeological corroboration of this period. Written in paleo-Hebrew ink on clay sherds, they are military dispatches from the final months before Jerusalem fell. **Letter 4** states: *"We are watching for the fire signals of Lachish according to all the signs which my lord has given, because we cannot see Azeqah"* — directly confirming **Jeremiah 34:7** that only Lachish and Azekah remained as the last strongholds.
