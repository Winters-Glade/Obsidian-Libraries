## Summary

God says they're my sheep, but these pastors are scattering them and destroying them. Therefore, thus saith the Lord God of Israel against the pastors that feed my people. ## 🏛️ Historical Context

> [!qt-verified] Historical Context

Jeremiah 23 contains the "**Righteous Branch**" prophecy (23:5–6) — a Messianic promise of a Davidic king who will reign wisely and bring salvation: "This is the name by which He will be called: The Lord Our Righteousness." This oracle stands in contrast to the failures of Judah's kings and the lies of false prophets.

Chapter 25 prophesies **70 years of Babylonian captivity** (25:11–12), a specific timeline that Daniel would later study (Daniel 9:2) and that would be fulfilled from 605–539 BC (the first deportation to Cyrus's decree). The "cup of the wine of wrath" (25:15–38) — God's judgment poured out on all nations — is an image that reappears in **Revelation 14:10, 16:19, and 18:6**.

## ✝️ New Testament Fulfillment

**Jeremiah 23:5–6** — "The Lord Our Righteousness" is a Messianic title pointing to Jesus Christ, whom Paul describes as "our righteousness" (**1 Corinthians 1:30**). The New Testament presents Christ as the righteous Davidic King whose reign brings salvation, fulfilling Jeremiah's prophecy. **Revelation 19:11** depicts the returning Christ as the King who "judges and makes war in righteousness."
