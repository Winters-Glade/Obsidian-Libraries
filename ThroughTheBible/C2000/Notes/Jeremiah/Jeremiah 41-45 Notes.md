## 🏛️ Historical Context

> [!qt-verified] Historical Context

Jeremiah 41–45 recounts the tragic aftermath of Jerusalem's fall. **Gedaliah**, appointed governor by Nebuchadnezzar, was assassinated by **Ishmael** (a royal prince), triggering a panic. Against Jeremiah's explicit warning from the Lord, the remnant fled to **Egypt** under **Johanan**, taking Jeremiah and Baruch with them against their will.

In Egypt, Jeremiah continued his prophetic ministry, predicting Nebuchadnezzar's invasion of Egypt itself. Chapter 45 contains a personal word of hope for **Baruch**, who was discouraged: "I will give you your life as a prize of war in all places where you go" — a promise that God preserves and cares for individual faithfulness even amid national catastrophe.
