## Summary

So we turn to Jeremiah, the 11th chapter, as we continue our study through the book of Jeremiah. Chapter 11 begins a new section of the book, not a whole new division, but it's a new message. And Jeremiah is divided into many messages that the Lord gave to him. And with chapter 10, we concluded the message of the Lord to Jeremiah that he was to give at the temple gate. Now he is to give a message to the people concerning their broken covenant with God.

## 🏛️ Historical Context

> [!qt-verified] Historical Context

Jeremiah 11–15 includes the "covenant lawsuit" (11:1–17), Jeremiah's personal complaints or "confessions" (11:18–12:6, 15:10–21), the prophecy of the ruined linen belt (13:1–11), the wine jugs of wrath (13:12–14), and the severe drought that struck Judah (14:1–6). These chapters highlight the personal cost of Jeremiah's ministry — his own townsmen in Anathoth plotted against his life (11:21), and he lamented his very birth (15:10).
