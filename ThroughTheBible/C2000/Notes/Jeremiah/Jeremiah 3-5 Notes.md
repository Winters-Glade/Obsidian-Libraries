## 🏛️ Historical Context

> [!qt-verified] Historical Context

Jeremiah 3–5 contains the prophet's call to **repentance** using the powerful metaphor of Israel as a faithless wife who had "played the harlot with many lovers" (3:1). The prophet foresees a "coming disaster from the north" (4:6) — the Babylonian invasion under Nebuchadnezzar.

The reign of **Josiah** (640–609 BC) saw religious reforms and the rediscovery of the Book of the Law in the Temple (622 BC, 2 Kings 22–23). Yet Jeremiah's message indicates that these reforms, while genuine, did not penetrate deeply enough into the nation's heart — a diagnosis of superficial religion that echoes the prophets before him.
