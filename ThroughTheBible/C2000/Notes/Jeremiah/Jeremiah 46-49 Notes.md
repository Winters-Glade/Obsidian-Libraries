## Summary

Let's turn now in our Bibles to Jeremiah 46. Beginning with chapter 45, we came into the sixth part or section of the book of Jeremiah. And this sixth section is comprised of miscellaneous prophecies that are directed mainly to those nations that were around Israel. And so as we get into chapter 46, we find the introduction to these series of prophecies as he addresses them to the Gentiles, the Gentile kingdoms.

## 🏛️ Historical Context

> [!qt-verified] Historical Context

Chapters 46–49 form the **oracles against the nations** — a section paralleled in Isaiah (13–23), Ezekiel (25–32), and Amos (1–2). Jeremiah pronounces judgment on:

- **Egypt** (46) — the Battle of Carchemish (605 BC), recorded in both Jeremiah and the **Babylonian Chronicles** (BM 21946), where Nebuchadnezzar defeated Pharaoh Necho and Egyptian power was broken
- **Philistia** (47)
- **Moab** (48) — the longest oracle, reflecting the longstanding hostility between Moab and Israel
- **Ammon** (49:1–6)
- **Edom** (49:7–22) — also the subject of Obadiah's entire book
- **Damascus** (Syria, 49:23–27)
- **Kedar and Hazor** (Arab tribes, 49:28–33)
- **Elam** (49:34–39)

These oracles demonstrate God's sovereignty over all nations, not just Israel, and His judgment on pride, cruelty, and idolatry wherever it appears.
