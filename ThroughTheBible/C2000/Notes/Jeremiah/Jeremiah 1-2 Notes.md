## Summary

At this time, shall we turn in our Bibles to the book of Jeremiah. About 60 years after Isaiah died, God called Jeremiah to what I feel must have been the hardest task any minister has ever been called upon to perform for the Lord. Jeremiah had to oversee the death of the nation. He had to watch it as it was in its final death throes, as it went into convulsions and died and was carried away captive to Babylon. His ministry was destined from the beginning for failure.

## 🏛️ Historical Context

> [!qt-verified] Historical Context

Jeremiah began his ministry in approximately **627 BC** (the 13th year of Josiah's reign, Jeremiah 1:2) — about 60 years after Isaiah's death, as the summary notes. This placed him in the final decades of the **Kingdom of Judah**, as the **Neo-Assyrian Empire** was declining and the **Neo-Babylonian Empire** under Nabopolassar (626–605 BC) was rising to power.

The "weeping prophet" was called to deliver a message of judgment to a nation in its death throes. His commission (chapter 1) — "to uproot and tear down, to destroy and overthrow, to build and to plant" — set the tone for a ministry of confrontation with a people committed to idolatry and political alliances that betrayed their covenant with God.
