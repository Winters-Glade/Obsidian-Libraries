## 🏛️ Historical Context

> [!qt-verified] Historical Context

Jeremiah 26 recounts the Temple sermon that nearly cost Jeremiah his life. The priests and prophets demanded his death, but he was spared through the intervention of certain elders who cited the precedent of **Micah** (Micah 3:12, also cited in Jeremiah 26:18). **Uriah** the prophet, who prophesied similarly, was not so fortunate — he was executed by Jehoiakim (26:20–23).

Chapter 27 records Jeremiah's dramatic act of wearing a yoke of straps and bars as a symbol that all nations must submit to **Nebuchadnezzar**. The yoke motif symbolized the inevitability of Babylonian domination as divine judgment.
