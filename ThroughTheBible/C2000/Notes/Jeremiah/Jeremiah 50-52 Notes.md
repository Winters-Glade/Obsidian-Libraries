## 🏛️ Historical Context

> [!qt-verified] Historical Context

Jeremiah 50–51 contain a lengthy oracle against **Babylon** itself — the superpower that God had used as His instrument of judgment but would now judge for its own pride and brutality. The prophecy that Babylon "will never again be inhabited" (50:39–40, 51:62) was fulfilled so completely that by the early centuries AD, Babylon was largely abandoned. When Saddam Hussein attempted to rebuild the city in the 1980s, the project remained unfinished.

Chapter 52 serves as a historical appendix, recounting the fall of Jerusalem in 586 BC, the fate of Zedekiah, and the release of **Jehoiachin** from prison in Babylon by Evil-Merodach (Amel-Marduk) in 561 BC. This note of grace — a captive king eating at the king's table — ends Jeremiah on a faint note of hope, pointing toward the restoration yet to come.
