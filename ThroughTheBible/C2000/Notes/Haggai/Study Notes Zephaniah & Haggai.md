## Historical Context
These prophecies date to the [[Post-Exilic Period]] after the return from Babylon (c. 520-430 BC). Haggai and Zechariah encouraged the rebuilding of the temple; Malachi addressed spiritual decline a generation later.

## Overview
This teaching from [[Pastor Chuck Smiths]] [[_HOME|C2000 Series]] covers Zephaniah & Haggai, chapters 1-3.

**Zephaniah:** Zephaniah warns of the coming ''Day of the Lord'' - a day of wrath, trouble, and distress that will come upon Judah and all nations. But the book ends with a message of hope: God will gather a humble remnant and rejoice over them with singing. Zephaniah's prophecy of the international scope of God's judgment and salvation is striking.

**Haggai:** Haggai's ministry spans just four months in 520 BC, during the post-exilic period. He challenges the returned exiles to prioritize rebuilding the temple over their own paneled houses. His message: ''Consider your ways!'' The glory of the latter house will be greater than the former, and God's blessing will return when His house is rebuilt.
