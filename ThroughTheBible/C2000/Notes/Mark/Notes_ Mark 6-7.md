## Historical & Cultural Context

> [!NOTE] QT Verification
> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### Rejection at [[Nazareth]]
Jesus' rejection in his hometown (Mark 6:1-6) — [[Nazareth]] — highlights the difficulty of a prophet being accepted among those who knew him as a child. Archaeological excavations of first-century [[Nazareth]] show a small, conservative Jewish village of perhaps 200–400 people, with rock-cut tombs, wine presses, and terraced agriculture. The population would have known Jesus and his family well.

### The Mission of the Twelve
The sending of the Twelve (Mark 6:7-13) with instructions to take only a staff, sandals, and one tunic (contrast Matthew 10:10, where they are told to take no staff) reflects the urgency and itinerant nature of their mission. The act of shaking off dust (Mark 6:11) was a Jewish practice of dissociation from Gentile impurity.

### Death of [[John the Baptist]]
The execution of [[John the Baptist]] (Mark 6:14-29) is described in a flashback during [[Herod the Great.md]]' guilty reflection on Jesus. [[Herodians.md]]' daughter is named [[Absalom.md]] by the first-century historian Josephus. The fortress of [[Zacchaeus.md]] (modern Jordan) was the site of John's imprisonment and execution, confirmed by archaeological excavations revealing a palace complex with a dungeon.

### Feeding the 5000 and Walking on Water
The feeding of the 5,000 (Mark 6:30-44) and walking on water (Mark 6:45-56) are the only miracles recorded in all four Gospels (cf. [[Matthew 14_13-33.md]], [[Luke 9_10-17.md]], [[John 6_1-21]]). Mark's version emphasizes the disciples' lack of understanding (Mark 6:52: "their hearts were hardened").

### Tradition vs. Commandment
The controversy about hand-washing (Mark 7:1-23) is explained for Mark's Gentile audience: "For the Pharisees and all the Jews do not eat unless they wash their hands properly, holding to the tradition of the elders" (Mark 7:3-4). This was part of the *Oral Law*, not the written [[Torah]]. Jesus quotes [[Isaiah 29_13.md]] and declares all foods clean (Mark 7:19), a critical step toward the apostolic decision at the [[Council of Jerusalem]] ([[Acts 15]]).
