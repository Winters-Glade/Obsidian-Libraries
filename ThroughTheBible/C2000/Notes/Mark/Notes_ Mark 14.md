## Historical & Cultural Context

> [!NOTE] QT Verification
> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### The Plot Against Jesus
The conspiracy of the chief priests and scribes (Mark 14:1-2) occurred during the [[Passover]] season, a politically volatile time when [[Jerusalem]]'s population swelled from perhaps 40,000 to over 200,000. The decision to wait "not during the feast" shows their fear of popular uprising.

### The Anointing at Bethany
The anointing at [[Bethany]] (Mark 14:3-9) uses expensive [[spikenard|ointment of spikenard]] — a fragrant oil imported from India, worth 300 denarii (a year's wages for a laborer). The woman's act of breaking the alabaster jar signifies complete, unrepeatable devotion. Jesus interprets this as preparation for his burial.

### The Last Supper
The [[Last Supper]] (Mark 14:12-26) was a Passover Seder, likely held on Thursday evening (14 Nisan). The Upper Room was a *cenaculum* — a large upper room typical of wealthy [[Jerusalem]] homes, possibly the home of [[Mary (mother of John Mark)|Mary, mother of John Mark]] (cf. [[Acts 12_12]]). The betrayal by [[Judas Iscariot]] and the prediction of Peter's denial frame the institution of the Lord's Supper, emphasizing human failure alongside divine institution.

### [[Gethsemane]]
[[Gethsemane]] (Mark 14:32-42, from Aramaic "oil press") was located on the lower slopes of the [[Mount of Olives]]. The site today contains ancient olive trees; dendrochronological studies suggest some may be over 900 years old, possibly offshoots of the original trees. Jesus' prayer — "Abba, Father" (Mark 14:36) — uses the Aramaic intimate term for father, unique in Jewish prayer.

### The Arrest and Trial
The arrest (Mark 14:43-52) includes the detail of a young man fleeing "naked" (Mark 14:51-52) — possibly a self-reference by Mark (the "young man" tradition). The trial before the [[Sanhedrin]] (Mark 14:53-65) violates several Mishnaic legal procedures: capital cases were not to be tried at night, nor on the eve of a Sabbath or festival, nor could a verdict of condemnation be reached on the same day as the trial. Peter's denial (Mark 14:66-72) occurs in the courtyard of the high priest.
