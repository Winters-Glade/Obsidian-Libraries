## Historical & Cultural Context

> [!NOTE] QT Verification
> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### Mark's Gospel — The Earliest Account
[[Mark]] is widely considered by scholars to be the earliest Gospel written (c. AD 65–70), and a primary source for Matthew and Luke ([[Mark]] in the [[_transcripts/The Problem Defined, Mark 14_35|Two-Source Hypothesis]]). Mark's Gospel is characterized by its fast-paced, action-oriented narrative — the Greek word *eutheōs* ("immediately") appears over 40 times. Mark's audience was likely Gentile Christians in Rome, which explains his frequent explanations of Jewish customs.

### [[John the Baptist]]
John's ministry in the wilderness (Mark 1:2-8) fulfills [[Isaiah 40_3]] ("Prepare the way of the Lord, make his paths straight"). The [[Dead Sea Scrolls]] show that the [[Jesse.md]] community at [[Qumran]] also applied this Isaiah passage to their own wilderness retreat, interpreting the "way of the Lord" as preparation for the eschaton. John's baptism of repentance echoes Jewish proselyte baptism and the ritual washings practiced at Qumran.

### Baptism and Temptation
Jesus' baptism and the descent of the Spirit (Mark 1:9-11) echoes the creation account ([[Genesis 1_2]], the Spirit hovering over the waters) and the calling of the prophets ([[Isaiah 42_1]], "my servant whom I uphold"). The temptation (Mark 1:12-13) makes Jesus the new Adam in a new Eden, "with the wild beasts." Only Mark mentions the presence of wild beasts.

### Calling of the Disciples
The call of [[Peter|Simon]], [[Andrew]], [[Zebedee|James]], and [[John the Apostle|John]] (Mark 1:16-20) reflects the fishing industry on the [[Sea of Galilee]]. Recent archaeological excavations at [[Magdala]] (the hometown of [[Mary Magdalene]]) have uncovered a first-century fishing harbor, fish-processing facilities, and a synagogue with a carved stone table depicting the [[Second Temple]].

### [[Capernaum]] Ministry
[[Capernaum]] (Mark 1:21-39) served as Jesus' Galilean base. Its synagogue (excavated by archaeologists) was a large limestone structure built over an earlier basalt foundation dating to the first century. Peter's house, also excavated, was a simple insula-style dwelling that was venerated by early Christians, as evidenced by graffiti including crosses and the name "Peter."
