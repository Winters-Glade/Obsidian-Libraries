## Historical & Cultural Context

> [!NOTE] QT Verification
> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### The Trial Before Pilate
Mark's portrayal of [[Pontius Pilate]] (Mark 15:1-15) shows him as a weak governor who yields to the crowd. The title "King of the Jews" — the charge against Jesus — was the political crime of sedition against Rome. The release of [[Barabbas]] (whose name means "son of the father" in Aramaic) creates an ironic contrast: the guilty "son of the father" is released, while the innocent Son of the Father is condemned.

### The Crucifixion
Jesus' crucifixion at 9 AM (or about 9 AM — the "third hour" by Jewish reckoning, Mark 15:25) on the site of [[Golgotha]] (Mark 15:22, "Place of a Skull"). The [[Spirit of Adoption]] in [[Jerusalem]] marks the traditional site, with archaeological evidence of a first-century tomb and a rock escarpment that could be [[Golgotha]]. The offering of wine mixed with [[myrrh]] (Mark 15:23) was a traditional narcotic to dull pain, which Jesus refused.

### The Death of Jesus
The darkness from the "sixth hour to the ninth hour" (noon to 3 PM, Mark 15:33) may reflect a solar eclipse or the *khamsin* (desert storm) phenomenon. Jesus' cry — "Eloi, Eloi, lema sabachthani?" (Mark 15:34, quoting [[Psalm 22_1]]) — is the only saying of Jesus from the cross recorded by Mark. The tearing of the Temple veil (Mark 15:38) symbolizes the opening of access to God's presence.

### The Burial
[[Joseph of Arimathea]] (Mark 15:42-47), a respected member of the Council (Sanhedrin), boldly requests Jesus' body. The quick burial before sundown on Friday was necessary because of the approaching Sabbath (Friday evening to Saturday evening). The tomb was a rock-hewn tomb with a rolling stone.

### The Resurrection
The discovery of the empty tomb by [[Mary Magdalene]], [[Mary (Mother of Jesus).md]], and [[Absalom.md]] (Mark 16:1-8) — three women who had witnessed the crucifixion — is the earliest resurrection account. The "young man in a white robe" at the tomb (an angelic figure) delivers the message: "He has risen; he is not here." The women's fear and silence (Mark 16:8) is the original ending of Mark's Gospel. The longer ending (Mark 16:9-20) was added later by scribes, as the earliest Greek manuscripts do not contain it.
