## Historical & Cultural Context

> [!NOTE] QT Verification
> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### The Second Feeding (4000)
The feeding of the 4,000 (Mark 8:1-10), preceded by the healing of the Syrophoenician woman's daughter, likely occurs in the Decapolis (Gentile territory). Some scholars see this as a "Gentile feeding" parallel to the "Jewish feeding" of the 5,000 in Jewish [[Galilee]]. The numbers and details differ: 7 loaves, 7 baskets of fragments — perhaps symbolizing completeness for the Gentiles.

### Peter's Confession
Peter's confession at [[Caesarea Philippi]] (Mark 8:27-30) marks the central turning point of Mark's Gospel. Jesus asks, "Who do you say that I am?" — and Peter answers, "You are the Christ." Jesus then immediately speaks of his suffering and rebukes Peter when he protests (Mark 8:31-33). This is the first explicit prediction of the passion in Mark.

### The Transfiguration
The [[Transfiguration]] (Mark 9:2-13) may have occurred on [[Mount Hor.md]] (near [[Caesarea Philippi]]) or [[Mount Tabor]]. The presence of [[Moses]] and [[Elijah]] represents the Law and the Prophets. Peter's suggestion to build three tabernacles (*sukkot*) reflects the Jewish Feast of Tabernacles. The cloud and voice echo the baptism (Mark 1:11). The conversation about [[Elijah]] coming first (Mark 9:11-13) references [[Malachi 4_5-6.md]], interpreted by Jesus as fulfilled in [[John the Baptist]].

### The Epileptic Boy
The healing of the epileptic boy (Mark 9:14-29) contrasts the disciples' inability with the father's desperate faith: "I believe; help my unbelief!" (Mark 9:24). Jesus' statement that "this kind cannot be driven out by anything but prayer" (Mark 9:29 — some manuscripts add "and fasting") reflects the need for spiritual preparation.
