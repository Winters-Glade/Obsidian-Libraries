# Micah — Chapters 1-7

## Overview

Teaching from **Micah chapters 1-7** by Pastor Chuck Smith, part of the **C2000 Series** verse-by-verse exposition of the Bible.

## Summary

So we turn now to the book of Micah, as is the very typical opening of most of the books of the prophets. The word of the Lord that came to Micah, the Moreshite, in the days of Jotham, Ahaz, and Hezekiah, the kings of Judah, which he saw concerning Samaria and Jerusalem. So he introduces himself, Micah, the city from which he hailed, Moreshite. The time of his prophecy, it is about the same time that Isaiah was prophesying. He was a contemporary to Isaiah. As we go back in the history of second kings, and we look at Ahaz, he was one of the bad kings of the southern kingdom of Judah. And he did not that which was right in the sight of the Lord. But he established high places for the worship of the false gods in Jerusalem. Hezekiah was a good king, instituted reforms when he came to the throne. Now prophesying during this period of time would mean that he was prophesying during the time that the northern kingdom, that the northern kingdom of Israel fell to the Assyrians. The Assyrians, having conquered the northern kingdom, then invaded the southern kingdom at the time of Hezekiah. And of course, were defeated by the work of the Lord, the intervention of God. So the time that Samaria and the northern kingdom of Israel fell, so his prophecy is against Samaria and also against Jerusalem.
