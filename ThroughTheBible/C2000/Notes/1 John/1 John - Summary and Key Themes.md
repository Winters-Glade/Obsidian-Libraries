## Historical & Contextual Background

> [!QUESTION]- This enrichment needs QT verification

**The Three Witnesses:** The "water, blood, and Spirit" (5:6–8) likely refer to Jesus's baptism (water), crucifixion (blood), and the Spirit given at Pentecost. The author argues against Docetic teachers who separated the divine Christ from the human Jesus — insisting the same Jesus who was baptized also died and was vindicated by the Spirit. The later Johannine Comma (1 John 5:7–8, King James Version) adding the "three that bear record in heaven" (Father, Word, and Holy Spirit) is a late Latin interpolation absent from all early Greek manuscripts.

**Assurance of Salvation:** 1 John 5:13 states the letter's purpose: "that you may know that you have eternal life." This is written to believers (unlike John's Gospel, written for unbelievers — John 20:31). The assurance rests on three tests: right belief (confessing Christ), right behavior (keeping His commandments), and right relationships (loving the brethren).

**Sin unto Death:** The "sin leading to death" (5:16–17) is debated: it likely refers to the apostasy of the secessionists who denied the Son (cf. Hebrews 6:4–6; 10:26), which places one beyond the community's intercessory prayer.

**The True God and Eternal Life:** The closing statement — "keep yourselves from idols" (5:21) — is surprising after the majestic conclusion. In a city like Ephesus (home of the Temple of Artemis), the practical danger of idolatry was ever-present for new believers from pagan backgrounds.
