## Historical & Cultural Context

> [!NOTE] QT Verification
> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### Miracles in Synoptic Context
Matthew 8–9 presents a concentrated collection of ten miracles, demonstrating Jesus' authority over disease, nature, demons, and death. This structure mirrors the pattern established in the [[Sermon on the Mount]]: as Jesus taught with authority, so he acts with authority. In [[Mark]], these same miracles are spread across a longer narrative (Mark 1–5).

### Healing and Jewish Purity Laws
The healing of the leper (Matthew 8:1-4) directly involves the purity system of [[Leviticus 14.md]]. Leprosy (*tzara'at*) rendered a person ceremonially unclean and socially isolated. Jesus' command to "show yourself to the priest and offer the gift that Moses commanded" demonstrates his respect for the [[Torah]] while exercising authority over the laws of purity.

### The Centurion's Faith
The centurion (Matthew 8:5-13) was likely a Roman officer stationed in [[Capernaum]], a garrison town on the border of [[Herod Agrippa.md]]' territory. [[Capernaum]] served as a customs post on the Via Maris trade route. The centurion's understanding of authority (commanding soldiers under him) reflects the Roman military chain of command. His faith is remarkable for a Gentile — Jesus declares it greater than any in Israel.

### The Gadarene Demoniac
The region of the Gadarenes (Matthew 8:28-34) was in the Decapolis, a predominantly Gentile area east of the [[Sea of Galilee]]. The presence of a herd of swine (unclean animals for Jews) confirms the Gentile character of the region. Archaeological excavations at [[Tel Haror.md]] and [[Kursi]] have identified Byzantine churches commemorating this event.

### Jairus and the Hemorrhaging Woman
The story of Jairus' daughter and the woman with the issue of blood (Matthew 9:18-26) involves two instances of Jewish purity concerns. A woman with a discharge of blood was considered *niddah* — in a state of ritual impurity that could be transmitted by touch. Jesus' willingness to be touched and to touch the dead demonstrates his authority over the purity system.
