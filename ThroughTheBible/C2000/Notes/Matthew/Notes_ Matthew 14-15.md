## Historical & Cultural Context

> [!NOTE] QT Verification
> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### [[Herod Agrippa.md]] and [[John the Baptist]]
The death of [[John the Baptist]] (Matthew 14:1-12) is recounted in a flashback explaining [[Herod Agrippa.md]]' guilty conscience. Josephus (*Antiquities* 18.5.2) confirms that Herod imprisoned and executed John at [[Zacchaeus.md]] fortress because he feared John's popularity and his preaching about Herod's unlawful marriage to [[Herodians.md]], his brother Philip's wife. This marriage violated [[Leviticus 18_16.md]] and [[Leviticus 20_21.md]].

### Feeding the 5000
The feeding of the 5,000 (Matthew 14:13-21) is the only miracle recorded in all four Gospels ([[Mark 6_30-44.md]], [[Luke 9_10-17.md]], [[John 6_1-14]]). The location was near [[Bethsaida]] on the northeastern shore of the [[Sea of Galilee]]. The five loaves and two fish (barley loaves and pickled fish, the food of the poor) echo the manna in the wilderness ([[Exodus 16.md]]) and [[Elisha]]'s feeding of 100 men with 20 loaves ([[2 Kings 4_42-44.md]]).

### Walking on Water
Jesus walking on the water (Matthew 14:22-33) echoes divine power over the sea in the Hebrew Bible — [[Job 9_8]] speaks of God "trampling the waves of the sea," and [[Psalm 77_19]] describes God's path through the waters.

### Tradition of the Elders
The controversy over hand-washing (Matthew 15:1-20) concerns the "tradition of the elders" (the *Oral Law*), which later became codified in the [[Mishnah]]. The biblical law only required priests to wash before temple service ([[Exodus 30_18-21.md]]), but Pharisaic tradition had extended this to all Jews as a matter of purity. Jesus quotes [[Isaiah 29_13.md]] to distinguish human tradition from divine commandment.

### The Syrophoenician Woman
The encounter with the [[The woman with the issue of blood|Canaanite woman]] (Matthew 15:21-28) takes place in the region of [[Tyre]], Gentile territory in Phoenicia. Her faith is praised despite Jesus' initial testing response about "bread for the children."
