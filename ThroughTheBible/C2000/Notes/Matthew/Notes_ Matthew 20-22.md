## Historical & Cultural Context

> [!NOTE] QT Verification
> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### The Triumphal Entry
Jesus' entry into [[Jerusalem]] (Matthew 21:1-11) during the Passover season was a deliberate enactment of [[Zechariah 9_9.md]] ("Behold, your king is coming to you, humble and mounted on a donkey"). Pilgrims flooded [[Jerusalem]] for the feast, and Jesus' entry from the [[Mount of Olives]] along the route through the [[Kidron Valley]] would have been witnessed by thousands. The crowds shouting "Hosanna" (Hebrew *hoshia na* — "save now") were quoting [[Psalm 118_25-26.md]], a Psalm associated with the feast.

### Cleansing the Temple
The cleansing of the Temple (Matthew 21:12-17) was a dramatic protest against the commercialization of the worship system. The money changers converted various currencies into the Tyrian shekel (the only coin accepted for the temple tax), and animal sellers provided sacrificial victims. Jesus' action targeted the Court of the Gentiles, the only area where non-Jews could pray, making his citation of [[Isaiah 56_7]] ("a house of prayer for all nations") particularly pointed.

### Parables of Judgment
The parables of the two sons, the wicked tenants, and the wedding banquet (Matthew 21:28–22:14) all address Israel's rejection of God's messengers. The parable of the tenants echoes [[Isaiah 5_1-7.md]] (the Song of the Vineyard). The stone quotation (Matthew 21:42) from [[Psalm 118_22-23.md]] — "The stone the builders rejected has become the cornerstone" — was an early Christian proof text for Jesus' resurrection and exaltation.

### The Greatest Commandment
The question about the greatest commandment (Matthew 22:34-40) was a common rabbinic debate topic. Jesus' answer combines [[Deuteronomy 6_5.md]] (the [[Shema]]) with [[Leviticus 19_18.md]] (love your neighbor). The Shema — "Hear, O Israel, the Lord our God, the Lord is One" — was recited daily by observant Jews.
