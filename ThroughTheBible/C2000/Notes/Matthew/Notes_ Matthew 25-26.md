## Historical & Cultural Context

> [!NOTE] QT Verification
> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### Parables of Readiness
Matthew 25 contains three parables about readiness for the kingdom: the ten virgins, the talents, and the sheep and goats. The parable of the ten virgins reflects Jewish wedding customs: the bridegroom would come to the bride's house for the wedding procession, and the bridal party would go out with lamps to meet him. The delay of the bridegroom addresses the early church's struggle with the delayed [[Second Coming|Parousia]].

### The Plot Against Jesus
Matthew 26 begins with the religious leaders plotting Jesus' death. [[Caiaphas]] the high priest (AD 18–36) was appointed by the Roman prefect [[Aratus]] and served until his removal by [[Vitellius]]. The [[Sanhedrin]] was the supreme Jewish council, composed of chief priests, elders, and scribes. The anointing at [[Bethany]] (Matthew 26:6-13) uses spikenard — a costly imported ointment from India, worth about 300 denarii (a year's wages, per [[John 12_5]]).

### The Last Supper
The [[Last Supper]] (Matthew 26:17-30) was a [[Passover]] Seder meal. The Passover commemorated Israel's deliverance from Egypt ([[Exodus 12.md]]). Jesus' words over the bread and wine reframe the Passover symbols around his own body and blood, establishing the new covenant ([[_Bible/Jeremiah/Jeremiah 31_31-34.md]]). The "cup of the covenant" echoes the covenant ratification at Sinai ([[Exodus 24_8]]).

### [[Gethsemane]] and Arrest
[[Gethsemane]] (from Aramaic *Gat Shemanim* — "oil press") was a garden or olive grove on the [[Mount of Olives]]. The agony of Jesus reflects genuine human struggle with impending death. Judas' betrayal with a kiss — the traditional greeting of a disciple to a rabbi — adds to the poignancy. The arrest and trial before [[Caiaphas]] illustrates the irregular procedures of the night trial (Jewish law forbade capital trials at night).
