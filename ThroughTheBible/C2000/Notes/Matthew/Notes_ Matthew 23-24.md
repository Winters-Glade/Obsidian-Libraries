## Historical & Cultural Context

> [!NOTE] QT Verification
> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### Woes to the Pharisees
Matthew 23 is Jesus' most scathing denunciation of the [[Pharisees]] and [[Scribes|scribes]]. The seven "woes" echo prophetic oracles of judgment (cf. [[Isaiah 5_8-23.md]]). The Pharisees' practice of tithing "mint and dill and cumin" (Matthew 23:23) reflects their meticulous interpretation of the law beyond what was required (the "fence around the Torah"). The whitewashed tombs analogy (Matthew 23:27) was especially vivid in a culture where contact with tombs caused ritual impurity.

### The Olivet Discourse
The [[curse]] (Matthew 24) takes place on the [[Mount of Olives]], overlooking the [[Temple (Jerusalem)|Temple Mount]]. Jesus' prediction that "not one stone here will be left upon another" was literally fulfilled in AD 70 when the Roman army under [[Titus]] destroyed [[Jerusalem]] and the Temple. The siege was so severe that Josephus records over a million deaths. Archaeology confirms the extensive destruction — the [[Titus]] in Rome still depicts Roman soldiers carrying away Temple treasures.

### The Abomination of Desolation
The "abomination of desolation" (Matthew 24:15) references [[Daniel 9_27]], [[Daniel 11_31.md]], and [[Daniel 12_11.md]], originally referring to Antiochus Epiphanes' desecration of the Temple in 167 BC. Jesus reapplies this prophecy to a future event, possibly the Roman siege or an eschatological fulfillment.

### Synoptic Parallels
Parallels to the Olivet Discourse appear in [[Mark 13]] and [[Luke 21_5-36.md]]. Luke's version specifies that [[Jerusalem]] will be "surrounded by armies" (Luke 21:20), making the reference to AD 70 explicit. Matthew's version preserves a more apocalyptic focus.
