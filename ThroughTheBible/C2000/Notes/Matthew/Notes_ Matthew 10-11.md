## Historical & Cultural Context

> [!NOTE] QT Verification
> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### The Mission of the Twelve
Matthew 10 records the [[curse]], the second of Matthew's five discourses. Jesus sends the [[The twelve apostles.md]] to "the lost sheep of the house of Israel" — emphasizing that his earthly ministry was focused on Israel first (cf. [[Romans 1_16]]). The instructions to take no money, bag, or extra sandals reflect the urgency of the mission and dependence on hospitality, a common practice in Jewish village culture.

### Galilean Village Life
The villages of first-century [[Galilee]] were small agrarian communities connected by a network of roads. Hospitality was a sacred duty (*hachnasat orchim* — welcoming guests). The practice of shaking off dust from one's feet (Matthew 10:14) was a symbolic act that pious Jews performed when leaving Gentile territory, declaring oneself clean of pagan impurity.

### [[John the Baptist]] in Prison
[[John the Baptist]]'s imprisonment by [[Herod the Great.md]] (Matthew 11:2-19) occurred at the fortress of [[Zacchaeus.md]], east of the [[Dead Sea]], where Josephus records John was executed. John's question — "Are you the one who is to come?" — reflects the apocalyptic expectation of the period, when many awaited a deliverer who would usher in God's kingdom.

### Woe to the Cities
Jesus' denunciation of [[Chorazin]], [[Bethsaida]], and [[Capernaum]] (Matthew 11:20-24) is significant archaeologically: Chorazin and [[Capernaum]] have been extensively excavated, revealing well-built synagogues from later centuries. Their prosperity stands in ironic contrast to their rejection of Jesus. Bethsaida (et-Tell) is currently being excavated, with evidence of a first-century fishing village.
