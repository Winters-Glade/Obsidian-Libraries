## Historical & Cultural Context

> [!NOTE] QT Verification
> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### The Transfiguration
The [[Transfiguration]] (Matthew 17:1-8) is one of the most significant theophanic events in the Gospels. The traditional site — [[Mount Tabor]] in lower [[Galilee]] — has been commemorated by a church since the 4th century. Some scholars suggest [[Mount Hor.md]] (near [[Caesarea Philippi]]) may be the actual location, given the chronological proximity to Peter's confession. The appearance of [[Moses]] (representing the Law) and [[Elijah]] (representing the Prophets) confirms Jesus as the fulfillment of both. The cloud and heavenly voice echo [[Mount Sinai]] ([[Exodus 24.md]]) and the baptism of Jesus (Matthew 3:17).

### The Epileptic Boy
The healing of the epileptic boy (Matthew 17:14-21) contrasts the disciples' inability with Jesus' power. The saying about faith as a mustard seed moving mountains is a hyperbolic Jewish expression (cf. the rabbinic idiom "to uproot mountains" for removing great difficulties).

### The Temple Tax
The episode of the temple tax (Matthew 17:24-27, the *didrachma* or half-shekel) refers to the annual tax for the Temple maintenance, based on [[Exodus 30_11-16.md]]. This tax was collected from all adult Jewish males. Jesus' teaching about the "sons being free" (the king's sons are exempt from tax) and the miraculous provision from the fish's mouth reflects his divine identity as the Son of God, yet he pays the tax to avoid causing offense.

### Synoptic Parallels
The Transfiguration appears in all three Synoptics ([[Mark 9_2-13]], [[Luke 9_28-36.md]]). Luke uniquely mentions that Jesus was praying during the event and that Moses and Elijah spoke of his "departure" (exodos) in [[Jerusalem]]. Only Matthew mentions the temple tax episode.
