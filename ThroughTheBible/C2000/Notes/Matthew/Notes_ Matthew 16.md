## Historical & Cultural Context

> [!NOTE] QT Verification
> The enrichment below has been added by the Batch 6 Research workflow. Verify facts before using in teaching.

### Pharisees and Sadducees
The alliance of [[Pharisees]] and [[Sadducees]] (Matthew 16:1-12) is noteworthy, as these groups were normally opposed. The Pharisees were a lay religious party emphasizing [[Law]] and resurrection; the Sadducees were the priestly aristocracy who accepted only the written [[Torah]] and denied resurrection. Their temporary alliance against Jesus shows how seriously they viewed his challenge.

### [[Caesarea Philippi]]
[[Caesarea Philippi]] (Matthew 16:13-20, modern [[Ananias.md]]) was located at the foot of [[Mount Hor.md]] near one of the sources of the [[Jordan River]]. [[Herod the Great.md]] had built a city there, naming it after Caesar Augustus and himself. The site had a grotto dedicated to the god Pan (Paneas), making it a center of pagan worship. Jesus' question "Who do men say that I am?" and Peter's confession take on special significance in this pagan setting — the rock (petra) of Peter's confession stands against the backdrop of a pagan cult center.

### Peter's Confession and the Keys
The "keys of the kingdom" (Matthew 16:19) draw on [[Isaiah 22_22.md]], where Eliakim receives the "key of the house of David." The power to "bind and loose" (Hebrew *asar ve-hittir*) was a rabbinic formula for forbidding and permitting, or for excommunicating and readmitting.

### First Prediction of the Passion
Jesus' first explicit prediction of his death and resurrection (Matthew 16:21-28) marks a turning point in Matthew's Gospel. Peter's rebuke and Jesus' response — "Get behind me, Satan" — echoes the temptation narrative (Matthew 4:1-11), where Peter unwittingly becomes a tempter.
