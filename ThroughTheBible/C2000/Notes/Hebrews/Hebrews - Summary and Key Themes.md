## Historical & Contextual Background

> [!QUESTION]- This enrichment needs QT verification

**Mount Sinai vs. Mount Zion:** Hebrews 12:18–24 contrasts the terrifying theophany at Sinai (Exodus 19–20) with the joyful assembly of the heavenly Jerusalem — the "church of the firstborn." This contrast between the old and new covenants summarizes the epistle's entire argument.

**Divine Discipline:** The quotation of Proverbs 3:11–12 ("My son, do not despise the chastening of the Lord") reflects the wisdom tradition's understanding of suffering as divine discipline. The author of Hebrews reinterprets persecution as God's fatherly training rather than abandonment.

**Outside the Camp:** The call to go "outside the camp" bearing Christ's reproach (13:13) echoes the Day of Atonement ritual where the scapegoat was led outside the camp (Leviticus 16:27). It's a call to sever ties with the old covenant system and identify fully with Christ's rejection.

**Closing Greetings:** The epistle closes with greetings from "those from Italy" (13:24), suggesting the author may have been writing from or to Rome. The reference to Timothy (13:23) provides one of the few personal details.
