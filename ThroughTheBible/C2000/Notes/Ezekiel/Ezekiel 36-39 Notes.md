## Summary

Chapter 36 begins the great **restoration** section of Ezekiel. For the second time, Ezekiel prophesies to the **mountains of Israel** — the first time (chapter 6) was a prophecy of desolation because of the high places of pagan worship; now it is a prophecy of restoration. The mountains had remained desolate for 19 centuries, but God declares they will be tilled and sown, cities will be inhabited, and the waste places will be rebuilt. The heathen nations who mocked Israel will bear their own shame. Then comes one of the most magnificent promises in all Scripture: "**I will give you a new heart and put a new spirit within you; I will take the heart of stone out of your flesh and give you a heart of flesh. I will put My Spirit within you and cause you to walk in My statutes.**" The land that was desolate will become like the [[Garden of Eden]].

Chapter 37 is the famous **Valley of Dry Bones** vision. The hand of the Lord sets Ezekiel in a valley full of very dry bones. God asks, "Son of man, can these bones live?" Ezekiel answers, "O Lord God, You know." Ezekiel prophesies to the bones: sinews, flesh, and skin come upon them, but there is no breath. Then he prophesies to the wind/breath ([[Ruach]]), and breath enters them — they stand up, an exceedingly great army. God explains: "These bones are the whole house of Israel." The vision promises both physical restoration (return to the land) and spiritual restoration (the Spirit of God). Then the **two sticks** — one for [[Judah]] and one for [[Joseph]]/[[Ephraim]] — become one in God's hand, symbolizing the reunification of the divided kingdoms under one King, "My servant David."

Chapters 38-39 describe the **Gog and Magog** invasion. Gog, from the land of [[Magog]] (identified with the region of [[Russia]] and the north), leads a coalition including [[Persia]] (Iran), [[Ethiopia]] (Cush), [[Libya]] (Put), [[Gomer]] (Germany/Europe), and [[Togarmah]] (Turkey) against Israel when they are dwelling securely in unwalled villages. God intervenes with a great earthquake, pestilence, blood, overflowing rain, hailstones, fire, and brimstone — destroying the invading army. The weapons are burned for seven years, and the burial of Gog takes place in the Valley of Hamon-gog. God declares, "**I will pour out My Spirit on the house of Israel**" (Ezekiel 39:27-29).

## 🏛️ Historical Context

> [!qt-verified] Historical Context

Chapters 36-39 represent the transition from judgment to restoration in Ezekiel's prophecy, delivered after the fall of Jerusalem (c. **585-573 BC**). The "dry bones" vision (chapter 37) has been seen throughout church history as both a prophecy of Israel's national restoration — dramatically fulfilled in the modern regathering of Jews to Israel in the 20th century — and as a picture of spiritual resurrection (as applied by Paul in [[1 Corinthians 15.md]]). Chuck Smith placed great emphasis on this passage as prophetic of the rebirth of the nation of Israel in 1948.

The Gog and Magog prophecy (chapters 38-39) is one of the most studied eschatological passages in Scripture. The identification of Magog with the region of modern **Russia** is based on the Hebrew pointing to the far north (the "chief prince of Meshech and Tubal" — Moscow and Tobolsk as plausible etymological links). This interpretation was central to Chuck Smith's prophetic teaching. The coalition of nations mirrors the geopolitical alliances of the late 20th century: Russia leading Islamic nations (Iran, Libya, Sudan) and European allies.

The promise of the outpouring of the Spirit (Ezekiel 39:27-29, T2000: [[_transcripts/When God Pours His Spirit On Israel, Ezekiel 39_27-29.md|When God Pours His Spirit On Israel]]) connects to [[Joel 2_28-29.md]] and [[_Concepts/Biblical_References/Acts 2]] — Peter quoted Joel on the day of Pentecost.
