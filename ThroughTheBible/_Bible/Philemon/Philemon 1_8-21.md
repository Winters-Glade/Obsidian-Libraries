# Philemon 1:8-21


## Passage

> **8** Wherefore, though I might be much bold in Christ to enjoin thee that which is convenient, **9** Yet for love's sake I rather beseech {thee}, being such an one as Paul the aged, and now also a prisoner of Jesus Christ. **10** I beseech thee for my son Onesimus, whom I have begotten in my bonds: **11** Which in time past was to thee unprofitable, but now profitable to thee and to me: **12** Whom I have sent again: thou therefore receive him, that is, mine own bowels: **13** Whom I would have retained with me, that in thy stead he might have ministered unto me in the bonds of the gospel: **14** But without thy mind would I do nothing; that thy benefit should not be as it were of necessity, but willingly. **15** For perhaps he therefore departed for a season, that thou shouldest receive him for ever; **16** Not now as a servant, but above a servant, a brother beloved, specially to me, but how much more unto thee, both in the flesh, and in the Lord? **17** If thou count me therefore a partner, receive him as myself. **18** If he hath wronged thee, or oweth {thee} ought, put that on mine account; **19** I Paul have written {it} with mine own hand, I will repay {it}: albeit I do not say to thee how thou owest unto me even thine own self besides. **20** Yea, brother, let me have joy of thee in the Lord: refresh my bowels in the Lord. **21** Having confidence in thy obedience I wrote unto thee, knowing that thou wilt also do more than I say.
>
> — Philemon 1:8-21 (KJV)
