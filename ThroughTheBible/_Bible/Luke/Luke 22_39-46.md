# Luke 22:39-46


## Passage

> **39** And he came out, and went, as he was wont, to the mount of Olives; and his disciples also followed him. **40** And when he was at the place, he said unto them, Pray that ye enter not into temptation. **41** And he was withdrawn from them about a stone's cast, and kneeled down, and prayed, **42** Saying, Father, if thou be willing, remove this cup from me: nevertheless not my will, but thine, be done. {willing, remove: Gr. willing to remove} **43** And there appeared an angel unto him from heaven, strengthening him. **44** And being in an agony he prayed more earnestly: and his sweat was as it were great drops of blood falling down to the ground. **45** And when he rose up from prayer, and was come to his disciples, he found them sleeping for sorrow, **46** And said unto them, Why sleep ye? rise and pray, lest ye enter into temptation.
>
> — Luke 22:39-46 (KJV)
