# Arabia

## Description

Arabia in biblical times refers broadly to the vast desert region south and east of Israel, encompassing much of the Arabian Peninsula. It is mentioned in connection with several key biblical events and people. Paul spent time in Arabia after his conversion (Galatians 1:17), likely in the desert region near Nabataea. Mount Sinai is sometimes located in Arabia (Galatians 4:25 refers to "Mount Sinai in Arabia"). The Queen of Sheba came from Arabia (likely modern Yemen) to test Solomon's wisdom. Arabia was known for its trade in spices, gold, and incense. The prophets Isaiah, Jeremiah, and Ezekiel pronounce oracles against Arabian tribes. In Acts, visitors from Arabia were present at Pentecost (Acts 2:11). Chuck Smith notes that Arabia represents the wilderness where God often prepares His servants through solitude and testing.
