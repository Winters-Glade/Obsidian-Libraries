# Miletus

## Description

Miletus was an ancient Greek city on the western coast of Asia Minor (modern Turkey), located near the mouth of the Meander River, about 30 miles south of Ephesus. It was one of the wealthiest and most powerful cities of Ionian Greece, a center of philosophy, science, and commerce. In the New Testament, Miletus is the location of Paul's emotional farewell to the Ephesian elders (Acts 20:15-38). Paul had sailed past Ephesus to avoid delay, summoning the elders to meet him in Miletus. His farewell speech is a deeply moving pastoral address in which he warns of "savage wolves" coming after his departure, commends them to God and "the word of His grace," and reminds them of his own example of hard work and generosity. The elders wept and kissed him, grieving over his words that they would see his face no more. Paul also left Trophimus ill in Miletus near the end of his life (2 Timothy 4:20).
