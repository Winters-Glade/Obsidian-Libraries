# Hebron

## Description

Hebron is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, located in the Judean hills about 19 miles south of Jerusalem. It is situated approximately 3,000 feet above sea level. Originally called Kiriath Arba ("city of four"), it was associated with the patriarchs: Abraham lived there, built an altar, and purchased the cave of Machpelah as a burial site for Sarah (Genesis 23). Abraham, Isaac, Rebekah, Jacob, and Leah were all buried in the cave of Machpelah at Hebron. The city was given to Caleb after the conquest (Joshua 14:6-15). Hebron served as David's first capital, where he reigned for seven years before moving the capital to Jerusalem (2 Samuel 2:1-11). It was there that his sons were born, including Absalom, who later launched his rebellion from Hebron (2 Samuel 15:7-10). Hebron was designated a Levitical city and a city of refuge (Joshua 21:13).
