# Goshen

## Description

Goshen was a fertile region in the northeastern Nile Delta of Egypt, assigned by Pharaoh to Jacob and his family when they settled in Egypt during Joseph's administration (Genesis 46:28-34). It was located near the land of Rameses, probably in the Wadi Tumilat region east of the Nile Delta. Goshen was ideal for shepherds (the Israelites were shepherds, which Egyptians despised) and was also close to the Egyptian capital. During the plagues, Goshen was spared from the plagues of flies, hail, and darkness — demonstrating God's protection of His people in the midst of judgment (Exodus 8:22-24, 9:26, 10:23). The Israelites lived in Goshen for 430 years before the Exodus. Goshen represents God's provision and protection for His people during times of sojourn and testing.
