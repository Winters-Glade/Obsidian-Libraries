# Assyria

## Description

Assyria was a mighty empire centered in northern Mesopotamia (modern northern Iraq), with its capitals at Nineveh, Ashur, and Calah. It rose to dominate the ancient Near East from approximately 900 to 612 BC, known for its military prowess, brutal conquests, and sophisticated administration. The Assyrian Empire was the first great empire to threaten and conquer the Kingdom of Israel.

Chuck Smith teaches that Assyria was God's instrument of judgment against the Northern Kingdom of Israel for its idolatry and covenant unfaithfulness. Under kings like Tiglath-Pileser III, Shalmaneser V, and Sargon II, Assyria conquered the Northern Kingdom in 722 BC, deporting its inhabitants and resettling the land with foreigners — creating the mixed population later known as Samaritans. The Assyrians also threatened Judah under Sennacherib, but God miraculously delivered Jerusalem in response to Hezekiah's faith and Isaiah's prophecy (2 Kings 18-19; Isaiah 36-37).

Pastor Smith often presents Assyria as an example of how God uses pagan nations to discipline His people, while also holding those nations accountable for their own pride and cruelty. The fall of Nineveh in 612 BC, prophesied by Nahum and Zephaniah, demonstrates God's judgment on human arrogance.
