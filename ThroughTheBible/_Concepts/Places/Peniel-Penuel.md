# Peniel/Penuel

## Description

Peniel (also called Penuel) means "Face of God." It was the place by the Jabbok River where Jacob wrestled with the angel of the Lord throughout the night. Jacob refused to let go until he received a blessing, and his name was changed to Israel, "one who strives with God and prevails." Jacob named the place Peniel, saying, "I have seen God face to face, and my life is preserved." Chuck Smith highlights this as the turning point in Jacob's life—the moment of surrender when human striving gives way to divine blessing.
