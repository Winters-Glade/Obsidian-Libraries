# Jerusalem

## Description

Jerusalem is the most significant city in biblical history, the capital of Israel, and the spiritual center of God's redemptive plan. Located in the Judean hills at approximately 2,500 feet above sea level, it sits on a plateau surrounded by the Kidron Valley (east), the Hinnom Valley (south and west), and the Tyropoeon Valley (central). Originally a Canaanite city called Jebus, it was conquered by King David around 1000 BC and became the political and religious capital of Israel.

Chuck Smith emphasizes Jerusalem as the place where God chose to put His name—the site of the Temple, the ministry of Jesus, the birth of the Church at Pentecost, and the focal point of end-times prophecy. In the T2000/C2000 context, Jerusalem is presented as the city of the Great King, the scene of Christ's crucifixion and resurrection, and the future capital of Christ's millennial kingdom.

The city has been destroyed twice (586 BC by Babylon, AD 70 by Rome) and besieged numerous times, yet it remains central to God's covenant purposes. Pastor Smith often highlights that Jerusalem's spiritual tragedy was its rejection of the Messiah who wept over it (Luke 19:41-44).
