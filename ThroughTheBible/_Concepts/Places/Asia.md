# Asia

## Description

In the New Testament, "Asia" does not refer to the entire continent but to the Roman province of Asia, located in western Asia Minor (modern Turkey). Its capital was Ephesus, and it included major cities such as Smyrna, Pergamum, Thyatira, Sardis, Philadelphia, Laodicea, Miletus, and Colossae. The province was rich, highly urbanized, and thoroughly Hellenized, with a strong Roman administrative presence.

Chuck Smith notes that Asia was a strategic region for early Christian expansion. Paul was forbidden by the Holy Spirit to preach in Asia during his second missionary journey (Acts 16:6) but later spent nearly three years in Ephesus during his third journey, "so that all who dwelt in Asia heard the word of the Lord Jesus, both Jews and Greeks" (Acts 19:10). The seven churches of Revelation 2-3 are all located in the province of Asia.

The province was also home to the great Temple of Artemis at Ephesus, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, and was a center of emperor worship, which created significant pressure for Christians.
