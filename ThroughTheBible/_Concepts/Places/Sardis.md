# Sardis

## Description

Sardis (modern Sart, Turkey) was the ancient capital of Lydia in western Asia Minor, located at the foot of Mount Tmolus in the Hermus River Valley, about 50 miles east of Smyrna. It was the seat of the wealthy Lydian kingdom under King Croesus, known for inventing coinage and amassing legendary wealth. The acropolis was considered impregnable — yet was conquered twice by surprise attacks (by Cyrus the Persian in 546 BC and by Antiochus III in 215 BC). In the New Testament, Sardis was one of the seven churches of Revelation. Jesus's message to the church in Sardis is sobering: "I know your works, that you have a name that you are alive, but you are dead" (Revelation 3:1). The church is called to "be watchful" and strengthen what remains. A few faithful souls are commended — "who have not defiled their garments" (Revelation 3:4). Sardis represents the danger of outward religious reputation without inward spiritual vitality. The city declined after a devastating earthquake in AD 17.
