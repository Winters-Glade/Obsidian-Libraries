# Horeb

## Description

Horeb ("desolate" or "dry") is another name for Mount Sinai, the mountain of God where Moses received the Law and where Israel made and worshiped the golden calf. The terms "Sinai" and "Horeb" are often used interchangeably, though some scholars distinguish between them (Horeb may refer to the broader range, while Sinai is the specific peak). Key events at Horeb include: the burning bush (Exodus 3:1-6); the giving of the Ten Commandments (Deuteronomy 5); the people's rebellion with the golden calf (Exodus 32); Elijah's encounter with God in "a still small voice" (1 Kings 19:8-18); and the ground where Moses struck the rock for water (Exodus 17:6). Chuck Smith often refers to Horeb as the place where God revealed His covenant Law and where the prophet Elijah learned that God's voice is not in the dramatic but in the quiet — a lesson in the nature of divine communication.
