# Galilee

## Description

Galilee is the northern region of Israel, bordered by the Mediterranean Sea to the west, the Jordan River and Sea of Galilee to the east, the Jezreel Valley to the south, and the Lebanon mountains to the north. It is a fertile, hilly region divided into Upper Galilee (higher, more rugged) and Lower Galilee (rolling hills and valleys). The Sea of Galilee (also called Lake Gennesaret or Sea of Tiberias) is a freshwater lake about 13 miles long and 7 miles wide, situated 680 feet below sea level.

Chuck Smith emphasizes Galilee as the primary setting for Jesus' public ministry — often called "Galilee of the Gentiles" (Isaiah 9:1-2; Matthew 4:15-16). Jesus grew up in Nazareth, chose most of His disciples from among Galilean fishermen, performed His first miracle at Cana, and preached extensively around the Sea of Galilee — including the Sermon on the Mount, the calming of the storm, the feeding of the 5,000, and many healing miracles. Galilee was also the region where Jesus appeared to His disciples after the resurrection.

Pastor Smith highlights that Galilee was a crossroads of cultures — Jewish and Gentile — making it a fitting launch point for the gospel that would eventually go to all nations. The region was less religiously rigid than Judea, and its people were known for their openness and zeal.
