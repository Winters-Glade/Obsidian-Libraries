# Gilgal

## Description

Gilgal was the first campsite of the Israelites after crossing the Jordan River into the Promised Land, located east of Jericho (Joshua 4:19-24). Here Joshua erected twelve memorial stones taken from the Jordan River bed to commemorate the miraculous crossing. At Gilgal, the new generation was circumcised (renewing the covenant), the Passover was celebrated, and the manna ceased as the people began to eat the produce of the land (Joshua 5:1-12).

Chuck Smith teaches that Gilgal represents a place of spiritual renewal and new beginnings. It was where the reproach of Egypt was rolled away, where the covenant was renewed, and where Israel began its conquest of Canaan. However, Gilgal later became a center of idolatry and false worship (Hosea 4:15, 9:15; Amos 4:4), demonstrating that even sacred places can be corrupted when the heart turns from God. The prophets denounced the worship at Gilgal as empty ritual.

Gilgal was also the site where Saul was crowned king (1 Samuel 11:14-15) and where he later disobediently offered sacrifices (1 Samuel 13:7-15), leading to God's rejection of his dynasty.
