# Gibeon

## Description

Gibeon was a major city in the hill country of Benjamin, about 6 miles northwest of Jerusalem. It is best known for the Gibeonites' deception of Joshua (Joshua 9). Fearing Israel's conquest, the Gibeonites disguised themselves as travelers from a distant land and made a treaty with Joshua, which Israel later honored even after discovering the deception — making them "hewers of wood and drawers of water" for the congregation. When the Amorite kings attacked Gibeon for making peace with Israel, Joshua came to their aid, and the Lord caused the sun to stand still over Gibeon (Joshua 10:1-14). Gibeon later became a Levitical city (Joshua 21:17). The tabernacle of the Lord was located at Gibeon during part of Solomon's reign (1 Chronicles 16:39, 21:29). It was also the site of Joab's and Abner's deadly contest (2 Samuel 2:12-17) and the place where Solomon offered sacrifices and received wisdom from God (1 Kings 3:4-15).
