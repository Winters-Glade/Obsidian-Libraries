# Cyprus

## Description

Cyprus is the third-largest island in the Mediterranean Sea, located about 60 miles west of Syria. In the Old Testament, it is referred to as "Kittim" (a descendant of Javan, Genesis 10:4). In the New Testament, Cyprus holds significant importance as the birthplace of Barnabas (a Levite from Cyprus, Acts 4:36) and as the first destination of Paul and Barnabas on their first missionary journey (Acts 13:4-12). They preached in the synagogues of Salamis and confronted the false prophet Bar-Jesus (Elymas) at Paphos, where the proconsul Sergius Paulus believed. The island later hosted Paul's ship on his final journey to Jerusalem (Acts 21:3). Cyprus is also mentioned as one of the regions whose Jewish pilgrims were at Pentecost (Acts 2:10). The early church spread rapidly on Cyprus.
