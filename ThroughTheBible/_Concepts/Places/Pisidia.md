# Pisidia

## Description

Pisidia was a mountainous region in southern Asia Minor (modern Turkey), north of Pamphylia and west of Cilicia. It was a rugged, difficult area known for its warlike tribes and banditry until Roman control brought order. The most important city in Pisidia for the New Testament is Pisidian Antioch (distinct from Syrian Antioch). Paul and Barnabas visited Pisidian Antioch on their first missionary journey (Acts 13:13-52), where Paul preached a landmark sermon in the synagogue on the Sabbath, presenting Jesus as the fulfillment of Old Testament promises. Many Jews and Gentile God-fearers believed, but Jewish opposition stirred up persecution, forcing Paul and Barnabas to leave. They then traveled to Iconium, Lystra, and Derbe — all cities in the broader Phrygian-Pisidian region. Paul revisited the area on his second and third journeys (Acts 14:21-25, 18:23). The churches of Pisidia represent the early spread of the gospel into the interior of Asia Minor.
