# Judea

## Description

Judea is the southern region of the land of Israel, corresponding roughly to the territory of the ancient tribe of Judah. It stretches from the hill country around Jerusalem south to the Negev desert and from the Mediterranean coastal plain east to the Dead Sea and the Jordan Valley. The region includes Jerusalem, Bethlehem, Hebron, and the Judean wilderness.

In the Persian and Roman periods, Judea was the name of the province that encompassed the Jewish homeland. It was governed by Persian officials, then by the Ptolemies and Seleucids, then by the Hasmoneans, and finally by Roman prefects and procurators (such as Pontius Pilate). Herod the Great ruled Judea as a client king under Rome.

Chuck Smith emphasizes Judea as the heartland of Jewish identity and faith. In the New Testament, Judea is the setting for Jesus' birth in Bethlehem, His ministry in the villages, His crucifixion in Jerusalem, and the early church's expansion (Acts 1-12). The region symbolizes the continuity of God's covenant promises from Abraham through the Messianic age.
