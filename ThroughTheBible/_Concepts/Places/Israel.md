# Israel

## Description

Israel is the land promised by God to Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob — the geographical stage for most of biblical history. It sits at the crossroads of Africa, Asia, and Europe, roughly 150 miles long and 60 miles wide, bordered by the Mediterranean Sea to the west, the Jordan River and Dead Sea to the east, the hills of Lebanon to the north, and the Negev desert to the south.

Chuck Smith teaches that Israel is central to God's covenant purposes — chosen not because of its size or power but because of God's love and faithfulness to His promises. The land was given as an everlasting inheritance, and its history of conquest, division, exile, and restoration mirrors the spiritual journey of God's people. In the T2000/C2000 context, Israel serves as both the historical setting for the Old and New Testaments and a prophetic signpost for the end times. Pastor Smith emphasizes that God's covenant with Israel remains valid and that the modern restoration of Israel fulfills biblical prophecy.

The land's geography is remarkably diverse, from the fertile Galilee region to the barren Judean wilderness, from the coastal plain to the mountainous spine running north-south. This diversity shapes much of the biblical narrative.
