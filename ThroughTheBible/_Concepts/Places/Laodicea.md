# Laodicea

## Description

Laodicea (Laodicea ad Lycum) was a wealthy city in the Lycus Valley of Phrygia (southwest Asia Minor, near modern Denizli, Turkey). It was located about 10 miles west of Colossae and 6 miles south of Hierapolis. Founded by Antiochus II (261-246 BC) and named after his wife Laodice, it became a prosperous commercial and banking center, known for its black wool cloth and an eye salve made from local minerals. Its water supply was lukewarm, piped from hot springs at Hierapolis — a fact Jesus used in His message to the church at Laodicea: "you are neither cold nor hot... I will vomit you out of My mouth" (Revelation 3:14-22). Paul wrote a letter to the Laodiceans (Colossians 4:16), and Epaphras labored earnestly in prayer for them (Colossians 4:12-13). The Laodicean church represents the danger of spiritual lukewarmness and self-sufficiency arising from material wealth.
