# Sidon

## Description

Sidon (modern Saida, Lebanon) was an ancient Phoenician port city on the Mediterranean coast, about 25 miles south of Beirut and 20 miles north of Tyre. It was one of the oldest and most important cities of Phoenicia, known for its maritime trade, purple dye industry, glass manufacturing, and metalwork. In the Old Testament, Sidon was the firstborn son of Canaan (Genesis 10:15) and was part of the territory allotted to the tribe of Asher, though Asher never fully conquered it (Judges 1:31). Sidon was the home of Jezebel, daughter of King Ethbaal of Sidon, who married King Ahab and promoted Baal worship in Israel (1 Kings 16:31). The prophets Isaiah (Isaiah 23), Jeremiah (Jeremiah 25:22), Ezekiel (Ezekiel 28:20-24), Joel (Joel 3:4-8), and Zechariah (Zechariah 9:2) pronounced judgments on Sidon. In the New Testament, Jesus visited the region of Tyre and Sidon and healed the Syro-Phoenician woman's daughter (Mark 7:24-30). Paul stopped at Sidon on his voyage to Rome (Acts 27:3).
