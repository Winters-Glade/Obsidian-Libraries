# Edom

## Description

Edom was a nation and region located south of the Dead Sea, in the mountainous area of modern-day southern Jordan. The Edomites were descendants of Esau, Jacob's twin brother, making them close relatives of the Israelites. Their territory stretched from the Zered River in the north to the Gulf of Aqaba in the south, including the famous rock-carved city of Petra (likely biblical Sela). The region is characterized by rugged mountains, deep canyons, and red sandstone.

Chuck Smith discusses Edom in the context of the long-standing conflict between Jacob and Esau's descendants. Edom refused Israel passage through their territory during the Exodus (Numbers 20:14-21), and hostility continued through much of biblical history. David conquered Edom, but it later rebelled. The prophets pronounced severe judgment on Edom for its pride and its violence against Israel (Obadiah, Isaiah 34, Ezekiel 35). Despite their kinship, Edom became a symbol of those who oppose God's people.

Pastor Smith notes that the book of Obadiah, the shortest book of the Old Testament, is entirely devoted to Edom's judgment — a reminder that God holds all nations accountable for their treatment of His covenant people.
