# Salamis

## Description

Salamis was an ancient city on the eastern coast of Cyprus, the island's capital and major port in the Hellenistic and Roman periods. It was located near modern Famagusta. In the New Testament, Salamis is where Paul and Barnabas began their first missionary journey (Acts 13:4-5). After sailing from Seleucia (the port of Antioch in Syria), they arrived at Salamis and "preached the word of God in the synagogues of the Jews." John Mark was with them as their assistant. Salamis had a substantial Jewish population, and the presence of multiple synagogues shows the Jewish community was sizable and organized. From Salamis, Paul and Barnabas traveled across the island to Paphos, where they confronted Elymas the sorcerer and led Sergius Paulus to faith. Salamis declined after a series of earthquakes and was eventually abandoned in the Middle Ages.
