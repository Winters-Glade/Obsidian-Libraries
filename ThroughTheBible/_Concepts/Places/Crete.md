# Crete

## Description

Crete is a large mountainous island in the Mediterranean Sea, southeast of Greece. It was the center of the ancient Minoan civilization. In the New Testament, Paul sailed past Crete during his voyage to Rome (Acts 27:7-21), and the ship found a harbor called Fair Havens on the south coast. Paul left Titus in Crete to "set in order the things that are lacking, and appoint elders in every city" (Titus 1:5). The Epistle to Titus addresses challenges in the Cretan church, quoting the Cretan poet Epimenides: "Cretans are always liars, evil beasts, lazy gluttons" (Titus 1:12). Paul instructed Titus to teach sound doctrine and good works. Crete represents both the challenge of cross-cultural evangelism and the transformative power of the gospel to change entire communities.
