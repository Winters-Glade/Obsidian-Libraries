# Emmaus

## Description

Emmaus was a village about seven miles (60 stadia) northwest of Jerusalem, best known as the site where the resurrected Jesus appeared to two discouraged disciples on the day of His resurrection (Luke 24:13-35). As they walked from Jerusalem to Emmaus, discussing the crucifixion and reports of the empty tomb, the risen Christ joined them — but their eyes were kept from recognizing Him. He explained the Scriptures concerning Himself, beginning with Moses and all the Prophets. When they arrived in Emmaus, He broke bread with them, and their eyes were opened. He then vanished from their sight. They returned immediately to Jerusalem to tell the eleven. This "Emmaus Road" encounter is a powerful example of how the Old Testament points to Christ and how Jesus reveals Himself through Scripture and fellowship. The exact location of Emmaus is uncertain; three sites have been proposed (Emmaus Nicopolis, El Qubeibeh, and Abu Ghosh).
