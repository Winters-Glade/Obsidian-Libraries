# Dead Sea

## Description

The Dead Sea is a large saltwater lake at the southern end of the Jordan Rift Valley, bordering Israel, the West Bank, and Jordan. It is the lowest point on Earth at approximately 430 meters (1,410 feet) below sea level. Its water is nearly ten times saltier than the ocean, making it unable to support fish or plant life — hence its name. In the Bible, it is called the Salt Sea (Genesis 14:3, Numbers 34:3), the Sea of the Arabah (Deuteronomy 3:17), and the Eastern Sea (Ezekiel 47:18, Joel 2:20). The cities of Sodom and Gomorrah were located near its southern end. The Dead Sea Scrolls were discovered in caves at Qumran on its northwestern shore. Ezekiel's vision of a river flowing from the Temple that will heal the Dead Sea (Ezekiel 47:8-10) is interpreted prophetically as a picture of life flowing from God's presence. Chuck Smith often referenced this prophecy in the context of the millennial kingdom.
