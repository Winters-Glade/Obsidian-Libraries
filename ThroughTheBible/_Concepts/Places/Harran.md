# Harran

## Description

Harran was an ancient city in Upper Mesopotamia (modern southeastern Turkey, near the Syrian border), located on the Balikh River, a tributary of the Euphrates. It was a major commercial center on trade routes connecting Mesopotamia, Syria, and Anatolia. In the Bible, Harran is the place where Terah, Abraham's father, settled with his family after leaving Ur (Genesis 11:31). Abraham (then Abram) lived in Harran until God called him to go to Canaan (Genesis 12:1-5). It was also the home of Abraham's brother Nahor and his family; Abraham's servant traveled to Harran to find a wife for Isaac, resulting in Rebekah (Genesis 24). Jacob later fled to Harran to escape Esau's anger and lived there for 20 years, marrying Leah and Rachel and fathering most of his sons (Genesis 29-31). Harran was associated with the worship of the moon god Sin.
