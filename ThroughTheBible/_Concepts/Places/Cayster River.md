# Cayster River

## Description

The Cayster River (modern Küçük Menderes) was a river in western Asia Minor that flowed past the city of Ephesus into the Aegean Sea. In ancient times, Ephesus was a major port city at the mouth of the Cayster, making it a thriving commercial center. However, over centuries, the river gradually silted up the harbor, forcing the city to relocate westward and eventually contributing to its decline and abandonment.

Chuck Smith notes the Cayster River in the context of Ephesus' historical setting. The silting of the Cayster River harbor is a well-documented natural phenomenon that illustrates both the rise and fall of cities in the ancient world. The river that brought trade and prosperity to Ephesus eventually became the agent of its demise — a reminder of the temporal nature of human achievement. When Paul ministered in Ephesus, the harbor was still functioning, making the city a strategic base for the spread of the gospel throughout the province of Asia.
