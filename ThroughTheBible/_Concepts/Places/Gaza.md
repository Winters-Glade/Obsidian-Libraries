# Gaza

## Description

Gaza was one of the five major Philistine cities (the pentapolis) and the southernmost, located near the Mediterranean coast on the border of Canaan and Egypt. It was assigned to the tribe of Judah (Joshua 15:47) but remained under Philistine control for much of Israel's early history. Gaza is most famous as the place where Samson, after being betrayed by Delilah and having his eyes gouged out, was imprisoned and forced to grind grain. In his final act, Samson pulled down the temple of Dagon, killing himself and thousands of Philistines (Judges 16:21-31). The city was later conquered by Hezekiah (2 Kings 18:8). The prophets Amos (Amos 1:6-7), Jeremiah (Jeremiah 47), and Zephaniah (Zephaniah 2:4) pronounced judgment on Gaza. In the New Testament, Philip the evangelist traveled on the road from Jerusalem to Gaza when he encountered the Ethiopian eunuch (Acts 8:26-39). Gaza has been continuously inhabited for over 3,000 years.
