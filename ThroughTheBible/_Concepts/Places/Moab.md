# Moab

## Description

Moab was a nation and region located east of the Dead Sea, in what is now modern Jordan. The Moabites were descendants of Lot through his elder daughter (Genesis 19:30-38), making them distant relatives of the Israelites. The territory was bounded by the Arnon River to the north, the Zered River to the south, the Dead Sea to the west, and the desert to the east. The region is a high plateau, fertile enough for agriculture and grazing.

Chuck Smith discusses Moab in the context of Israel's journey to the Promised Land. The Israelites camped in the plains of Moab before crossing the Jordan into Canaan (Numbers 22-36). It was there that Balak, king of Moab, hired Balaam to curse Israel, and there that Moses delivered his final sermons recorded in Deuteronomy. Moab was frequently in conflict with Israel, though notable exceptions include Ruth (a Moabite who became the grandmother of King David) and David placing his parents under the protection of the king of Moab.

Pastor Smith notes that Moab illustrates how God works through imperfect human relationships and can redeem those outside His covenant people.
