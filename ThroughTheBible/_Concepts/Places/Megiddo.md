# Megiddo

## Description

Megiddo was a strategically vital Canaanite and later Israelite city located at the entrance to the Jezreel Valley (Plain of Esdraelon), guarding one of the most important military and trade routes in the ancient Near East (the Via Maris). It was conquered by Joshua (Joshua 12:21) and later fortified by Solomon, who made it one of his chariot cities (1 Kings 9:15). The city had an elaborate water system and multiple levels of fortification spanning 20+ layers of civilization. Megiddo was the site of several pivotal battles: Barak and Deborah defeated Sisera near its waters (Judges 5:19); Josiah was killed by Pharaoh Necho at Megiddo (2 Kings 23:29-30). The name Armageddon (Greek: *Harmagedon*, from Hebrew *Har Megiddo*, "Mount of Megiddo") appears in Revelation 16:16 as the gathering place for the final battle between the forces of good and evil at Christ's return. Chuck Smith taught that Megiddo/Armageddon is the prophesied site of the climactic battle of the end times.
