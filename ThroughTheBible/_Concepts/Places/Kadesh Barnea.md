# Kadesh Barnea

## Description

Kadesh Barnea (also called simply Kadesh) was the most significant campsite of the Israelites during their 40-year wilderness journey. Located in the Wilderness of Zin on the southern border of Canaan, about 50 miles south of Beersheba, it served as the base from which the twelve spies were sent to explore the Promised Land (Numbers 13:26). The spies returned with a report that sparked rebellion, leading to God's judgment that the exodus generation would die in the wilderness (Numbers 14). From Kadesh, Moses sent messengers to the king of Edom requesting passage through his territory (Numbers 20:14-21). It was at the waters of Meribah in Kadesh that Moses struck the rock in anger rather than speaking to it, an act of disobedience that cost him entry into the Promised Land (Numbers 20:1-13). Kadesh marks the place between the 40th year of wandering and the new beginning that led to conquest.
