# Golgotha

## Description

Golgotha, also called Calvary (from Latin *calvaria*, "skull"), was the site outside Jerusalem's walls where Jesus was crucified. The name comes from the Aramaic *Golgotha*, meaning "the skull," possibly referring to the shape of the hill or its use as a place of execution. The exact location is debated; the traditional site is within the Church of the Holy Sepulchre, while "Gordon's Calvary" (near the Garden Tomb) is an alternative popular among Protestants. John 19:20 states the site was near the city. The Gospel accounts note that Golgotha was visible from a distance (Mark 15:40) and that it was near a garden with a new tomb (John 19:41-42). Chuck Smith emphasizes Golgotha as the place where the Son of God died for the sins of the world — the focal point of redemptive history where judgment and mercy met.
