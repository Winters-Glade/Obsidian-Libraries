# Capernaum

## Description

Capernaum was a fishing village on the northwestern shore of the Sea of Galilee, in the region of Galilee. It became the center of Jesus' public ministry during His Galilean years — so much so that it is called "His own city" in Matthew 9:1. Located on the Via Maris, the major trade route between Damascus and the Mediterranean coast, it was a prosperous settlement with a Roman garrison and a customs post.

Chuck Smith highlights Capernaum as the base of operations for Jesus' Galilean ministry. Here Jesus called Matthew (Levi) the tax collector, Peter and Andrew, and James and John from their fishing businesses. He performed many miracles in Capernaum, including healing Peter's mother-in-law, the centurion's servant, the paralytic let down through the roof, and the man with the withered hand. Despite witnessing these miracles, Capernaum rejected Jesus, leading to His lament: "And you, Capernaum, will you be lifted up to heaven? No, you will go down to Hades" (Matthew 11:23).

The ruins of Capernaum, including a fourth-century synagogue built over the one where Jesus taught, are a major archaeological site today.
