# Gethsemane

## Description

Gethsemane (from Aramaic *Gat Shmanim*, "oil press") is a garden at the foot of the Mount of Olives, across the Kidron Valley from Jerusalem's eastern wall. It was a place Jesus frequently visited with His disciples. On the night of His betrayal, Jesus went to Gethsemane to pray after the Last Supper. There He experienced profound agony, sweating "as it were great drops of blood" (Luke 22:44), as He prayed "Father, if it is Your will, take this cup away from Me; nevertheless not My will, but Yours, be done" (Luke 22:42). It was in Gethsemane that Judas betrayed Jesus with a kiss, and where Peter cut off the ear of Malchus, the high priest's servant. Jesus was arrested there, beginning the chain of events that led to the crucifixion. Chuck Smith emphasizes Gethsemane as the place where Jesus fully submitted to the Father's will, demonstrating the model of prayer and surrender in the face of suffering.
