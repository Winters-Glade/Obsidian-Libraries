# Mount of Olives

## Description

The Mount of Olives is a mountain ridge east of Jerusalem, separated from the Temple Mount by the Kidron Valley. It rises to about 2,700 feet above sea level and offers a panoramic view of the Old City and the Temple Mount. Its slopes were covered with olive trees in biblical times, giving it its name.

Chuck Smith highlights the Mount of Olives as a site of profound significance in the life of Jesus and in biblical prophecy. Jesus frequently went to the Mount of Olives with His disciples — it was where He gave the Olivet Discourse (Matthew 24-25; Mark 13; Luke 21), where He prayed in the Garden of Gethsemane at its foot, where He was arrested, and from where He ascended into heaven (Acts 1:9-12). The prophet Zechariah (14:4) prophesied that the Messiah's feet would stand on the Mount of Olives at His return, causing it to split in two.

Pastor Smith emphasizes that the Mount of Olives is the location of Jesus' ascension and the promised site of His second coming — connecting the past, present, and future of God's redemptive plan.
