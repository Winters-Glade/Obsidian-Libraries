# Mediterranean

## Description

The Mediterranean Sea is the large inland sea that borders Israel to the west, separating Europe, North Africa, and Asia. In the Bible, it is called the "Great Sea" (Numbers 34:6-7, Joshua 15:47), the "Western Sea" (Deuteronomy 11:24), or the "Sea of the Philistines" (Exodus 23:31). It served as the western boundary of the Promised Land. Major Mediterranean ports in biblical history included Joppa, Caesarea, Tyre, Sidon, and Alexandria. The sea was a highway for commerce and travel — Jonah sailed on it, Solomon's trading fleet sailed on it, and Paul traveled extensively across it on his missionary journeys and ultimately to Rome. The Mediterranean is also prophesied to be the region from which the Antichrist's empire will emerge (Daniel 7, Revelation 13). In the millennial kingdom, the sea's coast will be a territorial inheritance (Ezekiel 47).
