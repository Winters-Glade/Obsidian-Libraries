# Samaria

## Description

Samaria is both a region and a city in the hill country of central Israel, located north of Judea and south of Galilee. The region of Samaria lies between the Jezreel Valley to the north and the Judean hills to the south, with Mount Gerizim and Mount Ebal at its heart. The city of Samaria was built by King Omri of Israel as the capital of the Northern Kingdom around 880 BC (1 Kings 16:23-24). It became a magnificent royal city under the dynasty of Omri and Ahab.

Chuck Smith teaches that Samaria holds a complex place in biblical history. As the capital of the idolatrous Northern Kingdom, it was the center of Baal worship promoted by Ahab and Jezebel, and the target of Elijah and Elisha's prophetic ministries. The city fell to the Assyrians in 722 BC after a three-year siege, resulting in the deportation of the ten tribes. In New Testament times, the Samaritans were a mixed-race people despised by Jews for their syncretistic worship, yet Jesus deliberately ministered to them (John 4, the Samaritan woman at Jacob's well). The early church spread into Samaria through Philip's ministry (Acts 8), and Jesus had commanded His disciples to be witnesses "in Jerusalem, and in all Judea, and in Samaria, and unto the uttermost part of the earth" (Acts 1:8).
