# Berea

## Description

Berea (modern Verria in northern Greece) was a city in Macedonia visited by Paul and Silas during Paul's second missionary journey. After being driven out of Thessalonica by Jewish opposition, Paul and Silas traveled to Berea, where they found a more noble-minded Jewish community. Luke records that the Bereans "received the word with all readiness, and searched the Scriptures daily to find out whether these things were so" (Acts 17:11). Many Bereans believed, both Jews and prominent Greek women and men. However, Jews from Thessalonica followed Paul to Berea and stirred up trouble, forcing him to leave for Athens while Silas and Timothy remained. The Bereans are celebrated as a model of careful, Scripture-based evaluation of teaching.
