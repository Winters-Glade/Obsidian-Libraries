# Myra

## Description

Myra was an ancient city in Lycia, on the southern coast of Asia Minor (modern Demre, Turkey). It was an important port city near the mouth of the Andriacus River. In the New Testament, Myra is mentioned in Acts 27:5-6 as the place where Paul and other prisoners were transferred from one ship to another during their journey to Rome. After sailing along the coast of Cilicia and Pamphylia, they arrived at Myra, where the centurion Julius found an Alexandrian ship bound for Italy and transferred the prisoners to it. Myra was also a significant early Christian center; Saint Nicholas (the historical figure behind the Santa Claus tradition) served as Bishop of Myra in the fourth century. The rock-cut tombs and a well-preserved Roman theater remain notable archaeological features.
