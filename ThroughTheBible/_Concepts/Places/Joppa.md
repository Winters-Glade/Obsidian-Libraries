# Joppa

## Description

Joppa (modern Jaffa, now part of Tel Aviv) is an ancient Mediterranean port city about 35 miles northwest of Jerusalem. It was one of the only natural harbors on the coast of Israel and served as the main port for Jerusalem throughout much of biblical history. In the Old Testament, Joppa was allotted to the tribe of Dan (Joshua 19:46). It was through Joppa that cedar logs from Lebanon were shipped for the construction of both Solomon's Temple (2 Chronicles 2:16) and the second Temple under Zerubbabel (Ezra 3:7). The prophet Jonah fled to Joppa to take a ship to Tarshish to escape God's call to preach to Nineveh (Jonah 1:3). In the New Testament, Peter raised Tabitha (Dorcas) from the dead in Joppa (Acts 9:36-42). While staying in Joppa at the house of Simon the tanner, Peter received the vision of the sheet with unclean animals, teaching him that the gospel is for Gentiles as well as Jews (Acts 10:9-23). This vision led to Cornelius's conversion. Joppa was also a center of early Christian activity.
