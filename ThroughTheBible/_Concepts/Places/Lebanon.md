# Lebanon

## Description

Lebanon is a mountainous region along the eastern Mediterranean coast, north of Israel, known throughout Scripture for its majestic cedar forests, snow-capped peaks, and abundant water sources. The Lebanon mountain range consists of two parallel ranges: Mount Lebanon and the Anti-Lebanon range (with Mount Hermon). The "cedars of Lebanon" were prized for construction — Solomon used them for the Temple and his palace (1 Kings 5:6-18; 2 Chronicles 2:8-10), and they were also used in the second Temple (Ezra 3:7). The psalmists and prophets frequently reference Lebanon for its beauty and grandeur (Psalm 104:16-17, Song of Solomon 4:8, Isaiah 35:2). Isaiah prophesied that the Messiah would be "a root out of dry ground" and that Lebanon would fall before Him (Isaiah 29:17). Hosea describes Israel's unfaithfulness as being "like a stubborn heifer" — yet God's love for them is like the fragrance of Lebanon (Hosea 14:5-7). The region was part of the Promised Land but was never fully subdued by Israel.
