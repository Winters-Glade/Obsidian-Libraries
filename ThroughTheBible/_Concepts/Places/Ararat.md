# Ararat

## Description

Ararat is a mountainous region in eastern Asia Minor (modern eastern Turkey, near the borders with Armenia and Iran). It is most famous as the resting place of Noah's Ark after the Flood (Genesis 8:4): "the ark rested in the seventh month, on the seventeenth day of the month, upon the mountains of Ararat." The highest peak, Mount Ararat (5,137 m / 16,854 ft), is a snow-capped volcanic mountain that dominates the landscape. In 2 Kings 19:37 and Isaiah 37:38, Ararat (also spelled "Armenia") is mentioned as the land where the sons of Sennacherib fled after assassinating their father. The region has been the site of numerous Ark expeditions. Chuck Smith affirmed the historical reality of Noah's Ark and the Flood, noting that the ark's resting place in Ararat testifies to the truth of God's judgment and mercy.
