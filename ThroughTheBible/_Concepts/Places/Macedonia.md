# Macedonia

## Description

Macedonia was a Roman province in the northern part of Greece, bordered by the Aegean Sea to the east and Illyria to the west. Its major cities included Philippi, Thessalonica, and Berea — all key sites in Paul's missionary work. Macedonia holds special significance because Paul received the "Macedonian call" — a vision of a man pleading "Come over to Macedonia and help us" (Acts 16:9-10) — which led to the gospel entering Europe for the first time. Paul planted churches in Philippi, Thessalonica, and Berea during his second missionary journey (Acts 16-17). The Macedonian churches were notable for their generosity despite deep poverty (2 Corinthians 8:1-5), giving sacrificially for the relief of believers in Jerusalem. Paul wrote 1 and 2 Thessalonians to the church in Thessalonica, the capital of the province. Macedonia remained a strong center of Christianity throughout the early centuries.
