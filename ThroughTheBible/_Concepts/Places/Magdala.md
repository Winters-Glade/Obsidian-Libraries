# Magdala

## Description

Magdala (Aramaic: "tower") was a town on the western shore of the Sea of Galilee, about 3 miles northwest of Tiberias. It is best known as the hometown of Mary Magdalene (Mary of Magdala), the devoted disciple from whom Jesus cast out seven demons (Luke 8:2). Magdala was a prosperous fishing and fish-processing center in the first century (known also by the Greek name Taricheae, "fish-salting factories"). It had a large synagogue (the "Magdala Stone" synagogue was discovered in 2009, featuring a carved stone relief of the Second Temple menorah). In the Talmud, Magdala is noted for its wealth and immorality. Jesus may have visited the area frequently. The town declined after the Jewish revolt of AD 66-70.
