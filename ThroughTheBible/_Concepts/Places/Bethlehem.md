# Bethlehem

## Description

Bethlehem is a small town in the hill country of Judea, approximately 5 miles south of Jerusalem. Its name means "House of Bread." Originally called Ephrath (Genesis 35:16-19), it was a small, seemingly insignificant village — yet it holds immense significance in biblical history as the birthplace of King David and, more importantly, of Jesus Christ the Messiah.

Chuck Smith emphasizes that God's choice of Bethlehem illustrates His pattern of using the humble and small to accomplish His greatest purposes. The prophet Micah (5:2) foretold that Bethlehem, "though you are little among the clans of Judah, out of you shall come forth to Me the One to be Ruler in Israel." This prophecy was fulfilled when Jesus was born there during the census of Quirinius (Luke 2:1-7). Bethlehem was also the setting for the story of Ruth and Boaz, the ancestors of David and Jesus.

Pastor Smith notes that Bethlehem's significance lies not in its size or prominence but in God's sovereign choice — a theme that runs throughout Scripture.
