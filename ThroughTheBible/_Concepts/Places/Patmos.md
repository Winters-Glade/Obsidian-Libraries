# Patmos

## Description

Patmos is a small rocky Greek island in the Aegean Sea, part of the Dodecanese archipelago, about 40 miles off the coast of Asia Minor (modern Turkey). It is world-famous as the place where the apostle John received the Revelation of Jesus Christ while in exile "for the word of God and for the testimony of Jesus Christ" (Revelation 1:9). John was banished to Patmos during the reign of the Roman emperor Domitian (c. AD 95), likely sent there as a political prisoner to work in the island's mines. While on the island, John received the apocalyptic visions recorded in the Book of Revelation — including the seven letters to the seven churches of Asia, the throne room of God, the seals, trumpets, bowls, the final judgment, and the New Jerusalem. The cave where tradition holds John received the Revelation is now the Monastery of the Apocalypse, a major pilgrimage site. Patmos represents the sovereignty of God in using even exile and suffering to reveal His purposes to His people.
