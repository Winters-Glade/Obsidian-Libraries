# Coastal Plain of Israel

The narrow strip of fertile land along the Mediterranean coast of Israel, historically a major trade route known as the Via Maris connecting Egypt to Mesopotamia. This region includes the Plain of Sharon, the Plain of Philistia, and key biblical cities such as Joppa, Caesarea, Ashdod, and Gaza. It was a contested frontier between Israel and its coastal neighbors.
