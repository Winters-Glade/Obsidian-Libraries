# Gath

## Description

Gath was one of the five principal cities of the Philistines (the Philistine pentapolis: Gaza, Ashdod, Ashkelon, Gath, Ekron), located about 12 miles east of Ashdod in the coastal plain. It is most famous as the home of Goliath, the giant warrior whom David defeated (1 Samuel 17:4). David twice sought refuge in Gath — once with the king Achish (1 Samuel 21:10-15) and later when he was given the city of Ziklag (1 Samuel 27). Gath was known for producing large warriors; descendants of the giants (Rephaim) were found there (2 Samuel 21:18-22). It was captured by King Uzziah of Judah (2 Chronicles 26:6) but later retaken. The city was destroyed by the Assyrian king Sargon II in 711 BC. The site is identified with Tell es-Safi in modern Israel. Excavations have revealed significant Philistine archaeological remains.
