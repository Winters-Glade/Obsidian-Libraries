# Egypt

## Description

Egypt is one of the world's oldest civilizations, located along the Nile River in northeastern Africa. In the Bible, Egypt plays a dual role — as a place of refuge and provision (Joseph, the infant Jesus) and as a place of bondage and idolatry (the oppression of the Israelites, the Exodus). The Nile River was the lifeblood of Egypt, providing irrigation for crops in an otherwise arid landscape.

Chuck Smith teaches that Egypt represents the world system in contrast to the Promised Land — a place of human reliance, worldly wisdom, and spiritual bondage. The Exodus from Egypt under Moses is the foundational redemption event of the Old Testament, prefiguring Christ's redemption of His people from sin. Pastor Smith often contrasts Egypt (slavery, judgment, plagues) with the Promised Land (freedom, blessing, God's provision). The ten plagues demonstrated God's supremacy over the Egyptian gods, and the Passover lamb is a direct type of Christ.

Egypt also served as a place of refuge for the holy family (Matthew 2:13-15) and was the home of significant Jewish and early Christian communities, particularly in Alexandria.
