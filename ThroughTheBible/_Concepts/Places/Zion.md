# Zion

## Description

Zion originally referred to the Jebusite fortress that David captured and renamed the City of David — the southeastern hill of Jerusalem. Over time, the term expanded to include the Temple Mount and eventually became a poetic name for all of Jerusalem and even the entire people of God. In biblical poetry and prophecy, Zion represents the dwelling place of God, the center of His redemptive purposes, and the object of His affection.

Chuck Smith teaches that Zion is rich with theological meaning. It is the place where God dwells among His people, the mountain from which salvation goes forth, and the ultimate destination of the redeemed. In the Psalms, Zion is celebrated as the city of the Great King. In the prophets, it is both rebuked for its sin and promised glorious restoration. In the New Testament, the writer of Hebrews speaks of the heavenly Zion — "the city of the living God, the heavenly Jerusalem" (Hebrews 12:22). Pastor Smith often connects Zion to the millennial reign of Christ, when the "law will go forth from Zion" (Isaiah 2:3) and the mountain of the Lord's house will be established above all others.
