# Shechem

## Description

Shechem was one of the most historically significant cities in the Old Testament, located in the hill country of Ephraim, between Mount Gerizim and Mount Ebal, about 35 miles north of Jerusalem. It was the first place Abraham stopped in Canaan, where God promised him the land (Genesis 12:6-7). Jacob bought land there and erected an altar (Genesis 33:18-20). Joseph's bones were buried at Shechem (Joshua 24:32). Joshua gathered Israel at Shechem for a covenant renewal ceremony in which blessings and curses were pronounced from Mount Gerizim and Mount Ebal (Joshua 8:30-35, 24:1-28). Shechem was a Levitical city and a city of refuge (Joshua 21:21). Abimelech, the son of Gideon, made himself king there in a violent episode (Judges 9). After Solomon's death, Rehoboam went to Shechem to be crowned, but Jeroboam led the northern tribes in rebellion at Shechem, making it the first capital of the northern kingdom of Israel (1 Kings 12). In the New Testament, Jesus conversed with the Samaritan woman at Jacob's well near Sychar, the New Testament name for the area (John 4:5-42).
