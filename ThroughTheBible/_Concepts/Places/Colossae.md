# Colossae

## Description

Colossae was a Phrygian city in southwest Asia Minor (near modern Honaz, Turkey), located on the Lycus River about 100 miles east of Ephesus. It was situated near the cities of Laodicea and Hierapolis. The church at Colossae was likely founded by Epaphras, a convert of Paul's ministry in Ephesus (Colossians 1:7-8). Paul wrote his Epistle to the Colossians to address false teaching (an early form of Gnosticism or Jewish mysticism emphasizing angel worship and asceticism). The letter emphasizes the preeminence and sufficiency of Christ. Though Paul had not personally visited Colossae at the time of writing (Colossians 2:1), he sent Tychicus and Onesimus there. The city declined after an earthquake in the first century AD and was eventually abandoned. Colossae represents the challenge of maintaining Christ-centered doctrine in the face of syncretistic teaching.
