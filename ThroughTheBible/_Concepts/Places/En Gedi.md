# En Gedi

## Description

En Gedi ("spring of the young goat") is an oasis on the western shore of the Dead Sea, about 35 miles southeast of Jerusalem. It is renowned for its lush vegetation, freshwater springs, waterfalls, and dramatic desert cliffs. In the Old Testament, it was allotted to the tribe of Judah (Joshua 15:62). It is most famously the wilderness area where David sought refuge from King Saul. Saul pursued David into the "strongholds of En Gedi," and David cut off a corner of Saul's robe in a cave while sparing his life (1 Samuel 24:1-22). En Gedi was also known for its vineyards (Song of Solomon 1:14) and its palm trees. Ezekiel 47:10 prophesies that fishermen will spread their nets at En Gedi when the waters of the Dead Sea are healed. The site is a nature preserve today, known for its ibex, hyrax, and unique desert ecosystem.
