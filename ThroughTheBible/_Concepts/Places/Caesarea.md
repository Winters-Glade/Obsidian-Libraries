# Caesarea

## Description

Caesarea Maritima was a major Roman port city on the Mediterranean coast of Israel, about 35 miles north of Joppa. Built by Herod the Great (22-10 BC) in honor of Caesar Augustus, it featured a massive artificial harbor, a hippodrome, a theater, an aqueduct, and a palace. It served as the capital of the Roman province of Judea and the official residence of Roman governors (including Pontius Pilate, Felix, and Festus). Caesarea is prominent in Acts: Philip the evangelist settled there (Acts 21:8), Peter baptized Cornelius the centurion there (Acts 10), Herod Agrippa I died there (Acts 12:19-23), and Paul was imprisoned there for two years before appealing to Caesar (Acts 23-26). It was also the home of the historian Eusebius. Chuck Smith notes Caesarea as a place where the gospel crossed cultural boundaries, particularly in Cornelius's conversion.
