# Ephraim

## Description

Ephraim was both a tribe of Israel and the territory assigned to that tribe in the central hill country of Canaan. Named after Joseph's second son, Ephraim was blessed by Jacob to be greater than Manasseh (Genesis 48:13-20). The tribe's territory included the cities of Bethel, Shiloh, Shechem, and Timnath-serah, making it geographically and strategically central in the land.

Because Ephraim was the leading tribe of the Northern Kingdom of Israel, the name "Ephraim" is often used by the prophets as a synonym for the entire northern kingdom. Chuck Smith notes that the prophets frequently address Ephraim as a way of speaking to the whole nation of Israel in its apostasy. Ephraim's idolatry, alliances with foreign powers, and eventual judgment by Assyria serve as warnings against spiritual unfaithfulness. Yet the prophets also speak of Ephraim's restoration in the last days — God's mercy triumphing over judgment.
