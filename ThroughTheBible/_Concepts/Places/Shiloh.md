# Shiloh

## Description

Shiloh was an ancient city in the hill country of Ephraim, about 20 miles north of Jerusalem and 10 miles south of Shechem. It was the religious capital of Israel for over 300 years during the period of the judges (Joshua 18:1). After the conquest of Canaan, the Tabernacle was set up at Shiloh, and it became the central sanctuary where the Ark of the Covenant was kept. It was at Shiloh that the land was divided among the tribes (Joshua 18-19). Hannah prayed at Shiloh for a son, and Samuel served there as a boy under Eli the priest (1 Samuel 1-3). The Lord called Samuel in the night at Shiloh. However, Shiloh was eventually abandoned because of Israel's sin and the Philistine capture of the Ark (Psalm 78:60-64, Jeremiah 7:12-15). The prophets Jeremiah and Micah used Shiloh's destruction as a warning to Jerusalem and the Temple. The site (modern Khirbet Seilun) was excavated and found to have been destroyed c. 1050 BC.
