# Jabbok

## Description

The Jabbok River (modern Wadi Zarqa) is the largest tributary of the Jordan River on the east side, flowing westward through the region of Gilead and emptying into the Jordan about 20 miles north of the Dead Sea. It marks the boundary between the territories of Ammon and the tribes of Reuben and Gad. Its most significant biblical event is Jacob's encounter with God at the Jabbok. After sending his family and possessions across the ford of Jabbok, Jacob wrestled with a "Man" (identified as the Angel of the Lord) throughout the night. Jacob prevailed and was renamed Israel ("he who struggles with God"), and his hip was dislocated (Genesis 32:22-32). This event is a powerful symbol of persevering prayer, the struggle of faith, and the transformation that comes through encountering God.
