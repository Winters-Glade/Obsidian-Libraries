# Malta

## Description

Malta is a small island country in the central Mediterranean Sea, about 50 miles south of Sicily. In the Bible, it is called Melita. Paul was shipwrecked on Malta during his voyage to Rome (Acts 27:27-28:10). After surviving a violent storm for 14 days, the ship ran aground on a reef at "a place where two seas met" — traditionally identified as St. Paul's Bay. All 276 people on board survived. On the island, Paul and his companions were shown "unusual kindness" by the natives. When Paul gathered brushwood for a fire, a viper fastened onto his hand. The islanders expected him to swell up or fall dead, but when he shook the viper off unharmed, they thought he was a god. Paul healed Publius's father of fever and dysentery, and then healed others on the island. The Maltese people honored the party with gifts when they departed three months later.
