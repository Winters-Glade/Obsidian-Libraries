# Rome

## Description

Rome was the capital of the Roman Empire and the largest city of the ancient world, with a population estimated at over one million during the first century AD. Located on the Tiber River in central Italy, it was the political, military, legal, and cultural center of the Mediterranean world. The city was built on seven hills and boasted magnificent architecture including the Forum, the Colosseum, the Pantheon, and aqueducts that supplied water from distant mountains.

Chuck Smith teaches that Rome represents the world power that both opposed and eventually adopted Christianity. Paul wrote his most systematic theological work, the Epistle to the Romans, to the church in Rome — a church he had not yet visited but longed to see. Paul was eventually imprisoned in Rome (house arrest, Acts 28) and likely martyred there under Nero around AD 64-67. Peter also ministered and was martyred in Rome. The Roman church grew despite persecution under Nero, Domitian, and later emperors.

Pastor Smith emphasizes that Rome was both the stage for the spread of the gospel and the source of the persecution that purified the early church. Paul's journey to Rome (Acts 27-28) is a major narrative in Acts, showing how God orchestrated events to bring the gospel to the heart of the empire. The Book of Revelation was written to churches under Roman domination.
