# Gilead

## Description

Gilead was a mountainous region east of the Jordan River, extending from the Jabbok River in the south to the Yarmuk River in the north. It was allotted to the tribes of Reuben, Gad, and the half-tribe of Manasseh (Numbers 32). Known for its dense forests, rich pastures, and healing balm (Jeremiah 8:22, 46:11), Gilead was famed for its spices and medicinal resins. The "balm of Gilead" became a metaphor for healing and restoration. Several significant biblical events occurred in Gilead: Jacob and Laban made a covenant there on Mount Gilead (Genesis 31:44-53); Jephthah the Gileadite judged Israel from Mizpah in Gilead (Judges 10:17-11:40); David sought refuge there during Absalom's rebellion (2 Samuel 17:22-29); and Elijah the Tishbite came from Gilead (1 Kings 17:1).
