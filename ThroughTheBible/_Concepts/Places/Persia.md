# Persia

## Description

Persia (modern Iran) was the great empire that succeeded Babylon as the dominant power in the ancient Near East. Under Cyrus the Great (559-530 BC), the Persians conquered Babylon in 539 BC and established the largest empire the world had yet seen, stretching from India to Ethiopia. The Persian Empire was known for its efficient administration, the system of satraps (provincial governors), its postal system, and its generally tolerant policy toward subject peoples.

Chuck Smith emphasizes Persia as God's instrument for the restoration of Israel. Cyrus the Great issued the decree allowing the Jewish exiles to return to Jerusalem and rebuild the Temple (Ezra 1:1-4), fulfilling Isaiah's prophecy made over 150 years earlier (Isaiah 44:28-45:1). Pastor Smith notes that Persia is a powerful example of God's sovereignty over world history — He raises up and brings down rulers according to His purposes. The books of Ezra, Nehemiah, Esther, Daniel, and Chronicles were all written during the Persian period.

The Persian kings mentioned in the Bible include Cyrus, Darius I, Xerxes (Ahasuerus, husband of Esther), and Artaxerxes I and II.
