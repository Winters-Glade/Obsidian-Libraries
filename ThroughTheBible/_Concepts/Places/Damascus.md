# Damascus

## Description

Damascus is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, located in southern Syria about 55 miles northeast of the Sea of Galilee. In the Old Testament, Damascus was the capital of Aram (Syria) and frequently played a role in Israel's history. Abraham's steward Eliezer came from Damascus (Genesis 15:2). King David conquered the city (2 Samuel 8:5-6), and later it was the capital of the Aramean kingdom that opposed Israel. The prophets Isaiah (Isaiah 17:1) and Jeremiah (Jeremiah 49:23-27) pronounced judgment on Damascus. In the New Testament, Damascus is most famous as the place of Paul's conversion. On the road to Damascus, Saul of Tarsus encountered the risen Christ (Acts 9:1-19), was blinded, led into the city, and was healed and baptized by Ananias. Paul later preached in Damascus and escaped the city by being lowered in a basket through an opening in the wall (Acts 9:23-25).
