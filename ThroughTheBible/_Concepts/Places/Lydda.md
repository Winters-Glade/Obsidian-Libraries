# Lydda

## Description

Lydda (Hebrew: Lod) was a town in the Plain of Sharon, about 25 miles northwest of Jerusalem and 10 miles southeast of Joppa. In the Old Testament, Lod was founded by the tribe of Benjamin and was rebuilt after the Babylonian exile (1 Chronicles 8:12, Ezra 2:33, Nehemiah 7:37). In the New Testament, Lydda became a center of early Christian activity. Peter visited the saints in Lydda and healed Aeneas, a man who had been paralyzed for eight years, saying "Jesus the Christ heals you" (Acts 9:32-35). This miracle caused all who lived in Lydda and Sharon to turn to the Lord. The event preceded Peter's call to Joppa to raise Tabitha (Dorcas) and his subsequent vision leading to Cornelius's conversion. Lydda represents a key stage in the spread of the gospel through the apostolic ministry of healing.
