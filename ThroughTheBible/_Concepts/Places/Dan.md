# Dan

## Description

Dan was a city in the northernmost part of Israel, located at the foot of Mount Hermon near one of the sources of the Jordan River. Originally called Laish, it was conquered by the tribe of Dan during the period of the judges (Judges 18). The phrase "from Dan to Beersheba" came to represent the entire extent of the Promised Land, from north to south.

Dan is infamous in biblical history as the site where Jeroboam I erected one of two golden calves for the Northern Kingdom to worship (1 Kings 12:25-33), establishing a rival religious center to Jerusalem. This "sin of Jeroboam" became a recurring indictment against the kings of Israel. The Tel Dan Stele, discovered in 1993, contains the first extra-biblical reference to the "House of David," confirming the historical existence of David's dynasty.

Chuck Smith highlights Dan as both a geographical marker for the land of Israel and a symbol of religious apostasy — a place that was meant to be part of God's inheritance but became a center of idolatry.
