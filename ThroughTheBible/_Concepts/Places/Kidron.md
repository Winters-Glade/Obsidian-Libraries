# Kidron

## Description

The Kidron Valley is the deep ravine that runs along the eastern wall of Jerusalem, separating the city from the Mount of Olives. It is mentioned frequently in the Bible in connection with significant events. In the Old Testament, David crossed the Kidron while fleeing from Absalom's rebellion (2 Samuel 15:23). King Asa burned his grandmother Maacah's idol in the Kidron Valley (1 Kings 15:13). During the reforms of Kings Hezekiah (2 Chronicles 29:16) and Josiah (2 Kings 23:4-12), the Kidron was the place where idolatrous objects were taken and burned. Jeremiah prophesied that the valley would become a burial ground in judgment (Jeremiah 31:40). In the New Testament, Jesus crossed the Kidron with His disciples on the night of His betrayal, entering the Garden of Gethsemane (John 18:1). The Kidron is also associated with the Valley of Jehoshaphat, the site of future judgment in Joel's prophecy (Joel 3:2,12).
