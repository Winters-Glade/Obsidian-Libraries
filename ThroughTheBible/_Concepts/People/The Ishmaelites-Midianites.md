# The Ishmaelites/Midianites

## Description

The Ishmaelites and Midianites were two related groups of traders from the Arabian desert who passed through Canaan with caravans carrying gum, balm, and myrrh from Gilead to Egypt. They purchased Joseph from his jealous brothers for twenty shekels of silver and then sold him as a slave in Egypt. The terms Ishmaelites and Midianites are used somewhat interchangeably in the Joseph narrative, suggesting a mixed group of desert traders. Chuck Smith highlights God's sovereign hand working through this cruel transaction to eventually position Joseph to save his family.
