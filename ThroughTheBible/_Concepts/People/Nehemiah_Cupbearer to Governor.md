# Nehemiah: Cupbearer to Governor

Nehemiah served as cupbearer to the Persian king Artaxerxes I before being appointed governor of Judah to rebuild Jerusalem's walls (Nehemiah 1-2). The cupbearer was a position of high trust, responsible for tasting the king's wine to ensure it wasn't poisoned, which placed Nehemiah in a strategic position to request favor for his people.
