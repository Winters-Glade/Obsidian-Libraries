# Augustine

## Description

Augustine of Hippo (AD 354–430) was the most influential theologian of the Western Church, whose writings shaped Christian doctrine on grace, salvation, the Trinity, and the Church. His works *Confessions* and *City of God* remain classics of both theology and Western literature. Converted from a life of pagan indulgence and Manichaeism through the prayers of his mother Monica and the preaching of Ambrose, Augustine became Bishop of Hippo in North Africa. He defended orthodox Christianity against Donatism, Pelagianism, and Manichaeism. His doctrines of original sin, divine grace, and predestination deeply influenced later Reformers including Luther and Calvin. Key contributions include his teaching on the sovereignty of God in salvation, the unity of the Old and New Testaments, and the concept of just war. His hermeneutical principles for interpreting Scripture are foundational to Christian exegesis.
