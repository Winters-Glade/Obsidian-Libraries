# Justin Martyr

## Description

Justin Martyr (c. AD 100–165) was the foremost Christian apologist of the second century and a key figure in the early Church's engagement with Greek philosophy. A native of Flavia Neapolis (modern Nablus), he converted to Christianity after a long philosophical search through Stoicism, Aristotelianism, Pythagoreanism, and Platonism. He founded a school of Christian philosophy in Rome. His two *Apologies* defend Christianity as the true philosophy, arguing that Christ is the Logos (the divine Reason) through whom all truth is known. His *Dialogue with Trypho* records his debate with a Jewish scholar and presents Christ as the fulfillment of the Old Testament. He was martyred in Rome under Marcus Aurelius.
