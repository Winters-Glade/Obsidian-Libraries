# Luke the Evangelist

## Description

Luke the Evangelist is the traditional author of the Gospel of Luke and the Acts of the Apostles, which together comprise over 27% of the New Testament — more than any other writer. He was a physician by profession (Colossians 4:14), a Gentile (likely the only non-Jewish author of the New Testament), and a close companion of the Apostle Paul during his missionary journeys. Luke is known for his meticulous historical research (Luke 1:1-4), his emphasis on the universal scope of the gospel, his attention to the role of women and the poor, and his detailed accounts of Jesus' birth, parables, and resurrection appearances.
