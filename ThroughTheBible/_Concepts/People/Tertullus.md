# Tertullus

## Description

Tertullus was a Roman orator (rhetorician) whom the Jewish leaders hired to present their case against the apostle Paul before Governor Felix in Caesarea (Acts 24). His flattering opening address to Felix — "We enjoy great peace through you" — is a classic example of rhetorical flattery. He accused Paul of being a plague, a troublemaker, and a ringleader of the Nazarene sect who had attempted to desecrate the Temple. Paul's masterful defense followed, refuting each charge. Tertullus's role, though brief, illustrates the legal and rhetorical practices of the Roman provincial court system and the opposition Paul faced from Jewish religious authorities who employed professional advocates.
