# Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego

## Description

Shadrach (Hananiah), Meshach (Mishael), and Abednego (Azariah) were three Jewish youths taken captive to Babylon along with Daniel. They refused to bow to the golden image erected by King Nebuchadnezzar, even under threat of death in a fiery furnace. Their response to the king is a classic statement of faith: "Our God whom we serve is able to deliver us from the burning fiery furnace, and He will deliver us from your hand, O king. But if not, let it be known to you, O king, that we do not serve your gods, nor will we worship the gold image which you have set up" (Daniel 3:17-18). God honored their faith: they were thrown into the furnace but were unharmed, and a fourth figure — "like the Son of God" — appeared with them in the fire. Chuck Smith uses their account as a supreme example of uncompromising faith and the sovereignty of God over human rulers. Their story demonstrates that God may not always deliver from trials, but He is always with His people in them.
