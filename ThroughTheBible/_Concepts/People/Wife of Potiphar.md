# Wife of Potiphar

## Description

Potiphar's wife, whose name is not recorded in Scripture, was the Egyptian noblewoman who attempted to seduce Joseph while he served as steward of Potiphar's household. When Joseph repeatedly refused her advances on the grounds that it would be a sin against God and a betrayal of Potiphar's trust, she eventually caught him by his garment and falsely accused him of assault. Her accusation led to Joseph's imprisonment, which became a crucial step in God's sovereign plan to elevate Joseph to second-in-command over Egypt. Chuck Smith often uses this account to illustrate the importance of moral integrity, the fear of God as a deterrent to sin, and the principle that God uses unjust suffering to prepare His servants for greater responsibility. Potiphar's wife represents the ongoing temptation to compromise and the slander that often follows those who stand firm in their convictions.
