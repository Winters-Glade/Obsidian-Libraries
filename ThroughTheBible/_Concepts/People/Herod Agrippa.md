# Herod Agrippa

## Description

Herod Agrippa I (10 BC – AD 44) was the grandson of Herod the Great and the king of Judea who persecuted the early church. He executed James the apostle (the brother of John) with the sword — the first apostolic martyr — and imprisoned Peter, intending to execute him after Passover (Acts 12). An angel miraculously freed Peter, and the church prayed fervently. Agrippa died a dramatic death: during a public address at Caesarea, the people hailed him as a god, and because he did not give glory to God, he was struck down and eaten by worms (Acts 12:20-23). His son, Herod Agrippa II, later heard Paul's defense (Acts 25-26). Agrippa's persecution of the church and his tragic end illustrate God's protection of His people and the judgment due to those who oppose His work.
