# Festus (Porcius Festus)

## Description

Porcius Festus succeeded Felix as Roman procurator of Judea. He heard Paul's case and, seeking to appease the Jews, proposed a trial in Jerusalem. Paul, exercising his right as a Roman citizen, appealed to Caesar, which Festus granted. Festus discussed Paul's case with King Agrippa and remarked that Paul was not guilty of any capital crime (Acts 25-26).
