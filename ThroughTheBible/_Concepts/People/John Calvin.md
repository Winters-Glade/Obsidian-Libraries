# John Calvin

## Description

John Calvin (1509–1564) was a French theologian and pastor who became the leading figure of the Protestant Reformation in Switzerland. His *Institutes of the Christian Religion* (first published 1536) is one of the most influential systematic theologies ever written. Calvin's teaching emphasized the sovereignty of God in all things, including salvation (unconditional election, predestination). His biblical commentaries and expository sermons set a standard for Reformed biblical exegesis. Calvin established a theocratic government in Geneva that became a model for Reformed churches throughout Europe. His "Calvinist" doctrines — often summarized as TULIP (Total depravity, Unconditional election, Limited atonement, Irresistible grace, Perseverance of the saints) — deeply influenced the Puritans, the Reformed tradition, and many evangelical movements. Despite differences in eschatology and ecclesiology, his emphasis on the authority of Scripture and the centrality of Christ resonates with the expository teaching tradition represented in this vault.
