# Jephthah

## Description

Jephthah was a mighty warrior and one of the judges of Israel, who delivered Israel from the Ammonites after the sons of Gilead had driven him out because he was the son of a harlot. He made a vow to the Lord: if victorious, whatever came out of his house to meet him on his return would be offered to God. Tragically, his only daughter came out to greet him with timbrels and dancing. Jephthah's vow is one of the most difficult and debated passages in the Old Testament. Chuck Smith teaches that Jephthah's rash vow illustrates the danger of making hasty promises to God and the importance of understanding God's character before making vows. Despite his flawed background and tragic mistake, Jephthah is listed in the hall of faith (Hebrews 11:32), showing that God uses imperfect people to accomplish His purposes.
