# Felix

## Description

Antonius Felix was the Roman procurator of Judea before whom Paul was tried (Acts 23-24). He kept Paul imprisoned for two years, hoping for a bribe. He often conversed with Paul but was convicted by Paul's message about righteousness, self-control, and the judgment to come. He left Paul in chains when he was succeeded by Porcius Festus.
