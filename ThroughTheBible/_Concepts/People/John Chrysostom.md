# John Chrysostom

## Description

John Chrysostom (c. AD 347–407) was the Archbishop of Constantinople and one of the most influential early Church Fathers. Known as "Chrysostom" (Greek: "golden-mouthed") for his eloquent preaching, he is remembered for his expository sermons on the Gospels and Paul's Epistles, his strong stance against abuses of power, and his emphasis on practical Christian living. He served as a presbyter in Antioch before becoming patriarch of Constantinople, where his moral reforms and outspoken criticism of the imperial court led to his exile. His liturgical contributions include the Divine Liturgy of St. John Chrysostom still used in Eastern churches. His homilies on the Bible remain a treasure of early Christian interpretation, emphasizing the literal and moral sense of Scripture.
