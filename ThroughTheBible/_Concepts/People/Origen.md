# Origen

## Description

Origen (c. AD 184–253) was one of the most prolific and influential scholars of the early Church, based in Alexandria and later Caesarea. He is known for his extensive biblical commentaries, his work *On First Principles* (the first systematic theology), and the Hexapla — a six-column critical edition of the Old Testament juxtaposing Hebrew and Greek versions. Origen emphasized the allegorical and spiritual interpretation of Scripture, seeking deeper meanings beyond the literal text. His teachings on the preexistence of souls, universal restoration (apokatastasis), and the subordination of the Son within the Trinity were later deemed controversial. Despite this, his contributions to biblical textual criticism, apologetics, and Christian theology are immense. He died from injuries sustained under the Decian persecution.
