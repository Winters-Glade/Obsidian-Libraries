# Jacob/Israel

## Description

Jacob, later renamed Israel by God, was the son of Isaac and Rebekah, the grandson of Abraham, and the father of the twelve tribes of Israel. His life was marked by struggle — from wrestling with his twin brother Esau in the womb to his famous wrestling match with God at Peniel. After his transformative encounter, God changed his name to Israel, meaning "one who strives with God and prevails." Chuck Smith traces Jacob's spiritual journey from a scheming deceiver to a man broken and blessed by God, illustrating the pattern of grace in which God chooses the unworthy and transforms them through divine encounter.
