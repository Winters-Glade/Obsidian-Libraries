# Pontius Pilate

## Description

Pontius Pilate was the Roman governor of Judea who presided over the trial of Jesus. Though he found no fault in Jesus and sought to release Him, he ultimately capitulated to the crowd's demand for crucifixion. He famously washed his hands to symbolize his innocence. His rule was marked by insensitivity to Jewish customs and harsh governance.
