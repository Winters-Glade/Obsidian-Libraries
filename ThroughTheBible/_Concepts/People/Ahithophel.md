# Ahithophel

## Description

Ahithophel was David's trusted counselor whose wisdom was "as if one had inquired at the oracle of God." During Absalom's rebellion, Ahithophel deserted David and joined Absalom, offering strategic counsel that threatened David's kingdom. When Absalom rejected Ahithophel's advice in favor of Hushai's (David's spy), Ahithophel knew the rebellion would fail, went home, set his house in order, and hanged himself (2 Samuel 17:23). His suicide parallels Judas Iscariot — both were trusted men who betrayed their master and took their own lives. Chuck Smith often uses Ahithophel as a cautionary example of human wisdom without God, the bitterness of betrayal, and the consequences of forsaking loyalty.
