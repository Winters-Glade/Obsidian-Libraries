# Jael

## Description

Jael was the wife of Heber the Kenite who killed Sisera, the Canaanite commander, during the time of the judges. After Sisera's army was defeated by Barak and Deborah at the Kishon River, Sisera fled on foot and sought refuge in Jael's tent. She welcomed him, gave him milk to drink, and while he slept from exhaustion, she drove a tent peg through his temple, killing him. Deborah's song celebrates Jael as "most blessed among women" (Judges 5:24-27). Her decisive action brought about the deliverance of Israel from 20 years of Canaanite oppression. Chuck Smith presents Jael as a surprising instrument of God's deliverance — a woman who acted with courage and decisiveness when the moment demanded it.
