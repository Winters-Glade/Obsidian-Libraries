# Zacchaeus

## Description

Zacchaeus was a chief tax collector in Jericho who climbed a sycamore tree to see Jesus because he was short of stature. Jesus called him down and invited Himself to Zacchaeus's house, prompting the crowd to grumble that Jesus would stay with a "sinner." Zacchaeus repented, declaring he would give half his goods to the poor and restore fourfold anything he had taken dishonestly. Jesus responded, "Today salvation has come to this house" (Luke 19:9). Zacchaeus is a powerful example of the transformative power of an encounter with Christ — a wealthy, despised collaborator with Rome who was radically converted. Chuck Smith often uses this account to illustrate that no one is beyond the reach of God's grace and that genuine repentance produces visible fruit.
