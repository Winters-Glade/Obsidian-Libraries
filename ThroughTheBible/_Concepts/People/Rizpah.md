# Rizpah

## Description

Rizpah was the daughter of Aiah and a concubine of King Saul. After Saul's death, during a period of famine that David learned was due to Saul's bloodshed against the Gibeonites, seven of Saul's descendants (including two of Rizpah's sons) were handed over to the Gibeonites to be hanged. Rizpah's vigil is one of the most poignant scenes in the Old Testament: she spread sackcloth on a rock and guarded the bodies day and night, keeping birds and wild animals away from them, from the beginning of the barley harvest until the rains came. Moved by her devotion, David gathered the bones of Saul and Jonathan and the hanged men for proper burial, after which God responded to the land. Rizpah's story illustrates a mother's fierce love and the importance of justice and proper burial in ancient Israelite culture.
