# Vashti

## Description

Vashti was the queen of King Ahasuerus (Xerxes I) of Persia in the opening chapter of Esther. During a royal banquet, the king summoned her to display her beauty before his drunken guests. She refused, and her refusal led to her deposition. The king's advisors feared that Vashti's disobedience would encourage wives throughout the empire to despise their husbands, so a royal decree was issued banishing Vashti and establishing the principle that every man should rule in his own house. Vashti's removal opened the way for Esther to become queen — a key step in God's providential plan to save the Jewish people from Haman's plot. Vashti is variously viewed as a woman of dignity who refused to be objectified, or as a warning about the consequences of defying authority. Chuck Smith notes that God's sovereignty works through human affairs, even through flawed and fallen human decisions.
