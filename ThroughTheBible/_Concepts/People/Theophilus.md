# Theophilus

## Description

Theophilus (Greek: "friend of God" or "loved by God") is the person to whom both the Gospel of Luke and the Book of Acts are addressed. Luke addresses him as "most excellent Theophilus" (Luke 1:3), suggesting he was a person of high social rank, possibly a Roman official or a wealthy patron supporting Luke's writing. The name may also be symbolic, representing any "lover of God" who reads the account. Luke states his purpose: to write an orderly account so that Theophilus "may know the certainty of those things in which you were instructed" (Luke 1:4). Acts continues the narrative, covering the ascension, Pentecost, and the spread of the gospel to Rome. Theophilus represents the inquiring reader — one who has heard the gospel and seeks a firm foundation for faith.
