# Chief Priests

## Description

The chief priests were the leading religious authorities in Jerusalem during the time of Jesus, drawn primarily from the Sadducean aristocracy. They presided over the Temple, the Sanhedrin, and the religious life of the Jewish people. The Gospels portray them as the primary instigators of Jesus' arrest and crucifixion, delivering Him to Pilate out of envy and fear of losing their authority. Chuck Smith contrasts their religious position with their spiritual blindness, showing how human religious systems can oppose the very God they claim to serve.
