# The Ishmaelites / Midianites

The traders to whom Joseph was sold by his brothers for twenty shekels of silver (Genesis 37:25-28, 39:1). The terms 'Ishmaelites' and 'Midianites' appear interchangeably in the narrative—both were desert trading peoples descended from Abraham. They were traveling from Gilead to Egypt with caravans carrying spices, balm, and myrrh when they purchased Joseph, setting in motion God's plan to preserve His people through a famine.
