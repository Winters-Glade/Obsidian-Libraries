# Thaddaeus

## Description

Thaddaeus was one of the twelve apostles of Jesus, also known as Lebbaeus and as "Judas the son of James" (or "Judas, not Iscariot") in Luke's Gospel and Acts. He is mentioned only in the lists of the apostles (Matthew 10:3, Mark 3:18, Luke 6:16, Acts 1:13). His single recorded question to Jesus occurs at the Last Supper: "Lord, how is it that You will manifest Yourself to us, and not to the world?" (John 14:22). Jesus replied that those who love Him and keep His word will be loved by the Father, and the Father and Son will make their home with them. Tradition holds that Thaddaeus preached the gospel in Syria, Mesopotamia, and Armenia, where he was martyred. He is a reminder that many of Christ's faithful servants serve quietly, asking profound questions and leaving a legacy of devotion.
