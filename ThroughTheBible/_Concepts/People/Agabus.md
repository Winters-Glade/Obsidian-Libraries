# Agabus

## Description

Agabus was a Christian prophet from Jerusalem who appears twice in the Book of Acts. He first came from Jerusalem to Antioch to prophesy a severe famine throughout the Roman world (Acts 11:27-28), which led the disciples to send relief to the brethren in Judea. Years later, he met Paul in Caesarea at the house of Philip the evangelist and performed a symbolic act: taking Paul's belt and binding his own hands and feet, prophesying that the Jews of Jerusalem would bind Paul and deliver him to the Gentiles (Acts 21:10-14). Despite warnings, Paul continued to Jerusalem, demonstrating his willingness to suffer for Christ. Agabus represents the gift of prophecy in the early church as a means of edification and guidance.
