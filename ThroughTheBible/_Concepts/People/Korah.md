# Korah

## Description

Korah was a Levite who led a rebellion against Moses and Aaron in the wilderness, challenging their God-given authority. Along with Dathan, Abiram, and On (Reubenites), and 250 prominent Israelite leaders, Korah argued that the whole congregation was holy and that Moses and Aaron had exalted themselves above the assembly. Moses fell on his face and told the people to separate from the rebels. The earth opened its mouth and swallowed Korah, Dathan, Abiram, their households, and all their possessions. Fire from the Lord consumed the 250 men offering incense. Korah's rebellion is a sobering warning against pride, jealousy, and challenging God's appointed leadership. However, Korah's sons did not perish (Numbers 26:11) and later became gatekeepers and musicians in the Temple, with several Psalms attributed to "the sons of Korah" (Psalms 42, 44-49, 84-85, 87-88).
