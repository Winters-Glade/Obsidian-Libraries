# Ananias (High Priest)

## Description

Ananias son of Nedebaeus was the Jewish high priest from approximately AD 47 to 58, during the governorship of Felix and the trial of the apostle Paul. When Paul was brought before the Sanhedrin, Ananias commanded those standing near him to strike Paul on the mouth. Paul responded sharply, "God will strike you, you whitewashed wall!" but immediately apologized upon learning that Ananias was the high priest, citing Exodus 22:28. Later, Ananias traveled to Caesarea to present the case against Paul before Felix, employing the orator Tertullus (Acts 24:1-9). Ananias was known for his greed, violence, and collaboration with the Romans. He was assassinated by Jewish rebels in AD 66 at the outbreak of the Jewish revolt. He should not be confused with Ananias of Damascus, the disciple who baptized Paul.
