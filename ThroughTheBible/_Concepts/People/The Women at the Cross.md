# The Women at the Cross

## Description

The women at the cross were a group of faithful female followers of Jesus who remained present during His crucifixion when most of the male disciples had fled. They included Mary Magdalene, Mary the mother of James and Joses, Salome, and other women who had ministered to Jesus in Galilee. Their faithfulness at the darkest hour stands in stark contrast to the disciples' abandonment. Chuck Smith often highlights their devotion as an example of sacrificial love and the vital role women played in Jesus' ministry and at the founding events of Christianity.
