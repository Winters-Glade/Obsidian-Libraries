# Amarna Letters

A collection of clay tablets from the 14th century BC discovered at Tell el-Amarna in Egypt, containing diplomatic correspondence between the Egyptian administration and its vassal states in Canaan. These letters provide external historical evidence for the political and social conditions in Canaan before the Israelite conquest, referencing cities, rulers, and the 'Habiru' peoples.
