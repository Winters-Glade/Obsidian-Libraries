# Moses' Choice

The pivotal decision of Moses recorded in Hebrews 11:24-27, where he refused to be called the son of Pharaoh's daughter, choosing rather to suffer affliction with God's people than to enjoy the fleeting pleasures of sin. By faith he forsook Egypt, not fearing the king's wrath, 'for he endured as seeing Him who is invisible.' This choice exemplifies faith that values God's approval above all earthly privilege.
