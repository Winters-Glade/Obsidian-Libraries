# Babylonian Exile

## Description

The Babylonian Exile (586-539 BC) was the period when Judah was conquered by Babylon, Jerusalem was destroyed, and the people were taken into captivity. It lasted 70 years as Jeremiah prophesied, ending when Cyrus of Persia allowed the Jews to return. The exile profoundly shaped Jewish faith, leading to the development of synagogues and a renewed emphasis on Scripture.
