# Remnant

## Definition

A faithful portion of God's people preserved through judgment and exile. The concept runs throughout Scripture — Noah's family, the 7,000 in Elijah's day, the returning exiles, and the faithful Israelite believers in Paul's day. The remnant is both a historical reality and a theological principle of grace.
