# Chief of Sinners

Paul's self-description as the foremost or chief of sinners (1 Timothy 1:15), expressing his deep sense of unworthiness because he had persecuted the church before his conversion. Paul did not use this as a past-tense confession but as a present-tense identity statement, demonstrating that the awareness of one's own sinfulness is the foundation for appreciating grace.
