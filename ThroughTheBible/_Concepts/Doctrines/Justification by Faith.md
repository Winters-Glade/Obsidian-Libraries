# Justification by Faith

## Definition

The judicial act of God whereby He declares the sinner righteous on the basis of faith in Jesus Christ alone. Righteousness is imputed (credited) to the believer through faith, not earned by works. This doctrine, central to Paul's epistles and recovered during the Reformation, affirms that justification is a legal declaration, not an internal transformation.
