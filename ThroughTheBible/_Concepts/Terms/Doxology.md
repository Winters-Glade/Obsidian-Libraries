# Doxology

## Definition

A doxology is a liturgical formula of praise to God, typically expressing glory and honor to the Trinity. In the Bible, doxologies appear throughout the Psalms and the New Testament, often serving as concluding ascriptions of praise that acknowledge God's eternal glory, power, and sovereignty.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Psalm 80_3, 7, 19]] — Doxology concluding Book I of the Psalms
- [[Psalm 111]] — Doxology concluding Book II of the Psalms
- [[Psalm 8]] — Doxology concluding Book III of the Psalms
- [[Psalm 1]] — Doxology concluding Book IV of the Psalms
- [[Psalm 150]] — The final doxology of the entire Psalter
- [[Jude]] — One of the finest New Testament doxologies
- [[Romans 11_33-36]] — Paul's doxology at the depth of God's wisdom
- [[Ephesians 1_3-14]] — A doxology of blessing and praise

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith noted in his C2000 Psalms teachings that each of the five books of the Psalter ends with a doxology, demonstrating the liturgical structure of the Psalms. He frequently pointed to Jude 24-25 as one of the most beautiful doxologies in the New Testament, emphasizing that God is able to keep believers from stumbling and present them blameless before His glory.
