# Kingdom of Heaven

## Definition

"Kingdom of Heaven" (Greek: *basileia tōn ouranōn*) is a distinctive phrase used by the Gospel of Matthew (32 times) to describe the rule and reign of God, both in its present spiritual reality and its future consummation. Matthew uses "Kingdom of Heaven" where the other Gospels typically use "Kingdom of God," likely reflecting his Jewish audience's reverence for the divine name. The phrase encompasses Jesus' proclamation that God's reign has broken into history through His ministry, while also pointing forward to the full establishment of that reign at His return.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Matthew]] — "Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is at hand" — John the Baptist's proclamation.
- [[Matthew 4_17]] — Jesus' first preaching message: "Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is at hand."
- [[Matthew]] — "Blessed are the poor in spirit, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven."
- [[Matthew]] — The seven parables of the Kingdom (the sower, the wheat and tares, the mustard seed, the leaven, the hidden treasure, the pearl of great price, the dragnet).
- [[Matthew 16_19]] — "I will give you the keys of the kingdom of heaven."
- [[Matthew 28_18]] — "All authority has been given to Me in heaven and on earth" — the basis of the Great Commission.

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith's teaching through Matthew's Gospel gave special attention to the Kingdom of Heaven as both a present reality and a future hope. He often distinguished between the "mystery form" of the Kingdom (the present age where believers live under Christ's rule while the wheat and tares grow together) and the future Millennial Kingdom. His interpretation of the parables of the Kingdom in Matthew 13 was a highlight of his C2000 series, where he explained how these stories reveal the progress of the gospel in the present age.
