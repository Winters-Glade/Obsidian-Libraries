# Penitential Psalms

## Definition

The Penitential Psalms are seven psalms of repentance and confession — Psalms 6, 32, 38, 51, 102, 130, and 143 — that express deep sorrow for sin and a heartfelt plea for God's mercy and forgiveness. These psalms have been used throughout church history as prayers of penitence, particularly during Lent, and model the biblical pattern of confession, contrition, and restoration.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Psalm 56]] — A plea for mercy in the midst of suffering
- [[Psalm 32]] — The joy of forgiven sin and confessing transgression
- [[Psalm 8]] — A prayer of confession under God's discipline
- [[Psalm 51]] — David's great prayer of repentance after his sin with Bathsheba
- [[_transcripts/Gods Timing, Psalm 102_13]] — A cry of the afflicted when overwhelmed
- [[Psalm 100]] — "Out of the depths I cry to you" — waiting for redemption
- [[Psalm 43]] — A plea for deliverance and guidance

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith devoted considerable attention to the Penitential Psalms in his C2000 teaching, especially Psalm 51, which he connected to David's repentance after his sin with Bathsheba (2 Samuel 11-12). Smith used these psalms to illustrate the biblical pattern of confession — acknowledging sin, seeking cleansing, and receiving God's restoring grace. He often emphasized that Psalm 32 shows the blessedness of the person whose transgression is forgiven.
