# Lady Wisdom

## Definition

Lady Wisdom (Hebrew: *Hokmot*, Greek: *Sophia*) is the personification of wisdom as a righteous woman in the Book of Proverbs, particularly in chapters 1, 8, and 9. She is depicted as calling out in the public square, inviting all who hear to learn from her. In Proverbs 8, she is described as being present with God at the creation of the world, rejoicing before Him. This powerful personification presents wisdom not as an abstract concept but as a living, desirable quality that leads to life, blessing, and the fear of the Lord.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Proverbs 1]] — "Wisdom calls aloud outside; she raises her voice in the open squares."
- [[_transcripts/The Name of Jesus, Proverbs 18_10]] — Lady Wisdom's invitation: "Does not wisdom cry out, and understanding lift up her voice?"
- [[Proverbs 26-31]] — Wisdom's role in creation: "The LORD possessed me at the beginning of His way, before His works of old."
- [[Proverbs 2_1-6]] — Lady Wisdom builds her house, prepares a feast, and invites the simple to eat and live.
- [[_transcripts/The Name of Jesus, Proverbs 18_10]] — The blessing of finding wisdom: "She is a tree of life to those who take hold of her."

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith's C2000 teaching on Proverbs gave special attention to the personification of wisdom in Proverbs 8, where he connected Lady Wisdom's role in creation to the New Testament revelation of Christ as the "wisdom of God" (1 Corinthians 1:24). He taught that the invitation of Lady Wisdom is ultimately fulfilled in the gospel call of Jesus Christ, who invites all to come to Him and find life. This Christ-centered interpretation was characteristic of his approach to Old Testament wisdom literature.
