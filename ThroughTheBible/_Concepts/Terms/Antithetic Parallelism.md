# Antithetic Parallelism

## Definition

Antithetic parallelism is a Hebrew poetic device in which the second line of a couplet presents a contrast or opposite to the first line. It is especially common in the wisdom literature of the Old Testament, particularly the Book of Proverbs, where it sets the righteous against the wicked, the wise against the foolish, and the consequences of each path in sharp relief. This form of parallelism makes moral and spiritual truth memorable through the power of juxtaposition.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Proverbs 10_12]] — "A wise son makes a glad father, but a foolish son is the grief of his mother."
- [[Proverbs 15_16]] — "A soft answer turns away wrath, but a harsh word stirs up anger."
- [[Psalm 1_6]] — "For the LORD knows the way of the righteous, but the way of the ungodly shall perish."
- [[Proverbs 14_34]] — "Righteousness exalts a nation, but sin is a reproach to any people."

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Pastor Chuck Smith often used antithetic parallelism in his teaching to illustrate the clear choices presented in Scripture — the way of blessing versus the way of judgment, wisdom versus folly. His verse-by-verse exposition of Proverbs repeatedly highlighted how these contrasts are not merely literary devices but reflect the moral structure of reality that God has established.
