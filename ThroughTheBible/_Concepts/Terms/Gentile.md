# Gentile

## Definition

Gentile (from Latin "gentilis," meaning "belonging to a nation") is a term used in the Bible to refer to non-Jewish people or nations. In the Old Testament, Gentiles are often distinguished from Israel as those outside the covenant community, though God's plan always included blessing all nations through Abraham. In the New Testament, the Gospel breaks down the dividing wall between Jew and Gentile, creating one new humanity in Christ through faith.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Genesis 12_3]] — "All peoples on earth will be blessed through you"
- [[Isaiah]] — Gentiles welcomed to worship at God's house
- [[Acts 10]] — Peter and Cornelius — the Gospel opens to the Gentiles
- [[Acts 15]] — The Jerusalem Council on Gentile inclusion
- [[Romans 8_9-11]] — Paul's theology of Jews and Gentiles in God's plan
- [[Ephesians 2_11-22]] — Jew and Gentile united in Christ
- [[Galatians 3_28]] — "There is neither Jew nor Gentile... for you are all one in Christ"
- [[Matthew]] — The Temple as a "house of prayer for all nations"

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith frequently taught on the inclusion of Gentiles in God's redemptive plan throughout his C2000 series. He emphasized from Ephesians 2 that the dividing wall between Jew and Gentile has been broken down by Christ's sacrifice, and that the Church is composed of both Jewish and Gentile believers united in one body. Smith also highlighted the prophetic significance of Gentiles being blessed through Abraham's seed.
