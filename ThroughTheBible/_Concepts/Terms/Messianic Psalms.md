# Messianic Psalms

## Definition

The Messianic Psalms are those psalms in the Book of Psalms that contain prophecies, typology, or foreshadowing of the Messiah — Jesus Christ. These psalms speak prophetically about His person, His suffering, His resurrection, His priesthood, His kingship, and His coming kingdom. They are called "Messianic" because they find their ultimate fulfillment in the life, death, and resurrection of Jesus, as confirmed by the New Testament writers who frequently quote them.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Psalm 2]] — "The kings of the earth set themselves... against the LORD and against His Anointed" (quoted in Acts 4:25-26).
- [[Psalm 22]] — "My God, My God, why have You forsaken Me?" — detailed prophecy of the crucifixion, quoted by Jesus on the cross.
- [[Psalm 16_10]] — "The LORD said to my Lord, 'Sit at My right hand'" — quoted by Jesus regarding His messianic identity.
- [[Psalm 16_10]] — "You will not leave my soul in Sheol, nor will You allow Your Holy One to see corruption" (quoted by Peter at Pentecost).
- [[Psalm 80_3, 7, 19]] — "They gave me also gall for my food, and for my thirst they gave me vinegar to drink."
- [[Psalm 118_22-23]] — "The stone which the builders rejected has become the chief cornerstone."
- [[Psalm 2]] — The kingdom reign of the Messianic King.

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

In his C2000 teachings through the Psalms, Chuck Smith frequently paused to identify Messianic prophecies, showing how the psalmists wrote under the inspiration of the Holy Spirit about events they did not fully understand. He often highlighted Psalm 22 as one of the most remarkable prophetic passages in Scripture, noting how David described crucifixion centuries before that method of execution existed. This emphasis on fulfilled prophecy was a hallmark of his apologetic teaching style.
