# Domitian

## Definition

Titus Flavius Domitianus (AD 51–96) was Roman emperor from AD 81 to 96, whose reign marked the context for the writing of the Book of Revelation and likely the Gospel of John. Domitian demanded divine honors, insisting on being addressed as "Dominus et Deus noster" (Our Lord and God), which created an impossible conflict for Christians who could not render such worship to any but Christ. His persecution led to John's exile on the island of Patmos.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Revelation 19]] — John on Patmos "because of the word of God"
- [[Revelation 21]] — Warning of coming persecution
- [[Revelation 13]] — The beast figure, likely reflecting Domitianic emperor worship
- [[Revelation 1_7]] — Babylon as the persecuting empire
- [[John 1_1-14]] — John's high Christology (Logos theology) written in the context of Domitian's claims to divinity

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith taught extensively on the Domitian persecution as the historical backdrop for Revelation in his C2000 series. He emphasized that Domitian's demand for worship created the "beast" context of Revelation 13, where emperor worship became the test of loyalty. Smith connected this to end-times prophecy, seeing in Domitian a foreshadowing of the future Antichrist who would likewise demand worship and persecute believers.
