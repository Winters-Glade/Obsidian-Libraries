# Amarna Letters

## Description

The Amarna Letters are a collection of clay tablets dating to the 14th century BC, discovered at Amarna, Egypt. They contain diplomatic correspondence between Pharaohs Amenhotep III and Akhenaten and Canaanite vassal rulers. These documents provide valuable archaeological evidence for the political and social conditions in Canaan during the Late Bronze Age, around the time of the Exodus and Conquest. Chuck Smith references them as external confirmation of the biblical narrative's historical setting.
