# Lamb of God

## Definition

The Lamb of God is a title for Jesus Christ introduced by John the Baptist at the beginning of Jesus' public ministry: "Behold, the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world" (John 1:29). The title draws on the Old Testament sacrificial system — especially the Passover lamb (Exodus 12) and the Suffering Servant who is "led like a lamb to the slaughter" (Isaiah 53:7) — portraying Jesus as the ultimate, once-for-all sacrifice for sin.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[John 1]] — John the Baptist's declaration: "Behold, the Lamb of God"
- [[Exodus 12]] — The Passover lamb, the typological foundation
- [[Isaiah]] — "He was led like a lamb to the slaughter"
- [[1 Peter]] — Redeemed by the precious blood of Christ, a lamb without blemish
- [[Revelation 5_6-12]] — The Lamb worthy to open the scroll
- [[Revelation 1_7]] — "The Lamb at the center of the throne will be their shepherd"
- [[Revelation 2]] — The Lamb as the light of the New Jerusalem
- [[1 Corinthians 1]] — "Christ, our Passover lamb, has been sacrificed"

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith frequently returned to the Lamb of God theme in his C2000 series, particularly in his teachings on Exodus (Passover), Isaiah 53, and the Gospel of John. He emphasized that every animal sacrifice in the Old Testament pointed forward to Jesus, the perfect Lamb who would once and for all take away sin — not merely cover it. Smith also highlighted the Lamb's prominence in Revelation, where the Lamb is the central figure of heavenly worship and the only one worthy to open the scroll of God's judgment.
