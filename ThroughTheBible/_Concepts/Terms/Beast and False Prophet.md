# Beast and False Prophet

## Definition

The Beast and False Prophet are end-times figures described in the Book of Revelation who oppose God and persecute His people during the Great Tribulation. The Beast (often identified as the Antichrist or the political ruler of a revived Roman Empire) rises from the sea with blasphemous authority, while the False Prophet (the second beast rising from the earth) performs miraculous signs to deceive the world into worshiping the Beast and receiving his mark.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Revelation 13]] — The Beast rising from the sea and the second beast (False Prophet)
- [[Revelation 1]] — Unclean spirits from the dragon, beast, and false prophet
- [[Revelation 19-20]] — The Beast and False Prophet cast into the lake of fire
- [[Revelation 21]] — Their eternal punishment in the lake of fire
- [[2 Thessalonians 2_1-12]] — The man of lawlessness, a parallel figure
- [[Daniel 7]] — The four beasts, providing background for Revelation's imagery
- [[Revelation 1]] — Warning against worshiping the Beast

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith was well-known for his eschatological teaching and devoted extensive coverage to the Beast and False Prophet in his C2000 series on Revelation. He taught that the Beast represents a future world dictator who will rise to power, make a covenant with Israel, and then break it — demanding worship and persecuting believers. Smith identified the False Prophet as a religious leader who will deceive the world through miracles and enforce the worship of the Beast.
