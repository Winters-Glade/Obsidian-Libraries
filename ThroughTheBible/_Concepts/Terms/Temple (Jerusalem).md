# Temple (Jerusalem)

## Definition

The Jerusalem Temple was the central place of worship in ancient Israel, the dwelling place of God's presence (Shekinah) among His people. Three temples stood successively on Mount Moriah: Solomon's Temple (c. 957–586 BCE), Zerubbabel's Temple (c. 515–20 BCE, also called the Second Temple), and Herod's Temple (c. 20 BCE–70 CE), the latter being the temple Jesus knew, which was expanded and beautified by Herod the Great.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[1 Kings 1]] — The building and dedication of Solomon's Temple
- [[2 Chronicles]] — The construction details and dedication ceremony
- [[Ezra 3]] — The rebuilding of the Second Temple under Zerubbabel
- [[John]] — Jesus cleansing the Temple
- [[Matthew]] — Jesus' prophecy of the Temple's destruction
- [[John]] — Jesus teaching that true worship is in spirit and truth, not limited to a physical location
- [[1 Corinthians 3_16]] — Believers as the Temple of the Holy Spirit
- [[Revelation 21-22]] — No temple in the New Jerusalem

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith devoted extensive teaching to the Temple in his C2000 series, particularly in his exposition of 1 Kings, Ezra, and the Gospels. He emphasized the Temple's typological significance — how it foreshadowed Christ (the true Temple), the Church (the spiritual temple), and the believer (the temple of the Holy Spirit). Smith also taught on the eschatological significance of the Temple, noting its rebuilding as a sign of the end times.
