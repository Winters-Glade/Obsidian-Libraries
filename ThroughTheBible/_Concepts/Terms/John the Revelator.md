# John the Revelator

## Definition

John the Revelator is the traditional name for John the apostle, son of Zebedee, who authored the Gospel of John, the three Epistles of John, and the Book of Revelation. He was one of Jesus' twelve disciples and a member of His inner circle (along with Peter and James), known as "the disciple whom Jesus loved." Late in life, he ministered in Ephesus and was exiled to the island of Patmos under Emperor Domitian, where he received the apocalyptic vision recorded in Revelation.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Revelation 1]] — John's testimony of the revelation of Jesus Christ
- [[John 1]] — "The disciple whom Jesus loved" — John's self-reference
- [[John 1]] — John entrusted with Mary's care at the cross
- [[John]] — The identification of John as the beloved disciple
- [[Mark]] — Jesus appointing the twelve, including John
- [[1 John 1_1-4]] — John's eyewitness testimony of the Word of life

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith held to the traditional view that John the apostle wrote all the Johannine literature, and he frequently referenced John's character transformation in his C2000 teachings — from a "son of thunder" (Mark 3:17) who wanted to call fire down on a Samaritan village, to the "apostle of love" who wrote extensively about loving one another. Smith emphasized John's unique role as the last surviving apostle and his intimate relationship with Jesus.
