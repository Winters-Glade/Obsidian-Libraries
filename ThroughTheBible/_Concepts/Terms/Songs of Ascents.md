# Songs of Ascents

## Definition

The Songs of Ascents (Shir HaMa'alot) are a collection of fifteen psalms — Psalms 120 through 134 — traditionally sung by pilgrims as they ascended to Jerusalem for the three annual pilgrimage festivals (Passover, Pentecost, and Tabernacles). Each psalm bears the superscription "A Song of Ascents" and reflects the themes of pilgrimage, Zion, brotherly unity, and trust in God's protection.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Psalm 100]] — A cry for deliverance from lying lips, beginning the ascent
- [[Psalm 121]] — "I lift up my eyes to the hills" — God as keeper of Israel
- [[Psalm 22]] — "Pray for the peace of Jerusalem"
- [[Psalm 23]] — A plea for mercy
- [[Psalm 14]] — "If the Lord had not been on our side"
- [[Psalm 150]] — Trust in God's protection around His people
- [[Psalm 16]] — "The Lord has done great things for us"
- [[Psalm 17]] — "Unless the Lord builds the house"
- [[Psalm 18]] — Blessedness of the God-fearing family
- [[Psalm 19]] — Persecution and vindication
- [[Psalm 100]] — "Out of the depths I cry to you" — waiting for redemption
- [[Psalm 111]] — Humble trust like a weaned child
- [[Psalm 132]] — God's dwelling place in Zion
- [[Psalm 133]] — "How good and pleasant it is when brothers dwell in unity"
- [[Psalm 14]] — A final call to bless the Lord

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith taught the Songs of Ascents in his C2000 series as psalms of pilgrimage that express the joy and anticipation of worshiping God in Jerusalem. He often noted how Psalm 122 — "Pray for the peace of Jerusalem" — remains relevant for believers today, and he connected the spiritual ascent theme to the Christian's journey of faith toward the heavenly Jerusalem.
