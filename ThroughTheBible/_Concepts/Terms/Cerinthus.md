# Cerinthus

## Definition

Cerinthus was an early Christian heretic active in Ephesus during the late first century CE, known for teaching a form of Gnosticism that denied the full deity of Jesus Christ. According to early church tradition (particularly Irenaeus in Against Heresies), Cerinthus taught that the divine Christ descended upon the human Jesus at His baptism and departed before the crucifixion, and he also promoted a materialistic millenarianism that the apostle John vigorously opposed.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[1 John]] — "Who is the liar but he who denies that Jesus is the Christ"
- [[1 John]] — Testing the spirits — confessing Jesus Christ come in the flesh
- [[1 John]] — Faith that Jesus is the Christ
- [[John 1_14]] — "The Word became flesh" — a direct counter to Cerinthian teaching
- [[John]] — Warning against those who deny Jesus Christ coming in the flesh

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith's C2000 notes on 1 John reference Cerinthus as the heretical teacher whose Gnostic views shaped the background of John's epistle. Church tradition records that John the Apostle confronted Cerinthus in Ephesus and that John wrote his Gospel and epistles partly to counter these false teachings — particularly the denial of Jesus' full humanity and deity. Smith taught that 1 John provides the antidote to such heresy by affirming both the full deity and full humanity of Christ.
