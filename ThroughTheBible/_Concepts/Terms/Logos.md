# Logos

## Definition

Logos (Greek: λόγος, "Word") is the term used in John 1:1-14 to identify Jesus Christ as the pre-existent, divine Word who was with God and was God, through whom all things were created. The concept draws on both Greek philosophical traditions (the Stoic concept of the rational principle governing the universe) and Hebrew wisdom traditions (the personified Wisdom of God in Proverbs 8 and the creative Word of God in Genesis 1).

## Key Bible Verses

- [[John 1_1-5]] — "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God"
- [[John 1_14]] — "The Word became flesh and dwelt among us"
- [[Genesis]] — The Word in creation — "God said, Let there be light"
- [[Proverbs 26-31]] — Personified Wisdom, a precursor to Logos theology
- [[Colossians 2]] — Christ as the image of the invisible God, the agent of creation
- [[Hebrews]] — Christ as the radiance of God's glory
- [[1 John]] — "That which was from the beginning, which we have heard"

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith devoted significant attention to the Logos concept in his C2000 teachings on the Gospel of John. He explained that John deliberately used the Greek term Logos to communicate to both Jewish and Gentile audiences that Jesus is the eternal, pre-existent Word who was the agent of creation and the revelation of God. Smith emphasized that the Logos becoming flesh (John 1:14) is the central miracle of Christianity — God Himself taking on human nature.
