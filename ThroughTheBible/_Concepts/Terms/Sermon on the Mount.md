# Sermon on the Mount

## Definition

The Sermon on the Mount is Jesus' most extensive and foundational ethical teaching, recorded in Matthew 5–7. Delivered on a mountainside in Galilee early in His ministry, it contains the Beatitudes, teachings on the Law, the Lord's Prayer, and instructions on fasting, giving, judging, and trust in God. The Sermon presents the ethics of the Kingdom of Heaven, calling His followers to a righteousness that surpasses mere external compliance and flows from a transformed heart.

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith's verse-by-verse exposition of the Gospel of Matthew in the C2000 series gave careful attention to the Sermon on the Mount. He taught that these chapters are not a list of impossible ideals but the practical lifestyle of those who have entered the Kingdom of Heaven. He often emphasized that the Sermon must be understood in light of grace — that we do not live this way to be saved, but because we are saved. His practical, application-oriented teaching made the Beatitudes and the Lord's Prayer accessible to everyday believers.
