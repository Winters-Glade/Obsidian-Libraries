# Synonymous Parallelism

## Definition

Synonymous parallelism is a type of Hebrew poetic device in which the second line of a couplet restates or reinforces the thought of the first line using different but equivalent terms. Far from being mere redundancy, this repetition creates rhetorical emphasis, deepens emotional impact, and invites the reader to ponder the truth from a slightly different angle. It is the most common form of parallelism in the Psalms.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Psalm 41]] — "The earth is the LORD's, and all its fullness, the world and those who dwell therein."
- [[Psalm 19_1]] — "The heavens declare the glory of God; and the firmament shows His handiwork."
- [[Psalm 1]] — "He has not dealt with us according to our sins, nor punished us according to our iniquities. For as the heavens are high above the earth, so great is His mercy toward those who fear Him..."
- [[Proverbs 6_16-19]] — A catalog of seven things the Lord hates, each stated with parallel phrasing

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

In his C2000 series teachings on the Psalms, Chuck Smith frequently highlighted synonymous parallelism to show how the psalmist's repetition reinforces the certainty of God's promises. He taught that when a verse states the same truth in two ways, the reader should pause and meditate on both statements, as each brings out a different facet of the same divine reality.
