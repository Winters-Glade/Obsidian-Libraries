# Word of God

## Definition

"Word of God" is a rich biblical term with multiple layers of meaning. It refers to (1) God's self-revelation through the written Scriptures, which are "God-breathed" (2 Timothy 3:16) and carry divine authority; (2) the spoken prophetic message delivered through God's messengers; and (3) the ultimate and living Word — Jesus Christ Himself, the eternal Logos who was with God and was God (John 1:1). In Christian theology, the Word of God is both the written Word (Scripture) and the living Word (Christ), inseparably connected as the means by which God reveals Himself to humanity.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[John 1_1-5]] — "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God."
- [[John 1_14]] — "The Word became flesh and dwelt among us."
- [[2 Timothy 3_16-17]] — "All Scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness."
- [[Hebrews 4_12]] — "The word of God is living and powerful, and sharper than any two-edged sword."
- [[Isaiah]] — "So shall My word be that goes forth from My mouth; it shall not return to Me void."
- [[Psalm 119_105]] — "Your word is a lamp to my feet and a light to my path."

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

The Word of God was the foundation of Chuck Smith's entire ministry. His "Through the Bible" series (C2000) was born from a commitment to teach Scripture verse by verse, allowing the text to speak for itself. He often said that the Bible is not a book about religion but the very Word of God, and his teaching aimed to make it understandable and applicable to everyday life. His approach to Scripture was one of simple faith — he believed it meant what it said and said what it meant.
