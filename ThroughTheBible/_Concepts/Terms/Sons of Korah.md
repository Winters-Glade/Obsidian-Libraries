# Sons of Korah

## Definition

The Sons of Korah were a guild of temple musicians and singers descended from Korah (a Levite who led a rebellion against Moses in Numbers 16). Despite their ancestor's rebellion, the Korahites became prominent in Israel's worship, and eleven psalms are attributed to them in the Psalter: Psalms 42, 44-49, 84-85, 87-88. Their psalms are known for their deep spiritual longing, love for God's house, and poetic beauty.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Psalm 42]] — "As the deer pants for flowing streams" — a deep longing for God
- [[Psalm 44_3-8]] — A plea for God to act on behalf of His people
- [[Psalm 145]] — A royal wedding song
- [[Psalm 46]] — "God is our refuge and strength"
- [[Psalm 34_7]] — God is King over all the earth
- [[Psalm 148]] — The beauty and glory of Zion
- [[_Concepts/Biblical_References/Psalm 49_15]] — The futility of trusting in riches
- [[Psalm 8]] — "How lovely is your dwelling place"
- [[Psalm 8]] — A prayer for restoration
- [[Psalm 8]] — Zion as the city of God
- [[Psalm 8]] — A lament of deep suffering

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith noted in his C2000 teaching on the Psalms that the Sons of Korah demonstrate God's redeeming grace — despite Korah's rebellion, his descendants were elevated to serve in the house of God. He often highlighted Psalm 84 ("How lovely is your dwelling place") and Psalm 46 ("God is our refuge and strength") as among the most beloved psalms, reflecting the Korahites' deep devotion to worship.
