# Final Judgment

## Definition

The Final Judgment is the eschatological event at the end of human history when God will judge all people — the living and the dead — through Jesus Christ, rendering eternal rewards and punishments based on His perfect righteousness. The Bible describes this as the Great White Throne Judgment (Revelation 20:11-15), where those whose names are written in the Book of Life enter eternal life and those not found in the book face eternal separation from God.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Revelation 20_11-15]] — The Great White Throne Judgment
- [[Matthew 25_31-46]] — The Judgment of the Nations — separating sheep and goats
- [[John 5_28-29]] — The resurrection to life and resurrection to judgment
- [[Romans 5]] — The day of God's righteous judgment
- [[2 Corinthians 5_10]] — The judgment seat of Christ for believers
- [[Acts 17_31]] — God will judge the world through Christ
- [[Daniel 12_2]] — The resurrection to everlasting life or shame

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith's C2000 teaching on the Final Judgment emphasized both the certainty of judgment and the provision of salvation through Jesus Christ. He taught that there are two distinct judgments — the Judgment Seat of Christ (Bema) where believers receive rewards, and the Great White Throne Judgment for the unrighteous. Smith urged his listeners to live in light of eternity, knowing that every person will one day stand before God.
