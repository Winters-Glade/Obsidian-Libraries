# Babylonian

## Definition

The Babylonian period refers to the era of Jewish exile in Babylon (586–539 BCE), following the destruction of Jerusalem and the Temple by Nebuchadnezzar II. This pivotal period shaped Jewish identity, religious practice, and literature as the people of Judah were taken captive to Babylon and later allowed to return under Cyrus the Great.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[2 Kings]] — The conquest of Jerusalem and the beginning of the Babylonian exile
- [[Daniel]] — Daniel and his friends taken captive to Babylon
- [[Psalm 17]] — The lament of the exiles in Babylon
- [[Jeremiah]] — Jeremiah's letter to the exiles in Babylon
- [[Ezra 1_1-6]] — The decree of Cyrus allowing the return from Babylon
- [[Isaiah]] — Words of comfort addressed to the Babylonian exiles

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith frequently taught on the Babylonian captivity as a consequence of Israel's disobedience, emphasizing how God used a pagan nation to discipline His people while preserving a remnant. In his C2000 series, he highlighted how the exile purified Israel of idolatry and prepared the way for the coming of the Messiah. He also drew parallels between the Babylonian captivity and the end-times Babylonian system described in Revelation.
