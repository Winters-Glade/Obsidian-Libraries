# YHWH

## Definition

YHWH (also known as the Tetragrammaton, from Greek meaning "four letters") is the personal, covenantal name of God revealed in the Hebrew Bible. Consisting of the four Hebrew letters Yod-He-Vav-He (יהוה), it appears over 6,800 times in the Old Testament. The name is derived from the Hebrew verb *hayah* ("to be"), signifying God's self-existence, eternity, and faithfulness — "I AM WHO I AM" (Exodus 3:14). Out of reverence, Jewish tradition ceased to pronounce the name, substituting *Adonai* ("Lord") instead. Most English Bibles render it as "LORD" (in small capitals).

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Exodus]] — God reveals His name to Moses at the burning bush: "I AM WHO I AM."
- [[Exodus]] — The third commandment: "You shall not take the name of the LORD your God in vain."
- [[Leviticus 24_16]] — The penalty for blaspheming the name.
- [[Psalm 8]] — "That they may know that You, whose name alone is the LORD, are the Most High over all the earth."
- [[Isaiah]] — "I am the LORD, that is My name; and My glory I will not give to another."
- [[Malachi]] — "For I am the LORD, I do not change."

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith frequently discussed the significance of the name YHWH in his verse-by-verse teaching through the Old Testament. He emphasized that God's name is not merely a label but a revelation of His character — the covenant-keeping, self-existent God who is faithful to His promises. He often pointed out the contrast between *Elohim* (God as Creator) and YHWH (God as covenant Redeemer), showing how the names reveal different aspects of God's nature.
