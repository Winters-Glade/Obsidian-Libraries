# Synthetic Parallelism

## Definition

Synthetic parallelism (also called "formal" or "developmental" parallelism) is a Hebrew poetic device in which the second line of a couplet does not merely repeat or contrast the first, but advances, completes, or explains it. The second line adds new information — providing a reason, a consequence, an illustration, or a further specification. This form is common in descriptive and prophetic passages where the poet builds a complete picture line by line.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Psalm 132]] — "He shall be like a tree planted by the rivers of water, that brings forth its fruit in its season, whose leaf also shall not wither..."
- [[Psalm 95]] — "Oh come, let us worship and bow down; let us kneel before the LORD our Maker. For He is our God, and we are the people of His pasture..."
- [[Isaiah]] — "They shall beat their swords into plowshares, and their spears into pruning hooks..."
- [[Psalm 19]] — Each line adds a new attribute of God's law

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

In teaching through the prophetic books, Chuck Smith often noted how synthetic parallelism builds a cumulative case for God's judgment or mercy. His expository method naturally traced the logical flow of such passages, showing how each line adds a new layer of truth that contributes to the whole message. He emphasized that understanding this developmental structure is key to following the argument of the Hebrew prophets.
