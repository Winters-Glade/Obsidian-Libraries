# Imprecatory Psalms

## Definition

Imprecatory Psalms are psalms that call down judgment, curses, or vengeance upon the enemies of God and His people. These psalms — including Psalms 35, 55, 58, 59, 69, 83, 109, and 137 — express raw human emotion and a desire for divine justice, reflecting the psalmists' unwavering conviction that God will ultimately vindicate His righteousness.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Psalm 43_5]] — David calling for divine judgment on his enemies
- [[Psalm 50]] — A cry against a betrayer
- [[Psalm 8]] — A plea for God to break the teeth of the wicked
- [[Psalm 59]] — A request for deliverance from bloodthirsty enemies
- [[Psalm 69_9]] — Imprecations against those who hate David without cause
- [[Psalm 8]] — A call for God to destroy Israel's enemies
- [[Psalm 19]] — The most intense imprecatory psalm
- [[Psalm 17]] — The imprecation against Babylon

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith addressed the imprecatory psalms in his C2000 series, explaining that they must be understood in the context of the Old Covenant when God was directly involved in the national life of Israel. He distinguished these Old Testament calls for vengeance from the New Testament ethic of loving one's enemies, while affirming that the psalms express a legitimate longing for God's justice to prevail in the world.
