# Parables

## Definition

Parables are short, illustrative stories drawn from everyday life used by Jesus as a primary teaching method to convey spiritual truths about the Kingdom of God. Using familiar imagery — farming, fishing, shepherding, banquets, and family relationships — Jesus' parables both revealed truth to receptive hearers and concealed it from those who were hardened, fulfilling the prophetic pattern of Isaiah 6.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Matthew 1_3]] — The great collection of Kingdom parables (Sower, Wheat and Tares, Mustard Seed, Leaven, Hidden Treasure, Pearl, Dragnet)
- [[Luke 15]] — The parables of the Lost Sheep, Lost Coin, and Prodigal Son
- [[Luke]] — The Parable of the Good Samaritan
- [[Luke]] — The Parable of the Pharisee and the Tax Collector
- [[Matthew]] — The Parable of the Unforgiving Servant
- [[Matthew 25_14-30]] — The Parable of the Talents
- [[Mark 4_1-20]] — The Parable of the Sower with Jesus' explanation
- [[Matthew]] — The Parable of the Ten Virgins

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith was known for his clear, practical exposition of Jesus' parables in the C2000 series. He consistently emphasized that parables are earthly stories with heavenly meanings, and he drew out both the immediate application for Jesus' original audience and the enduring spiritual principles for contemporary believers. His teaching on the Parable of the Sower, in particular, highlighted the four types of heart responses to the Word of God.
