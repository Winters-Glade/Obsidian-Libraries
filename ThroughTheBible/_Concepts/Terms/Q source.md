# Q source

## Definition

The Q source (from German "Quelle," meaning "source") is a hypothetical written collection of Jesus' sayings that many scholars propose as a source used by Matthew and Luke in addition to the Gospel of Mark. Q is inferred from the approximately 200 verses of material — mostly sayings and teachings of Jesus — that Matthew and Luke share but are absent from Mark, such as the Beatitudes, the Lord's Prayer, and many parables.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Matthew]] and [[Luke]] — The Sermon on the Mount/Plain, much of which is Q material
- [[Matthew]] and [[Luke 11_2-4]] — The Lord's Prayer (Q material)
- [[Matthew]] and [[Luke]] — The centurion's servant (Q material)
- [[Matthew]] and [[Luke]] — John the Baptist's question (Q material)
- [[Matthew]] and [[Luke]] — Woes against the Pharisees (Q material)

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith's C2000 notes reference the Q source hypothesis in the study of the Synoptic Gospels, particularly in the Matthew notes where the material unique to Matthew and Luke but absent from Mark is discussed. Smith treated the Gospels as reliable historical documents while acknowledging scholarly frameworks used to understand their literary relationships.
