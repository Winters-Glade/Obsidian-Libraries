# Royal Psalms

## Definition

The Royal Psalms are a group of psalms that focus on the Davidic king — his coronation, reign, military victories, and divine appointment as God's anointed ruler. These psalms (including Psalms 2, 18, 20, 21, 45, 72, 89, 101, 110, 132, and 144) were used in Israel's royal liturgy and later came to be understood messianically, pointing forward to the ultimate Davidic King, Jesus the Messiah.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Psalm 2]] — "You are my Son; today I have begotten you" — God's decree for His Anointed
- [[Psalm 18]] — A thanksgiving psalm for the king's deliverance
- [[Psalm 2]] — A prayer for the king's victory in battle
- [[Psalm 145]] — A royal wedding song for the king
- [[Psalm 2]] — A prayer for the king's righteous reign
- [[Psalm 89]] — God's covenant with David and the Davidic line
- [[Psalm 16_10]] — "The Lord says to my Lord" — a messianic psalm of enthronement
- [[Psalm 132]] — God's choice of David and Zion

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith consistently interpreted the Royal Psalms as messianic prophecies pointing to Jesus Christ. In his C2000 teaching, he emphasized Psalm 2 and Psalm 110 as key texts demonstrating Christ's divine sonship and exaltation. He taught that the Davidic covenant (2 Samuel 7) forms the backdrop for understanding these psalms, and that Jesus fulfills the role of the ideal Davidic King who will reign forever.
