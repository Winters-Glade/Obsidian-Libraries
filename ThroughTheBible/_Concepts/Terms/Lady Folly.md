# Lady Folly

## Definition

Lady Folly (also called "Madam Folly" or "the foolish woman") is the personification of foolishness and moral perversity in the Book of Proverbs. She stands in stark contrast to Lady Wisdom as a seductive, loud, and undisciplined woman who lures the simple into the path of destruction. While Lady Wisdom builds her house and prepares a feast, Lady Folly sits at the door of her house, calling to those who pass by, promising stolen pleasures that lead only to death. The contrast between these two women presents the fundamental choice of the wisdom literature: the path of life versus the path of death.

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Proverbs 7_6-27]] — The graphic description of the seduction by the adulterous woman, a vivid portrayal of folly.
- [[_transcripts/The Name of Jesus, Proverbs 18_10]] — "A foolish woman is clamorous; she is simple, and knows nothing... Stolen water is sweet, and bread eaten in secret is pleasant."
- [[Proverbs 5_3-11]] — The warning against the strange woman whose end is bitter as wormwood.
- [[_transcripts/The Name of Jesus, Proverbs 18_10]] — "The mouth of an immoral woman is a deep pit; he who is abhorred by the LORD will fall there."
- [[Proverbs 14_12]] — "The wise woman builds her house, but the foolish pulls it down with her hands."

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

In his exposition of Proverbs, Chuck Smith contrasted Lady Folly with Lady Wisdom as a vivid illustration of the two paths set before every person. He taught that the warnings about Lady Folly are not merely about sexual sin but represent the broader choice between following God's wisdom and pursuing worldly诱惑. His practical, straightforward teaching style made these warnings accessible and urgent, emphasizing that sin always promises pleasure but delivers destruction.
