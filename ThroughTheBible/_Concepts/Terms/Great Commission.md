# Great Commission

## Definition

The Great Commission is Jesus' final指令 to His disciples before His ascension, recorded in Matthew 28:16-20. In it, the risen Christ declares that all authority in heaven and on earth has been given to Him, and He commissions His followers to "go therefore and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, teaching them to observe all that I have commanded you." The Commission concludes with the promise of Jesus' abiding presence "always, even to the end of the age."

## Key Bible Verses

- [[Matthew]] — The primary Great Commission text.
- [[Mark]] — "Go into all the world and preach the gospel to every creature."
- [[Luke]] — The Commission in Luke's Gospel: repentance and remission of sins preached to all nations.
- [[Acts 1_8]] — "You shall be witnesses to Me in Jerusalem, and in all Judea and Samaria, and to the end of the earth."
- [[John]] — "As the Father has sent Me, I also send you."

## Connection to Chuck Smith / C2000

Chuck Smith was a passionate advocate for fulfilling the Great Commission, and his ministry at Calvary Chapel exemplified this calling. His C2000 teaching on Matthew 28 emphasized that the Commission is not a suggestion but a command from the risen King. He stressed that the promise "I am with you always" is the basis for boldness in evangelism. His emphasis on simple, Spirit-empowered outreach — rather than elaborate programs — reflected his conviction that every believer is called to make disciples.
