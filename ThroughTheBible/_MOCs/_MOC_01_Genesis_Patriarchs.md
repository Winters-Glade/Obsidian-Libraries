# Batch 1 — Genesis & Early Patriarchs

> Map of Content for Batch 1 of the T2000 Topical Series — covering the foundational narratives of Genesis from the Fall of Man through the life of Joseph.

## Overview

This batch opens the T2000 Topical Series with 13 teachings from the book of Genesis, covering the major patriarchal narratives: the Fall of Man ([[Genesis 3]]), the judgment of the Flood ([[Genesis 6]]), the Tower of Babel ([[Genesis 11]]), the covenant with Abraham ([[Genesis 15]], [[Genesis 2_2]]), the destruction of Sodom ([[Genesis]]), the lives of Isaac and Jacob ([[Genesis 28]], [[Genesis 3.md]]), and the story of Joseph ([[Genesis 37]]–[[Genesis 50]]). These sermons lay the theological foundation for the entire T2000 series, exploring themes of sin, grace, covenant, faith, suffering, providence, and the character of God.

## Key Themes

- **[[Fall of Man.md]]** — The origin of sin and God's redemptive pursuit of hiding sinners (Genesis 3)
- **[[Protoevangelium]]** — The first gospel promise: the Seed of the woman who will crush the serpent's head (Genesis 3:15)
- **[[Divine Judgment.md]]** — The Flood and the salvation of Noah; Sodom's destruction and Lot's rescue
- **[[Covenant]]** — God's unconditional covenant with Abraham; the promise of land, seed, and blessing (Genesis 12, 15, 17, 22)
- **[[Faith]]** — Abraham's faith credited as righteousness (Genesis 15:6); tested in the binding of Isaac (Genesis 22)
- **[[Providence]]** — God's sovereign governance of all events, seen supremely in the life of Joseph
- **[[Grace]]** — Fig leaves (human effort) vs. garments of skin (God's provision of atonement)
- **[[Transformation.md]]** — Jacob's wrestling at Peniel: the crippling that brings blessing
- **[[Suffering]]** — Joseph's journey from the pit to the palace as a model of faithful endurance
- **[[Gods omniscience.md]]** — "Surely the LORD is in this place" (Genesis 28:16); God with Joseph in every circumstance
- **[[Integrity]]** — Joseph's refusal of Potiphar's wife; fleeing sin out of the fear of God
- **[[The Finger of God.md]]** — The secret of Joseph's strength: the arms of his hands made strong by the mighty God of Jacob

## Key Events

- **[[Fall of Man.md]] (Genesis 3)** — Adam and Eve's disobedience; the curse; God's promise of the Seed; expulsion from Eden
- **[[Cain]] (Genesis 4)** — The first murder; the spread of sin; the line of Seth
- **[[The Flood]] (Genesis 6–9)** — God's judgment on universal corruption; Noah's ark; the covenant of the rainbow
- **[[Tower of Babel.md]] (Genesis 11)** — Human pride and the confusion of languages; the scattering of nations
- **[[The Wall of Jerusalem.md]] (Genesis 12)** — God's call and promise; Abraham's departure by faith
- **[[The Abrahamic Covenant]] (Genesis 15)** — God's covenant cut with Abraham; the promise of descendants and land
- **[[The Destruction of Pharaohs Army.md]] (Genesis 19)** — Judgment on the cities of the plain; Lot's rescue; Lot's wife becomes a pillar of salt
- **[[The Nation of Israel.md]] (Genesis 22)** — Abraham's supreme test of faith; the ram provided as a substitute; a type of Christ
- **[[Bethel]] (Genesis 28)** — Jacob's dream of the ladder; God's promise; "Surely the LORD is in this place"
- **[[Wrestling with God.md]] (Genesis 32)** — Jacob's night of wrestling at Peniel; his name changed to Israel; the crippling blessing
- **[[Slavery]] (Genesis 37)** — Joseph's dreams; the jealousy of his brothers; the pit; sold to Ishmaelites
- **[[Potiphar's wife.md]] (Genesis 39)** — Temptation, integrity, false accusation, imprisonment
- **[[Josephs tomb.md]] (Genesis 41)** — Interpretation of dreams; Pharaoh's promotion; the seven years of plenty and famine
- **[[Egypt]] (Genesis 42)** — The first journey; Joseph's testing; "All things are against me"
- **[[Joseph]] (Genesis 45)** — The emotional reconciliation; "God sent me before you to preserve life"
- **[[Blessing of Joseph.md]] (Genesis 49)** — The fruitful bough; the secret of strength; the Shepherd and Stone of Israel

## Timeline

```
c. 4000 BC — Creation and the Fall (approximate Archbishop Ussher chronology)
c. 2348 BC — The Flood (Ussher)
c. 2247 BC — The Tower of Babel
c. 2091 BC — Abraham called from Ur; enters Canaan
c. 2067 BC — Destruction of Sodom and Gomorrah
c. 2056 BC — Isaac born to Abraham and Sarah
c. 2006 BC — Jacob and Esau born
c. 1915 BC — Joseph sold into slavery
c. 1898 BC — Joseph elevated to prime minister of Egypt
c. 1885 BC — Jacob and family move to Egypt
c. 1805 BC — Death of Joseph
```

*Note: Dates follow the traditional Ussher chronology. Many scholars place these events earlier or later depending on interpretive approach to biblical chronology.*
