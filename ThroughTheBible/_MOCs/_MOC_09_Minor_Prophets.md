# Map of Content — Minor Prophets

> **T2000 Series** — Pastor Chuck Smith  
> Covering the Twelve Minor Prophets (Hosea through Malachi)

## Overview

This MOC covers the twelve Minor Prophets — the final books of the Old Testament prophetic corpus. These books span several centuries and address the northern kingdom of Israel, the southern kingdom of Judah, surrounding nations, and the post-exilic community:

- **Pre-exilic prophets to Israel:** [[Hosea]], [[Amos]], [[Jonah]]
- **Pre-exilic prophets to Judah:** [[Joel]], [[Micah]], [[Nahum]], [[Habakkuk]], [[Zephaniah]]
- **Prophets against nations:** [[Obadiah]] (Edom), [[Nahum]] (Nineveh)
- **Post-exilic prophets:** [[Haggai]], [[Zechariah]], [[Malachi]]

## Key Themes

- **[[Day of the Lord]]** — A central theme throughout (Joel, Amos, Obadiah, Zephaniah, Malachi)
- **[[Covenant Lawsuit]]** — God's legal case against His people (Hosea, Micah, Amos)
- **[[Remnant]]** — God always preserves a faithful remnant (Joel, Amos, Micah, Zephaniah)
- **[[Sovereignty of God]]** — God rules over all nations (Habakkuk, Zechariah)
- **[[Messianic Prophecy.md]]** — Especially rich in Zechariah, also in Micah (Bethlehem)
- **[[Social Justice]]** — The prophetic call for righteousness (Amos, Micah)
- **[[Post-Exilic Period]]** — The return and rebuilding (Haggai, Zechariah, Malachi)
- **[[God]]** — The marriage metaphor (Hosea)
- **[[Peace of God.md]]** — Compassion even toward enemies (Jonah)

## Timeline

```
760-710 BC    — Hosea, Amos, Jonah (to Northern Kingdom / Nineveh)
750-700 BC    — Micah (to Judah)
630-590 BC    — Nahum, Zephaniah, Habakkuk (pre-exilic Judah)
520 BC        — Haggai, Zechariah (post-exilic, rebuilding temple)
480-430 BC    — Joel (possible post-exilic date)
430 BC        — Malachi (post-exilic)
586-539 BC    — Obadiah (possibly around fall of Jerusalem)
```

## Major Messianic Prophecies

- **[[Prophecy]]** (Micah 5:2) → Birth of Jesus in Bethlehem
- **[[Triumphal Entry]]** (Zechariah 9:9) → Palm Sunday
- **[[_Concepts/Doctrines/House of Israel]]** (Zechariah 11:12-13) → Judas' betrayal
- **[[Messiah]]** (Zechariah 12:10) → Crucifixion
- **[[Cleansing from Sin.md]]** (Zechariah 13:1) → Salvation
