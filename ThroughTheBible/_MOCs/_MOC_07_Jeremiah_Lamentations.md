## Overview

This batch covers ten topical lectures from the book of [[Jeremiah]] — the longest prophetic book and one of the most personal — together with one lecture from [[Lamentations]], the poignant poetic response to Jerusalem's fall that finds hope in God's unfailing mercies.

The book of Jeremiah spans the final chaotic decades of Judah before the Babylonian exile (627–586 BC). As the weeping prophet faced opposition, rejection, and a nation that refused to listen, his message remains strikingly relevant: the futility of forsaking God for broken substitutes, the danger of trusting in religious ritual rather than genuine obedience, and the enduring hope found in God's covenant faithfulness.

## Key Themes Across This Batch

- **Forsaking the Fountain** — The foundational sin is exchanging God (the fountain of living water) for broken, human-made substitutes that cannot satisfy (Jeremiah 2:13)
- **False Security** — Trusting in religious institutions, traditions, or formulas rather than genuine obedience brings a false sense of safety (Jeremiah 7)
- **God's Sovereign Work** — God is the Potter; we are the clay. He shapes, reshapes, and refines us for His purposes (Jeremiah 18)
- **The Cost of Truth** — Speaking God's truth often brings opposition, rejection, and suffering — Jeremiah's life demonstrates the cost of prophetic faithfulness (Jeremiah 38)
- **Complacency vs. Discipline** — Being undisturbed allows sin to settle; God's unsettling discipline is an act of love that purifies us (Jeremiah 48)
- **Hope in Devastation** — Even in the depths of lament, God's mercies are new every morning and His faithfulness is great (Lamentations 3)
- **Returning to God** — The journey back to God involves repentance, seeking His face, and finding our true resting place in covenant relationship (Jeremiah 50)
