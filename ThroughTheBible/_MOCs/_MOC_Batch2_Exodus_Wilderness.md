# Exodus through Deuteronomy — The Law & Wilderness (Batch 2)

> **T2000 Topical Series** — Pastor Chuck Smith  
> Batch 2: Exodus (7 lectures) + Leviticus (3) + Numbers (4) + Deuteronomy (4)  
> Playlist Index: 14–31

## Overview

This batch covers eighteen topical lectures spanning the four books of the Law and the wilderness journey — from the call of Moses at the burning bush (Exodus 3) to Moses' final plea on the plains of Moab to "choose life" (Deuteronomy 30). These are the foundational books that define Israel's identity: redemption from Egypt, the giving of the Law, the pattern of worship, the wilderness testing, and the covenant renewed before entering the Promised Land.

## Key Themes Across This Batch

- **The God Who Sees and Saves** — From the burning bush to the Red Sea, God reveals Himself as the One who sees affliction, hears cries, and acts in power to redeem His people
- **The Holiness of God** — The Law, the tabernacle, the strange fire of Nadab and Abihu — God's holiness is the foundational reality that shapes worship, ethics, and community
- **Redemption through Blood** — The Passover lamb, the sacrifices of Leviticus, and the bronze serpent all point forward to the atoning work of Christ
- **Grace in the Wilderness** — Despite Israel's grumbling, idolatry, and unbelief, God's grace persists — manna from heaven, water from the rock, and the presence that will not abandon His people
- **The Law as Revelation** — The Ten Commandments and the Book of the Covenant reveal not arbitrary rules but the character of God and the path to human flourishing
- **Covenant and Choice** — Deuteronomy presents the stark binary: life or death, blessing or curse, obedience or judgment — and calls every generation to choose life
- **Types of Christ** — This batch is rich with Messianic types: the Passover Lamb, the bronze serpent, the cities of refuge, the great Prophet, the manna from heaven
- **Intercessory Prayer** — Moses as mediator prefigures Christ's intercession and reveals the heart of standing in the gap for others
- **God's Presence** — Moses' declaration "If Thy presence go not with me, carry us not up hence" sets the priority of God's presence above every other blessing
