> Playlist Index: 1–13

## Overview

This batch opens the T2000 Topical Series with thirteen lectures from the book of [[Genesis]], covering the foundational narratives of the early Bible: the Fall of Man, the judgment of the Flood, the Tower of Babel, and the patriarchs [[Abraham]], [[Isaac]], [[Jacob]], and [[Joseph]]. Pastor Chuck Smith traces the themes of sin, grace, covenant, and providence that run from the garden of Eden to the throne of Egypt, establishing the theological framework for the entire biblical story.

## Key Themes Across This Batch

- **Sin and Grace** — From the Fall (Lecture 1–2) through the Flood (3) to Babel (4), human sin is met by God's judgment and grace
- **Covenant** — God's unconditional covenant with Abram (5) is the foundation of redemption
- **Substitutionary Sacrifice** — The coats of skin (2), the ram at Moriah (6), and the Passover typology all point to Christ
- **Providence** — The Joseph narrative (10–13) demonstrates God's sovereignty over human evil: "Ye thought evil against me, but God meant it unto good"
- **Transformation Through Suffering** — Jacob becomes Israel through brokenness (9); Joseph is prepared for the throne through the pit and prison (10–13)
- **The Presence of God** — God is with His people: in the garden (1), at Bethel (8), in the pit (10), in the prison (11), and in the famine (12)
- **Faith and Righteousness** — Abraham believed God and it was counted as righteousness (5); the same faith is tested on Moriah (6)

## Key People

- [[Adam]] and [[Eve]] — The first couple, whose fall introduces sin into the world
- [[Noah]] — The righteous man who found grace in a corrupt generation
- [[Abraham]] — The father of faith, recipient of the covenant
- [[Isaac]] — The beloved son, willing to be offered
- [[Israel]] — The supplanter transformed into the prince of God
- [[Joseph]] — The righteous sufferer who rises to rule, a type of Christ
- [[Lot]] — The compromised nephew who was saved by grace
- [[Melchizedek]] — The priest-king who blessed Abram (implied in Lecture 5 context)

## Key Places

- [[Garden of Eden]] — The place of innocence, fall, and God's pursuit
- [[Mount Moriah]] — The place of substitutionary sacrifice
- [[Bethel]] — The house of God, Jacob's ladder
- [[Penuel]] — Where Jacob wrestled with God
- [[Sodom and Gomorrah]] — Cities of judgment
- [[Egypt]] — The place of Joseph's slavery and exaltation
- [[Canaan]] — The promised land
