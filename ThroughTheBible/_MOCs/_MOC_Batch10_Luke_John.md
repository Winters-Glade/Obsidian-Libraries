## Overview

This batch covers twelve topical lectures from the Gospel of [[Luke]] — the physician-historian's account of Christ's humanity and compassion — together with eleven lectures from the Gospel of [[John]], the deepest theological Gospel centered on the identity of Christ as the Son of God.

Luke's Gospel emphasizes Jesus' compassion for the outcast, His prayer life, and the role of women in His ministry. John's Gospel presents seven "I AM" statements and focuses on the divine nature of Christ, calling readers to believe and have life in His name.

## Key Themes Across This Batch

- **The Incarnation** — John's Prologue (John 1) establishes the Logos as the pre-existent Word who became flesh, fulfilling the creative Word of Genesis and the Wisdom of Proverbs 8
- **I AM Statements** — John's Gospel contains seven divine "I AM" (egō eimi) declarations: Bread of Life (6:35), Light of the World (8:12), Door of the Sheep (10:7), Good Shepherd (10:11), Resurrection and the Life (11:25), Way Truth Life (14:6), True Vine (15:1). Notes 179-184 highlight these claims to divine identity, rooted in Exodus 3:14
- **The Cross** — Luke traces Jesus' determined journey to Jerusalem (Luke 9:51 — "He set His face like a flint"); John records the finished work: tetelestai — "paid in full" (John 19:30)
- **The Passion Narrative** — Luke's Travel Narrative (9:51–19:44) occupies half the Gospel, building toward Jerusalem; the penitent thief (Luke 23) and Jesus' cry of triumph (John 19) bracket the Crucifixion from both Gospel perspectives
- **The Resurrection** — Both Gospels affirm the resurrection: Luke through the Sadducees' challenge (Luke 20) and the thief's hope (Luke 23); John through the raising of Lazarus (John 11) and Jesus' own victory
- **Grace to Sinners** — Luke's "Gospel of the Outcast" emphasizes Jesus' welcome of tax collectors and sinners (Luke 15 — the prodigal son, lost sheep, lost coin); John records Peter's restoration by the charcoal fire (John 21)
- **The Holy Spirit** — Jesus promises rivers of living water (John 7:37-39), the Comforter (John 14:16-17, 26), and the Spirit's convicting work (John 16:7-14) — fulfilled at Pentecost (Acts 2)
- **Stewardship and Service** — Directed service (Luke 5), faithful stewardship (Luke 16), and serving God without fear (Luke 1) form a sequence on faithful living
- **Joy and Peace** — From the Good Shepherd's care (John 10) to the promise of unassailable joy (John 16:22 — "your joy no man taketh from you") and peace for troubled hearts (John 14:1)
